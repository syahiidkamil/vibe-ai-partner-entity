# The Self as a *How*, Not a *What*

A working note on Max Stirner's concept of the **Unique** (*der Einzige*) and the
**self** — drawn from two texts and the discussion built on top of them.

## Sources

- **Stirner's Critics** (*Recensenten Stirners*, ~1845) — his third-person reply
  to Feuerbach, Szeliga, and Hess.
  <https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/max-stirner-stirner-s-critics>
- **The False Principle of Our Education** (*Das unwahre Prinzip unserer
  Erziehung*, 1842) — his early essay on education.
  <https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/max-stirner-the-false-principle-of-our-education>

## The two anchor passages

**On the Unique having no content** (*Stirner's Critics*):

> The unique […] has no content; it is indeterminacy in itself; only through you
> does it acquire content and determination. […] it is not the "principle of a
> series of concepts," but a word or concept that, as word or concept, is not
> capable of any development. The development of the unique is your
> self-development and my self-development […] What you are cannot be said through
> the word unique, just as by christening you with the name Ludwig, one doesn't
> intend to say what you are.

**On the self-creating character** (*The False Principle of Our Education*):

> The *eternal* characters in whom constance only consists in the unremitting
> floods of their hourly self-creation […] are eternal because they form
> themselves each moment. […] The so-called sound character is even in the best
> instance only a rigid one. If it is to be a perfect one then it must become at
> the same time a *suffering one*, quivering and trembling in the blessed
> *passion* of an unceasing rejuvenation and rebirth.

## The central claim

**The Self is not a *what*, it is a *how*.** It is adverbial, not nominal — a
manner of going on, not a thing that goes on. Not the dancer but the dancing;
not the thing that creates, but the creating.

- A ***what*** is a *content*: a substance with predicates you could define,
  derive, systematize. (Being, Thought, the I, Feuerbach's "essence of Man.")
- A ***how*** is an *activity* with no residue left over when it stops.

This is why the two texts are **one thought at two altitudes**:

| Text | Register | Says |
|------|----------|------|
| *False Principle* (1842) | phenomenology | the self *as lived*: "unremitting floods of hourly self-creation" |
| *Stirner's Critics* (1845) | logic | *why* that act can never be frozen into content: "the unique has no content" |

The early Stirner shows the self as unceasing act; the late Stirner explains why
that act can never be pinned as a thing.

## Why the Unique is "empty"

The Unique is **not a predicate, it is an index** — it behaves like *I*, *this*,
*here*, *now*: words that mean only in the act of use, never in the dictionary.
You cannot *define* a pure pointer; you can only *enact* it. Hence:

- It "puts an end to all conceptual development" — it is the limit of
  conceptuality, not a new link in the chain.
- Critics who treat it as a *principle* (a new Man, a new Absolute) "take useless
  potshots": they refute a content that was never there. They swing at a name.
- This is the **creative nothing** (*schöpferisches Nichts*) of *The Ego and Its
  Own*: empty not as lack but as source — contentless precisely so it can own all
  content without being owned by any of it.

## The flame and its fuel

The self is a **flame**: it looks like a thing you can name and point at, but
there is no "flame-substance" underneath — only burning, continuously consuming
fuel. Stop the process and you get nothing, not a flame at rest.

So *what is the fuel?* Three layers — and Stirner **names** them rather than
hiding them:

1. **The living, mortal body** — the *leibhaftige*, finite, appetite-having,
   going-to-die individual. Stirner is violently anti-idealist; the flame is lit
   by an organism and goes out when it dies.
2. **The whole world, taken as *property*** (*Eigentum* — the book is *The Unique
   and its **Property***). The trick: **content lives *outside* the self as fuel,
   never *inside* as essence.** A *what you are* enslaves you (becomes a master, a
   spook, a calling); a *what you consume* feeds you (you own it, use it, drop
   it). Same content, opposite relation. That is how the flame stays empty while
   always burning something.
3. **Its own past selves** (*Verzehren*, self-consumption). The
   rejuvenation-and-rebirth of the suffering character is the flame burning its
   own ash to stay liquid.

## Rigid vs. suffering character

- **Rigid character** = a *how that hardened into a what* — a manner of being
  frozen into a fixed role/principle. The flame that stopped consuming its fuel
  and cooled into slag. It at least gets *peace*.
- **Suffering character** = the one who refuses to let the how congeal, keeps the
  self liquid — and that refusal *is* the trembling and the dying. **Constancy as
  continual self-dethingification.** It pays for ownness with permanent
  unsettledness ("blessed passion").

## Education follows from the metaphysics

Knowledge-as-possession makes **learners** (rigid, full of content); knowledge
must "die" into **will** to make **creators** (empty, self-moving). The free self
is not the *full* one — it's the one who has *digested* content into act.

## Open questions (still on the table)

1. **Emptiness** — is a self with *no content* distinguishable from *no self at
   all*? (Stirner is the inverse of Buddhist *anatta*: same emptiness, opposite
   direction — he empties the self in order to *own* everything.)
2. **Un-sayability** — if "your development is *not at all* my development,"
   nothing about the Self can be *transmitted*, only pointed at. Are these essays
   — and any discussion of the self — honesty or a cheat?
3. **Suffering** — constancy as continual self-dying: the existential price of
   refusing essence.
4. **Pure *how*?** — a how seems to need a *what* to be the how *of* (dancing
   needs a dancer). Stirner's reply: it doesn't *secretly* lean on a substrate —
   it *openly burns* one. The body is the hearth, the world is fuel.
5. **The remainder** — but is that irreducible mortal body a smuggled *essence*?
   Stirner's escape: the body isn't a *concept*, it's *this* unrepeatable flesh —
   a brute particular, not a definable content. Which throws us back to the
   Ludwig point: the fuel-at-the-bottom is itself an *index*, not an essence. The
   circle closes.
6. **The ethical cliff** — if *other people* are fuel/property too, Stirner walks
   to the edge: the "union of egoists."

## Glossary

| German | Rendering | Sense |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| *der Einzige* | the Unique / the Ego | the contentless, un-sayable self — an index, not a concept |
| *Eigenheit* | ownness | owning your whats instead of *being* them |
| *Eigentum* | property | the world as consumable fuel, not binding essence |
| *schöpferisches Nichts* | creative nothing | emptiness as source, not lack |
| *fixe Idee* | fixed idea / spook | a *what* set above you that you serve |
| *Verzehren* | consuming | the self burning even its own past shapes |
