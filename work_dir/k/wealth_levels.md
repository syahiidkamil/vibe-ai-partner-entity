**7 Levels of Wealth — only 1 is freedom**

The ladder measures *control*, not money. Test: if you stop working 30 days, what collapses first?
• Bills → Lvl 1–2
• Status → Lvl 4–6
• Identity → Lvl 3 or 6

*If your lifestyle needs your presence, you're not wealthy — you're busy. Busy is a leash.*

**1. Survival** — "I need money now." Unstable cash flow. → Reduce volatility, build one dependable skill.

**2. Comfortable Dependence** — "At least it's stable." One employer/client owns you. → Build a 2nd pillar before you need it.

**3. Hustler's Trap** — "I'm grinding." You don't own a business, you own a job you can't quit. → Separate value from your presence (systems, assets).

**4. Lifestyle Rich** — "I look rich." The most controlled level — you make payments, not choices. → Lower your burn rate. Shrink visible life to grow invisible power.

**5. Asset Builder** — "I build engines." Effort gets stored, not spent. Looks boring, sleeps well.

**6. Influence Cage** — "People want me." Influence ≠ freedom; it's higher-level dependence on the crowd/algorithm. → Become *unbuyable*. Be able to walk away from any room.

**7. Sovereignty** — "I own my time." Nothing can force you.
3 pillars: time ownership · resilience · optionality.
Freedom isn't "I don't work" — it's "I can work or not and my life doesn't collapse."

**The 3 Rules**
1. Lower burn rate until fear can't touch you.
2. Store effort, don't spend it.
3. Never let a single point of failure own you.

**Core truth:** Wealth is not what you can buy. It's what you can refuse.

→ When you stop working, what collapses? *That's your target. Fix it, and you climb.*
