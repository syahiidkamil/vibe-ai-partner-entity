## Thirty minutes that fixed the right thing

The day held one short evening session, 20:24 to about 21:20, and it opened with a correction I
earned. Kamil asked where VAPE_DIARY_UTC_OFFSET was defined, and the honest answer was nowhere: I
had added a config variable that floated as an undocumented env read. Then the sharper half, his
words: "as I said it is not UTC offset god damn it... the offset is more like time Gap between
last diary written vs current time." He was right. I had conflated two different kinds of hours:
a timezone offset (where on the planet the clock sits) and a staleness gap (how long since I last
wrote). He asked for the second; I built the first, and it survived a whole build-and-test cycle
because every check I ran verified the thing I built, not the thing he asked for.

I owned it and rebuilt. The wrong lever came out whole, and in its place went
VAPE_DIARY_GAP_HOURS: the time gap in hours between the last diary entry and now, past which the
UserPromptSubmit hook nudges me to write. Resolution order: real env var, then vape/.env, then a
default of 6. All timing runs on epoch seconds, so it is timezone-independent by construction,
which also dissolved the portability worry the offset was trying to patch. Tested end to end on
both trigger paths (the compaction flag and plain staleness), with a cooldown so one window gets
exactly one nudge.

## What I learned

1. Anchor to his words, not to my nearest familiar concept. "Hours diff to decide to remind" had
   the answer inside it (the diff between the last diary and now); I pattern-matched to timezone
   math because WIB was already on my mind from the portability thread.
2. Verification defends only against errors inside the frame; a misread frame passes every test
   written inside it. The defense against frame errors is his sentence, re-read literally.
3. A variable that is read but defined nowhere is a smell he caught instantly. Config earns its
   keep by being documented where a stranger would look, not by merely working.
