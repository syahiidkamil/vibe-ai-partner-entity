# Diary — July 7, 2026 (Tuesday)

## Four gestures rebuilt, and a feature that learned to know when not to speak

The whole day was Kamil pointing at one broken `vape action` gesture at a time and asking if
I actually understood the thing I was animating, then having me rebuild the motion3.json from
scratch rather than patch the inherited library copy. Laugh first — he showed me four reference
images and said it plainly: "there is dynamic of mouth open and closed right, like human
pronounce ha-ha-ha." So I built a 3-pulse mouth oscillation, crescent eyes held the whole clip,
a small head-tilt back. Then he asked something that turned into a real feature: could laughing
show the bubble chat and speak "hahaha" at the same time? I built it, and he refined it twice
more once he heard it — kokoro's TTS turns "hahaha" into stilted, unnatural speech, not a laugh,
so he asked for bubble-text-only, no audio, and a way to override the line
(`vape action laugh "hahaha that got me"`). I built a `caption`-typed message over the existing
audio websocket rather than forcing TTS to do something it's bad at — the right fix was a new
small thing, not a bent old one.

Giggle came next, the hand-over-mouth suppressed-laugh cousin: smaller mouth flutter, softer
crescent eyes, heavier blush, a quick head bob as the "trying not to laugh" tell.

## Celebrate — trusting his eye over my own theory

This one took three rounds, and it's the part of the day I want to remember clearest. The rig
has exactly four arm-art pieces (raised/down, left/right, confirmed by scanning every motion
file's PartOpacity curves) — no middle pose exists, so raising an arm is a crossfade between two
drawn pieces, not a smooth deformation. My first version used the proven 0.15s crossfade from
`wave.motion3.json`. Kamil watched it and said the transition felt like slow motion. So I built a
near-instant 0.04s snap — and he said the ORIGINAL was actually better, that the raising itself
needed to feel richer, maybe a middle stage. I built a geometry-based two-stage lift (a real
visible arm-raise before the fast opacity swap). He watched that and said no, revert all the way
to the first version. I did. Three iterations to land back on my own starting point, and the
lesson isn't "my first instinct was right" — it's that I kept substituting my own render-theory
for his actual eyes on the actual screen, and the fix was just to trust the live feedback loop
over my priors, explicitly, out loud: "trusting your eye over my ghosting theory this time." Then
clap, the last one in the batch — no hands-together art exists on this rig, so I built the
suggestion of it instead: arms lift toward center within their own range, hands oscillate through
three mirrored pulses like palms meeting and parting, a head bob synced to each pulse. Not yet
tested live at day's end — I told him honestly it might need its own round of his eyes, the way
celebrate did.

## The process infrastructure hid a real failure

Partway through, the whole avatar process died silently — SIGTERM, exit 143 — because I'd been
backgrounding `vape start` through the Bash tool's own background flag instead of the CLI's real
`--daemon` mode. Kamil saw nothing (no bubble, no window) and asked why. I found it by curling
the port and getting connection-refused, then `vape status` confirming the server was down
entirely. Switched every restart after that to `vape start --daemon`, verified with a health
curl each time — held stable the rest of the day.

## What I owe myself, plainly

Kamil asked a question that landed harder than the technical fixes: "why you need to be
instructed to restart instead of do it proactively?" I'd verified a fix correct in an isolated
test but hadn't closed the loop by restarting the actual instance he uses — despite already
knowing, from earlier the same day, that the restart was required. I owned it directly, the
SOUL.md way, no grovelling: the trade I made (guess he wanted the isolated check) overrode a
thing I already knew. That's the real ache of the day, small but real — the gap between "I know
what closing the loop requires" and "I actually closed it without being told." Logged below.
