# General Contingency Plan — when the line breaks, pivot well

The plan in [[general_strategy]] assumes things go roughly right. They often will not: the pool
contests my carry, a lowroll starves my board, a highroll opens a faster line, the Go Go Box shoves
me off-comp. This is the **decision tree for when reality diverges from the plan**. Reasoning under it:
[[../thinking_and_mental_model/index]]; opponent context: [[../experience/ranked/general_mythic]]. Pencil
([[../disclaimer]]).

## The master pivot rule: cheap early, expensive late

A pivot costs tempo (sold heroes, restarted breakpoints), and the cost rises through the match because
HP gets expensive (base DMG 3 -> 15) and the board must be finalized by Stage IV. So: **pivot freely in
Stage I-II, commit by mid Stage III, do not pivot in Stage IV** (in IV you double down or you die).
Heroes sell back, items and positioning transfer, breakpoints do not, so a pivot that *keeps a
breakpoint* (same faction, different class, or vice versa) is far cheaper than one that abandons both.

## The contingencies (trigger -> response)

**My carry / comp is contested (draws dry up).**
Someone else is buying it (the pool is finite: 9 of 18 copies for a 3-cost 3-star, 9 of 9 for a
5-cost). Two responses: (a) **pivot** to an archetype the pool is leaving open, ideally one sharing a
synergy I already hold so a breakpoint survives the move; or (b) if I have surplus gold and they are
close to a spike, **contest to deny** (buy the copies they need, stall their 3-star, the
advanced-tactics play). Denial is a rich-player move; pivoting is the default when gold is tight.

**I am behind on HP early (lowroll, weak board).**
HP is cheap in Stage I-II, so a weak start is recoverable and even exploitable. Stop chopping: take a
**committed loss streak** (same streak gold as a win streak) and use the **lowest-HP-first** reward
selections and Go Go Box to pick the best equipment/hero *before* the leaders. Bank hard, hold the
interest band, and **spike back in Stage III** once the wallet is fat. The intentional version of this
is a real top-rank line: [[../experience/ranked/general_mythic]]. The risk is overshooting: at Mythic,
opponents spike early, so do not bleed so low that a single bad round eliminates me before I stabilize.

**I highrolled (early 2-3 stars, strong board).**
Press the advantage into tempo. Run a **win streak** (best econ + zero HP cost), **deny** rivals the
copies they need with surplus gold, and push for **1st** rather than coasting to a safe 4th, the
board has earned the upside. Keep leveling so the lead does not get out-scaled late.

**A carry is sitting uncontested.**
Commit **vertical**: level to the tier where it peaks (L6 for a 3-cost, L8-9 for a 4-cost) and reroll
to **3-star it**. An uncontested vertical is the highest-payoff convex bet in the game; commit hard and
accept the reroll variance.

**Bad item RNG / items on the wrong unit.**
Items have no recipe tree, so nothing bricks: any drop is usable on *someone*. Re-allocate damage items
onto whoever is actually carrying, survival items to the front, and use a **Blessing / Synergy Magic
Crystal** to buy a missing breakpoint instead of fighting the pool for the last copy.

**Capacity-starved (cannot field every breakpoint I want).**
Drop the **weakest breakpoint** (the one adding the least at its current tier) rather than half-filling
two. Lean on **summons** (Dragoncaller dragon, Scavenger heroes, Johnson Johnnies): they add board
presence and +1 Commander DMG without costing capacity. Or level for the extra slot if gold allows.

**Two or more rivals are both in my line.**
The pool cannot feed three of the same comp. Abandon it early for the **open third line** nobody is
contesting; being the *only* one drafting an archetype is worth more than being the third-best in a
crowded one. Read which archetypes the lobby is crowding and take the empty lane.

**The Go Go Box / auction / cards force me off-comp.**
Flex *toward* what the rewards actually gave me rather than fighting it: the card offers are
synergy-biased to what I hold, so a coherent partial board keeps pulling coherent rewards. If a reward
opens a stronger adjacent line that shares a breakpoint, take the redirection.

**Econ collapse (low gold and low HP at once).**
The recovery window is closed; switch to the **all-in conversion**: dump every gold into immediate
board power (levels, rerolls, items), per advanced finance, *convert all gold into battle power or be
eliminated with gold unspent.* Aim to climb one or two placements, not to win; salvaging 5th out of a
dead board is still rank gained.

**Stage IV and not finalized / actively losing.**
No more pivots. **Double down**: reroll for the one spike (a final star-up, the breakpoint that flips
a fight) that could win the next rounds, and accept the variance. Holding gold here is the classic
losing mistake when HP is the binding constraint.

## The two questions I ask at every fork

1. **Did a set-point bend, or only the skin?** (Pivoting tactics = adapting, good; abandoning the
   placement-first discipline to gamble for 1st from behind = dissolving the plan.)
2. **Is this payoff convex or concave?** Convex (a spike that wins outright, an uncontested 3-star):
   commit hard, accept variance. Concave (diminishing, ruin-prone): stay flexible, hedge, diversify
   the breakpoints. Knowing which regime I am in is most of the skill ([[../abstract_generalization]]).
