# General Strategy — climbing to Mythical Honor and Top Global

The play-to-win spine: how to place high consistently enough to climb ranked to Mythic, Mythical
Honor, and Top Global. The reasoning under it is [[../thinking_and_mental_model/index]]; the pivots when a
plan breaks are [[general_contingency_plan]]; the opponent bar at the top is
[[../experience/ranked/general_mythic]]. Grounded in the first-party numbers in `../infos/`. Held in
pencil ([[../disclaimer]]); rebalanced every season.

## What "play to win" means for a climb

Ranked climbs on **placement**, not on first-place-or-nothing. The currency is consistent **top 4**;
a reckless line that wins 1st sometimes and dies 7th-8th often *loses* rank. So the policy is: **play
the line with the highest expected placement**, press for 1st only when the board has actually earned
it, and never throw a safe top-4 chasing a greedy 1st. Survival first, then upside. Read the whole
math of why in [[../thinking_and_mental_model/index]] (HP is the resource; tempo-to-breakpoint is the
objective).

## The game underneath: information, competition, efficiency

MCGG is an **information game** and a **competition**, not a solitaire puzzle. Information: the pool,
the offers, the card bias, and the seven enemy boards are all partly hidden, so I *read* them and act
on the read (scouting and enemy-commander intent: [[../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]]).
Competition: eight players draw from one finite pool, so my gain is often a rival's loss.

There is **no guaranteed win against the randomness; I force one through efficiency** -- converting
scarce gold, EXP, and HP into the *right* breakpoints faster and cheaper than rivals. Efficiency is the
lever that turns variance into wins, and it means spending on power that **lasts**, not power that
merely spikes early.

The cautionary example: **forcing a 1-gold 3-star is usually inefficient.** It eats a pile of rerolls
and gold and only wins the *early* game; a board of cheap 3-stars gets out-scaled the moment opponents
field 4-5 cost power and higher breakpoints. So force a low-cost 3-star only when it is **cheap to hit
AND carries into the mid-late game**, or as a deliberate early-aggro line I then **transition out of**
on schedule. Spend efficiency where it compounds, not where it merely wins round 6. (The reroll math:
[[../thinking_and_mental_model/reroll_contest_auction]].)

## The spine, by stage

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): stay open, do not commit
HP is cheapest here (base DMG 3), so this stage is for *information*, not for winning. Play the
strongest board the draws hand me and keep options wide. Take the **I-1 creep** equipment that fits
the most lines (a flexible damage/ATK-speed item over a niche one). At **I-2**, the Power Card stage:
my Commander's own Power Card is always offered, and offers are synergy-biased toward what I hold, so
pick the card that matches the *direction* my early draws are leaning, not the flashiest text (2
refreshes, one card at a time). Hold gold toward the interest band. Do not over-level. By the end-of-I
**Reward Selection** (lowest-HP-first, 8 equipment + 3 heroes), grab the best generic item or a hero
that opens my likely archetype.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): commit, and hit the first breakpoints
This is where flexibility turns into a comp. Read what the pool is letting me have (uncontested lines)
and what my cards are feeding, then **commit to one archetype** and start crossing breakpoints: aim
to cross a **faction breakpoint and a class breakpoint with the fewest slots** (heroes carry one of
each). From **II-1** the Blessing system is live: combining a 2-star grants a free **+1 synergy
count**, so use it to reach a breakpoint one hero short. Bank to the **interest band (>=20 gold)** and
build a clean **win streak** if the board can hold one (it pays streak gold + victory gold + costs no
HP, the strongest econ state there is). Take the **II-3** Go Go Card + creep. End of II is the
**Auction**: bid for the lots that complete my comp (a key Enhanced item, a needed 4-cost), but do
not blow the interest band on a luxury.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): spike, and take the lead
Now convert econ into board power. **Level to the tier my comp needs** (L6 to flood 3-costs, L8-9 to
fish 4-costs; see the shop clock in [[../thinking_and_mental_model/index]]) and reroll hard to **spike a
6-count synergy or a key carry to 2-3 star**. A synergy-payoff card here is the snowball lever (the
replay won on **Purest Power I**, buffing a 6-count mage core, though that match was vs weak AI, so read it as illustrative). HP is getting
expensive (base DMG 7-11), so the board must now be genuinely winning rounds. Two reward selections
this stage (III-3 has a card + creep + a *second* Blessing Shifter; end-of-III is another
lowest-HP-first reward selection): use them to finish breakpoints or grab a synergy-grant orb.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
Base DMG is 13-15 and **Battle Acceleration** (last 10s, healing/shields slashed, all damage ramps)
forces fast results, so the lineup must be **finalized** before this stage, not assembled in it. Spend
the wallet down: convert all surplus gold into board power (levels for the last capacity, rerolls for
the final star-ups, the best items on the carries). The interest band no longer matters when HP is
the binding constraint, exactly the advanced-finance rule: *when HP is almost out, convert all gold
into battle power, or be eliminated with gold unspent.* A dominant board snowballs here (my wins deal
more HP damage the more surviving high-star units I field), so press the lead to close out kills.

## The economy rules (the convex engine)

- **Ride the interest band.** Keep **>=20 gold** floating whenever survival allows (the +4/round cap
  = 20% ROI, the only free money). Spend only the surplus above 20, until Stage IV.
- **Pick a streak and keep it clean.** A **win streak** is the best state (streak gold + victory gold
  + zero HP cost). If the board genuinely cannot win, do not chop: take a **committed loss streak**
  (same streak gold) and harvest the lowest-HP reward priority while HP is cheap (the intentional-loss
  line: [[../experience/ranked/general_mythic]]). Win-lose-win-lose pays neither streak; it is the
  worst econ.
- **Spend triggers.** Spend gold (level or reroll) when: the spend wins more HP than the interest is
  worth (true early when HP is cheap is the *opposite*, so this bites mid-late), a key 3-star is one
  or two copies away, or HP is low enough that banking is moot.

## Leveling cadence (comp-dependent, not a fixed rush)

Leveling buys **capacity** (more breakpoints at once) and **high-tier shop odds**, paid 1 gold = 1
EXP on top of 2 free/round. Match the pace to the comp: **sit at the level where my target tier peaks**
and reroll (over-leveling dilutes the tier I am fishing), and **push levels fast** only when I want
4-5 cost units or need capacity to field another breakpoint. At Mythic, falling behind on level is a
common way to get out-scaled, so do not *under*-level either (see the Mythic note).

## Board craft (the policy the auto-battle runs)

- **Breakpoints over bodies.** A synergy off does nothing; at its count it flips kind. Field toward
  breakpoints; never spread thin below all of them.
- **Position front/back.** Melee/high-DEF front, ranged/high-DMG carries back and protected from the
  enemy's burst and divers. Carries go back-*center*, never naked in a corner, because most Swiftblade
  heroes jump to the back-row side to assassinate. Full positioning, including the dive counters:
  [[../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].
- **Front line: how many, and what star.** Roughly a third to a half of the board is front-row tank:
  about **2 tanks on a 6-unit board, 3 on an 8-unit, 3-4 on a 9-10-unit**, enough to wall so enemy
  melee cannot path around to the carry. The front must **lead on stars**: a 1-star tank melts and
  exposes the carry, so aim for **2-star front by mid Stage III** (HP ~1.8x per star step is the
  threshold that actually holds); 3-star tanks are a luxury, not a requirement. A **Dauntless** [2]/[4]
  (team-wide shields) or **Defender** [2]/[4] (DEF) substitutes for raw bodies, letting a smaller front
  hold. Carries can sit at 1-2 star if well-itemized; it is the front that must not be under-starred.
- **Items onto the carries.** No recipe tree, so any drop is usable; stack the damage/ATK-speed items
  on the unit doing the damage, and survival items on the front. Enhanced adds a passive.
- **Cards for the comp, not the text.** A Go Go Card bends the *whole run*; pick the one that pays the
  comp I am building (lifesteal for a sustain board, free-star-up for the heroes I am committing to,
  econ for a later spike).
- **Summons are free value.** Units that do not cost capacity (Dragoncaller dragon, Scavenger heroes,
  Johnson Johnnies) add board presence and +1 Commander DMG each; fit synergies that summon when the
  capacity is tight.

## The Mythic adjustment (the top of the ladder)

Edges that win at low rank stop working at Mythic; opponents power early (4-5 cost by early Stage II),
run **Scavenger/Neobeasts** econ, and **level fast**. So at the top: **match tempo** (do not sit on a
weak board for pure interest), **contest the economy synergies** or out-tempo them, **keep leveling**,
and **greed only from strength**. Full read: [[../experience/ranked/general_mythic]].

## The one-glance checklist

1. Stage I: open, gather info, cheap HP, take the fitting Power Card.
2. Stage II: commit to the open archetype, cross first breakpoints, ride interest + a clean streak.
3. Stage III: level to tier, spike a 6-count or carry, take the lead, grab reward orbs.
4. Stage IV: finalize, dump gold into power, close before HP gets expensive.
5. Always: >=20 gold floating, one clean streak, breakpoints over bodies, carries in back with items.
