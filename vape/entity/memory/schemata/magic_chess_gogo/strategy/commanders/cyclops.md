# Commander — Cyclops (Asta): the elimination scavenger

The deep, concrete playbook, round by round. **In pencil**, reasoned from

[[../../infos/encyclopedia/hero]] (the pool), and the round calendar [[../../infos/rounds/index]];

## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** late-scaling value / scavenger. He snowballs off *other players' deaths*, gold + stolen
  high-star heroes, and has a built-in catch-up.
- **Plays like:** a patient survivor, weather the early-mid, then feast as the lobby starts dying,
  harvesting gold and a free hero (at its star level) from each elimination.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is back-loaded; the skill is *surviving to the elimination phase* and a
  stable board that lasts, then flexing the stolen heroes.
- **Best into:** long games and a lobby that thins out. **Worst into:** fast aggro that kills *him* early
  (he gets nothing if out first) and a game that ends before eliminations pile up.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Cyclops, "Asta." Skins: Asta, Clover Charm. (There is no *hero* Cyclops in the S6 shop roster,
  so no commander/hero collision.)
- **Astral Blessing** [Passive] (verbatim): *"When another Commander is eliminated, Cyclops opens a
  Star-Core Jar to gain Gold equal to 14% of the eliminated Commander's lineup value and 1 random Hero(es)
  from their lineup at their current star level(s)."*
- **Astral Resonance** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"When your HP first drops to 80 or below, gain
  3 Hero(es) of your current highest-count Synergy and 1 Equipment Chest(s)."*
- **Power Card:** *Cyclops' Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Gain 8 Gold. When any Commander
  is eliminated, gain 15% of their deployed Heroes' total cost as Gold."*

Reading (in pencil): every time **another commander is eliminated**, Cyclops gets **gold (14% of their
lineup value) + a random hero from their lineup at its current star** (so he can inherit a dead player's
**2-3 star** unit, a huge free body). Astral Resonance is a **one-time catch-up**: at **HP <= 80**, gain
**3 heroes of my highest synergy + an Equipment Chest** (stabilizes a slipping board *and* thickens my
axis). The card adds **+8 gold now + more elimination gold**. He is a **back-loaded late-scaler**: weak
early (the kit needs deaths), brutal once the lobby thins.

Research notes (2026-07-10, `../../research/cyclops/`, three transcripts; the kit above verified
against live play): the inheritance ("warisan") fires on surrenders too, but **skilled opponents
resign EARLY at high HP with cheap boards**, and 14% of a small lineup is small ("the inheritance is
really tiny, only 4 gold"), a real consistency ding in good lobbies. The catch-up's 3-hero drop
arrives with items visible and can land as early as Stage I-II if the hold is aggressive. Last
season's mandatory hold-early line is gone: **the combo is free now** ("combos counter each other
this season, adjust to what the boxes hand you"). And the community's survival bar is concrete:
**outlive four players and Cyclops is safe**, the back half of the lobby is where the kit prints.

## Win condition / identity

**Survive to the elimination phase, then snowball off the dying.** Cyclops does little in Stage I-II (no
one is dead yet), so the plan is a **stable, survivable board** that reaches Stage III-IV intact; then as
commanders fall (base DMG 7-15, kills come), he harvests gold + free high-star heroes from each, rocketing
past the field. The Astral Resonance catch-up keeps him from being the *first* to die. He is a **value
engine triggered by eliminations**: the longer he survives and the more the lobby thins, the stronger he
gets.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Survive without feeding (the early-mid).** His kit is dormant until deaths start, so the whole early
   game is "do not die, and do not be the first out" (he gets nothing if eliminated first, and a soft
   board makes him a target). Spend enough to stay safe, lean on the Astral Resonance catch-up.
2. **Flex the stolen heroes.** Inherited heroes are whatever the dead players ran (random from their
   lineup), so **convert them**: slot a stolen carry, use one as star-up fodder, or sell. The stolen
   high-star units can reshape my board, stay flexible to absorb them.

## The scavenge plan (his signature, concrete)

The ordered plan:
1. **Build a stable, survivable board** (a frontline + a safe carry) that lasts to Stage III, the kit is
   useless if I die early. (The community bar: outlive four players and the run is safe.)
2. **Bank a healthy economy** so I am not forced low (the catch-up triggers at <=80 HP, but I would rather
   not flirt with elimination myself). The research line even opens with light Scavenger/Neobeast
   farming (the shop-lock trick, [[../scavenger_tempo]], serves him as well as anyone: his kit asks
   nothing of the shop).
3. **Steer the <=80 catch-up on purpose**: it reads my **highest-count synergy**, so commit an axis
   BEFORE dipping; a research game dipped early with Exorcist committed and the 3 free bodies +
   chest turned the slide around on the spot.
4. **As commanders fall (Stage III-IV), harvest** the gold + free high-star heroes; **flex** them into
   my board (a stolen 2-3 star carry can become my new threat). Budget auction Mirrors at <=9 gold
   (the research pilot's cap) and bank them for a **5-cost 3-star** late, the inheritance gold makes
   that hunt honest.
5. **Snowball the lead**: each elimination compounds, so press once the harvest starts. Expect less
   from lobbies of surrender-savvy players (early resigns shrink the 14%).

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): survive, build stable
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Build a stable early board (a frontline + a safe carry); the kit is dormant,
  so just be solid. Creep: a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take his card for the +8 gold** (the elimination gold pays later); it is somewhat
  back-loaded, so if I badly need early tempo a generic econ card is a fine deviation, but +8 now + scaling
  is usually fine. Keep, lean.
- **I-3 / I-4:** bank toward the band, keep the board stable.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a frontline piece (survival is the early plan).

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): stay alive, commit a synergy
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; **commit a synergy** (so the <=80 catch-up's 3
  free heroes thicken it).
- **Hold a stable board**, bank, do not over-extend (no one is dying yet, so my kit is still quiet).
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for a frontline/survival piece or a Synergy Magic Crystal.
- **Leveling:** steady to the carry's tier; do not fall behind (I need to survive to Stage III).

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): the harvest begins
- **As the first commanders fall**, harvest gold + a free high-star hero each; **flex** the steals into my
  board. The <=80 catch-up (if I dip) hands 3 synergy heroes + a chest.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, gear the carry.
- Press the snowball as the lobby thins.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): feast and close
- Every elimination compounds my gold + body lead; flex stolen high-star heroes into a dominant board.
  Close before a *single* spiked rival closes me out (I want to be the one harvesting, not the harvested).

## Leveling cadence

Steady and survival-first; he needs to **reach the elimination phase**, so do not under-level (a soft
board dies early and the kit never fires). The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3:
`L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. The elimination gold (and the catch-up
heroes) fund late spikes, so bank a stable economy through the early-mid.

## Best comps (concrete)

A **stable, survivable shell that lasts**, then absorbs steals:
- **Frontline + one carry** ([[../frontline_tank]]): the safest way to survive to the harvest; the stolen
  heroes flex in around it.
- **A committed synergy** (so the <=80 catch-up's 3 free heroes thicken my axis), e.g. a Mage or Dauntless
  core.
- The constant: **survival first**, then flex the elimination steals into a late-scaling board.

## Positioning

Standard, survival-tilted (a solid wall, carry back-center, spread vs AoE) because reaching the late game
intact is the plan. Stolen high-star heroes may want a seat, re-position to fit them. Full detail:
[[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## Power-card deep dive

His card (**+8 gold + 15% of eliminated commanders' deployed cost as gold**) is **on-kit but
back-loaded**: the +8 helps now, the elimination gold only pays once players die. Take it as a default
(the +8 alone is decent and the scaling is real), but **deviate to a generic econ card** if I need early
tempo to survive, his kit and card both reward the late game, so early HP safety can matter more. Use the
2 refreshes to weigh it.

**Cross-card fishing: Nana's card is his best borrow in the pool.** It is NOT marked exclusive
(8 gold + an Equipment Chest + **one fatal-DMG immunity on the commander**), and one extra survived
round is literally his kit's binding constraint, insurance on the survive-to-harvest plan that also
pays tempo now. Behind it: **Vexana's** (extra Go Go Box picks + 13g at the auction, feeding his
mirror-hunting budget) and **Fanny's** (+1 capacity, a thicker wall). All three often beat his own
back-loaded card in an aggressive lobby.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fast aggro** that kills *me* early is the nightmare (eliminated first = the kit never fires), so
  **survive at all costs** in the early-mid; I am the one commander who most must not die first.
- **Other late-scalers** (Neobeasts, value engines) also want a long game; I beat them *if* I survive to
  out-harvest the eliminations.
- I **profit from the lobby thinning**, so let aggressive players trade each other down while I stay
  stable.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **Behind early (soft board):** spend to stabilize, the <=80 catch-up (3 synergy heroes + chest) is a
  built-in rescue, but do not rely on it, survival is the plan.
- **Stolen heroes off-comp:** flex them as fodder/sell, or pivot toward a stolen high-star carry if it is
  strong enough to re-anchor my board.
- **No eliminations yet (mid-game lull):** play a normal stable board; the kit pays once deaths start, so
  patience.
- **Stage IV, leading off steals:** press hard, each elimination compounds; close before a rival spikes.

## The edge (the secret)

**He turns the lobby's deaths into his economy and his board.** Every elimination hands Cyclops gold + a
free high-star hero, and the <=80 catch-up keeps him alive to harvest, so the longer the game and the more
the field thins, the stronger he gets, scaling off the exact thing that ends other players. A back-loaded
engine that feasts on a dying lobby.

## Pitfalls

- **Dying first** (the kit never fires, and a soft board makes me a target).
- **Over-extending early** for tempo his kit does not reward yet.
- **Not committing a synergy** before the <=80 catch-up (the 3 free heroes are wasted off-axis).
- **Letting stolen heroes sit idle** instead of flexing them in.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** that eliminates him early is his hard counter (the kit is dormant until deaths, so an early
death = nothing). A **game that ends fast** (few eliminations) starves him. He is strongest in **long,
attritional games** where the lobby thins and he harvests; weakest in fast ones or if focused early. A
back-loaded scaler whose whole skill is surviving to the feast.

## Numbers that matter (the scaling, quantified)

- **Per elimination:** gold = **14% of the dead lineup's value** (kit) **+ 15% of their deployed cost**
  (card) **+ a free hero at its current star** (potentially a 2-3 star). With 7 rivals, that is a lot of
  compounding gold + bodies as the lobby thins.
- **The catch-up:** at **HP <= 80** (first time), **3 heroes of my highest synergy + 1 Equipment Chest**, a
  one-time stabilizer that also thickens my axis, hence committing a synergy first.
- **The clock:** eliminations start ~Stage III (base DMG 7-11) and accelerate in IV (13-15), so his power
  curve is back-loaded to match.

## Stage checkpoints (am I on pace?)

- vs **combat** (Kagura, Wanwan): their board is the threat; wall up, survive, and harvest the lobby's
