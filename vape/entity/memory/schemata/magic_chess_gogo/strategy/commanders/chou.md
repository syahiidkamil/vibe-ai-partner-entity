## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** economy / stable snowball. He gains gold on a **win OR a loss**, so his economy is
  result-agnostic, the steadiest in the roster.
- **Plays like:** a flexible econ player, bank gold no matter the outcome, ride either streak freely, and
  spike a strong mid-late board.
- **Difficulty:** low-medium. The kit is passive gold; the skill is converting the stable lead into a board
  (the value-engine anti-greed discipline).
- **Best into:** any lobby (his econ is universal). **Worst into:** fast aggro that out-tempos a slow build,
  and hoarding so long the board never spikes.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Chou, "Kung Fu Boy." Skins: Kung Fu Boy, Kung Fu Kid. (There is no *hero* Chou in the S6 shop
  roster, so no commander/hero collision.)
- **Warrior's Honor** [Passive] (verbatim): *"Gain 2 Gold after winning a round."*
- **Win or Lose** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Additionally, gain 3 Gold after losing a round."*
- **Power Card:** *Chou's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Obtain Chou's Sash. After killing 3
  enemies, obtain 1 Equipment Chest (max 4)."*

Reading (in pencil): he gains **+2 gold on a win** and (starred) **+3 gold on a loss**, so **every round
pays**, his economy does not care about win/loss streaks (which normally force a choice). That makes him
the most **flexible** econ commander: he can win-streak for tempo *or* loss-streak for reward priority and
**bank gold either way**, on top of the normal streak bonus. The card gives **Chou's Sash** (a strong
carry item: +60% Hybrid ATK, and an Equipment Chest per 3 kills). A **stable econ snowball**.

Research notes (2026-07-10, `../../research/chou/`, five transcripts): the lose-side gold is a
**recent buff** the community is loud about ("you earn extra gold whether you win or lose, a huge
economic advantage"), and top players now frame him as **a combat commander with a built-in economy**,
strong enough to fund 3-star 5-costs. The Sash's chest gate was also reportedly buffed (one video:
kills needed dropped 6 -> 4; the datamined text reads 3); verify the live number in-game before
relying on it.

## Win condition / identity

**Out-economy the lobby with result-proof gold, then spike a strong board.** Because he banks gold whether
he wins or loses, Chou rides whichever line the board wants (win-streak when strong, loss-streak when weak)
without sacrificing econ, accumulating a lead he converts into a spiked mid-late board. He is a **flexible
econ commander** like a steadier Lancelot (Lancelot is faster-leveling but volatile; Chou is stable and
banks). The skill is the value-engine discipline: convert the lead before greed kills him.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Win-streak vs loss-streak (he is free to choose).** Most commanders are punished for choppy results;
   Chou is paid either way, so **pick the line the board supports**, win-streak if strong (tempo + his +2 +
   the streak bonus), loss-streak if weak (reward priority + his +3 + the streak bonus), and never chop
   pointlessly.
2. **Convert the lead (anti-greed).** His stable gold tempts hoarding; **spike the board mid-game** rather
   than banking into a dead game (the Mythic anti-greed trap).

## The econ plan (his signature, concrete)

The ordered way to run the economy:
1. **Ride the band from 17-18, not 20 (the community's Chou-only trick).** His end-of-round gold tops
   the bank before the next interest tick, so holding **17-18 gold going into the fight** still lands
   the full band ("interest at 18: win and it reads 21"). He plays 2-3 gold deeper than everyone else
   at the same interest, every round, which compounds into the fastest honest leveling in the roster.
2. **Choose the streak the board supports** (win for tempo, loss for reward priority) and ride it clean,
   he banks gold on either, so the choice is purely board-driven.
3. **Stack Scavenger on top when the pieces flow** (Angela + Phoveus): "double farming", his passive
   plus the free hero, and the **shop-lock trick** ([[../scavenger_tempo]]) turns the [2] into
   guaranteed gold; he is one of the trick's most natural users since his own econ never depended on
   holding the shop.
4. **Take the card (Chou's Sash)** if I have a kill-heavy carry (a strong +60% ATK item + chests) and
   feed it to the item-farmer (Mia early, or a Lunox that spams ultimates: "put Chou's item on Lunox
   and it will definitely deliver"); else a generic econ/synergy card. **The Ruby-card tech** (from a
   rank-1-global match): borrow **Ruby's card** instead, his passive alone out-earns most flat-gold
   cards (Ruby's caps at 35; his passive passes 35 by ~round 12), so the card slot buys Ruby's
   Cloak+Scythe combat value on top of an economy he already has.
5. **Spike mid-game**: the stable lead funds a roll-down + star-ups; do not hoard into Stage IV.
6. **Stay flexible** on the comp (the gold funds any line the pool opens).

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): bank, stay flexible
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Flexible early board; his gold banks regardless of results. Creep: a flexible
  damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take Chou's Sash** if a kill-heavy carry is forming (a strong +60% ATK item +
  chests); else a **generic econ/synergy card**. Situational.
- **I-3 / I-4:** ride whichever streak the board supports (paid either way), bank the interest band.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero opening my line (if I lose-streaked, I pick
  early).

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): out-econ, commit
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit a synergy.
- **Bank the stable lead** (>=20 gold + his result-proof gold), commit the comp the pool opens.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** I am **gold-rich**, bid well on a key item or a Synergy Magic Crystal.
- **Leveling:** the lead funds a steady push to the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): spike
- **Convert the econ lead into board power:** roll to star up, field the carries, take a payoff card. This
  is the anti-greed moment, spend the lead.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, gear the carry.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Dump the bank into the last star-ups and items. Close before HP is the binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Steady; the result-proof gold funds a normal-to-fast level + roll cadence to the carry's tier. The upgrade
ladder, cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. He is
gold-rich, so he can both ride the interest band and level/roll; the discipline is spending the lead
mid-game, not hoarding.

## Power-card deep dive

His card (**Chou's Sash**: +60% Hybrid ATK + an Equipment Chest per 3 kills, max 4) is **good on a
kill-heavy carry** (a strong item + free chests), but he is an econ commander, so on a non-kill board a
**generic econ card** (Trust Fund, Vault Upgrade+) that stacks his lead is often better. Situational: take
the Sash for a carry, a generic econ for the snowball. Use the 2 refreshes to weigh it.

**Cross-card fishing (the Chou principle: buy COMBAT with the card slot, his passive already prints
gold).** Flat-gold borrowables are near-wasted on him (his passive out-earns Ruby's 35-cap by ~round
12, the rank-1-global game's exact argument), so fish the kit-pieces instead: **Ruby's borrowable**
(Scythe + Cloak, a free sticky front, the researched tech), **Karina's borrowable** (Twinblades on
his burst carry), **Zilong's borrowable** (Dragon Spear on the Marksman line), **Miya's** (12g +
Lunar Longbow feeds the 6-MM standard), or **Fanny's** (+1 capacity, the universal). The one gold
card that still earns: **Kagura's** (+9g AND a carry buff, both halves useful).

## Contingency and pivots

- **Weak board:** **loss-streak freely** (he is paid +3/loss + the streak bonus, and grabs reward
  priority), then spike back, his result-proof gold makes this line cleaner than for anyone.
- **Strong board:** win-streak (tempo + his +2 + streak + interest), press the HP lead.
- **Hoarding too long:** spend the lead now (anti-greed); a fat bank in a dead game is wasted.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** convert all gold into the board and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**Gold no matter the result.** Chou is the only commander truly free to choose his streak, win for tempo or
lose for reward priority, and bank gold either way (plus the normal streak bonus), so his economy is the
steadiest and most flexible in the roster. He out-econs the lobby without the win/loss tension everyone
else fights, and converts the stable lead into a spiked board.

## Pitfalls

- **Greed** (hoarding the stable lead into a dead game; spend it mid-game).
- **Choppy results** (he is paid for a *clean* streak either way; do not win-lose-win-lose pointlessly,
  though it stings less for him than most).
- **Taking the Sash without a kill-carry** (a generic econ card stacks his lead better there).
- **Slow-building into fast aggro** (convert the lead earlier if pressured).

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** out-tempos a slow build (convert earlier). **Hoarding** is the self-inflicted threat (a fat
bank that never spikes). He has no hard counter, his econ is universal, so his only real enemy is his own
greed and a game that ends before he spikes. A stable, flexible econ snowball, the safest economy in the
roster.
