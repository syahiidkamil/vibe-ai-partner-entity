## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** copy / value-flex. A trap steals 1-Star copies of *enemy* heroes off the lobby, plus gold
  per capture.
- **Plays like:** a scavenger of other people's boards, place the trap to catch an enemy each round,
  harvest free bodies + gold, and flex them into my comp or sell them.
- **Difficulty:** medium-high. The kit is positioning-driven (trap placement to catch enemies) and the
  captures are *random* (whatever steps in), so it rewards adaptability over a fixed plan.
- **Best into:** value mirrors and long games (captures + gold compound). **Worst into:** fast aggro that
  ends before the harvest pays, and boards that position to dodge the trap.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Popol and Kupa, "Icefield Companions" (one commander, a hero pair). Skins: Icefield
  Companions, Dustland Pals. (There is no *hero* Popol in the S6 shop roster, so no commander/hero
  collision.)
- **Copy Trap** [Passive] (verbatim): *"During the Preparation Stage, a movable Copy Trap will be
  generated on your side of the battlefield. When an enemy Hero steps into the trap, obtain their 1-Star
  version, up to 1 time(s) per round."*
- **Hunter's Mark** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Gain 1 Gold when copying a Hero. If no Hero is
  copied by the end of the round, gain 1 Gold."*
- **Power Cards:** exclusive *Popol and Kupa's Power* - *"Gain 2 Gold when capturing a Hero. Gain 2
  Equipment Chest(s) after capturing 10 Hero(es) for the first time."* **(Exclusive)**; borrowable
  *Popol and Kupa's Power* - *"From Round II-1, generate a Copy Trap. Once/round, if an enemy steps in,
  obtain their 1-Star version and 1 Gold; otherwise gain 1 Gold."*

Reading (in pencil): the **Copy Trap** harvests **free 1-Star copies of enemy heroes** (whatever steps in,
once per round), and Hunter's Mark pays **+1 gold per capture (or +1 if none)**, so it is **never a dead
round**. The exclusive adds **+2 gold per capture + 2 Equipment Chests at 10 captures** (a consistent
econ + gear reward). The catch: captures are **random** (the enemy's heroes, not mine) and
**positioning-driven** (the trap must be placed where enemies step), so he is a **flexible value/econ
harvester**, not a fixed vertical. The borrowable card just re-grants the trap, redundant on the owner.

Rethink (2026-07-10, first principles): his harvest is indexed to **the enemy's board, not the
shared pool**, which gives him two properties nothing else has: bench-holding cannot starve him
(the deny meta is aimed at shops and pools, his trap reads deployment), and his captures are
automatically **meta-correlated** (what steps into the trap is what the lobby actually fields, so
his fodder doubles as scouting made flesh: sell it, star it, or field the lobby's own tech back at
it). The trap is also a small **positional weapon**, enemies that path around it concede formation
shape for free. What caps him is the same mechanism: he cannot CHOOSE what he receives, so the kit
floors placement (never a dead round, chests at 10) and never spikes. Trick era: gold half
commoditized, capture half untouched; the trap and the shop-lock stack without conflict.

## Win condition / identity

**Out-resource the lobby by farming its own heroes, then flex the harvest into a board.** Every round he
banks gold and (usually) a free enemy copy, so his economy and body-count outpace the field while costing
him nothing. He is a **value/econ-flex** commander: the captures fill synergies, feed star-ups, or sell
for gold, and the steady gold + the 10-capture chests fund a strong late board. He does not force a comp,
he **lets the harvest and the open pool shape one**, like a Scavenger econ with theft attached.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Trap placement (the skill).** The trap catches an enemy only if positioned where one **steps**, so
   reading the enemy board's movement and placing the trap on a likely path is the whole capture rate.
   Bad placement = no capture (still +1 gold, but the body is the prize).
2. **What to do with random captures.** Captures are whatever the enemy fields, so I must **flex**: keep a
   captured hero that fits my synergy or feeds a star-up, **sell** one that does not (for gold), or use it
   as Blessing/synergy filler. Hoarding off-comp captures clutters; convert them.

## The trap loop (his signature, concrete)

The ordered plan for the harvester:
1. **Place the trap each Preparation Stage** on a tile enemies are likely to step onto (toward the
   contact line, or where an enemy carry/diver moves). Re-place it as I scout their board.
2. **Bank the capture + gold:** a captured enemy 1-star + Hunter's Mark gold (+ the exclusive's +2). Even
   a miss is +1 gold, so it is never wasted.
3. **Triage the capture:** keep it if it fits my synergy / feeds a star-up; **sell** it for gold if not;
   use it as Blessing or ritual/Core fodder for other plans if applicable.
4. **Chase the 10-capture chests** (exclusive): consistent capturing pays 2 Equipment Chests, so
   prioritize good trap placement to hit 10.
5. **Let the comp crystallize** around the densest captured + bought synergy; commit by mid-game.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): start harvesting, stay flexible
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Place the trap; build a flexible early board. Creep: a flexible damage
  basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take the exclusive** (+2 gold per capture + 2 chests at 10), NOT the borrowable
  (which redundantly re-grants the trap I already have). The exclusive monetizes the harvest; keep it.
- **I-3 / I-4:** harvest captures + gold each round, triage them, bank toward the band.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero opening the line the captures favor.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): commit around the harvest
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star (a captured copy can be the third) for a +1 count; commit a
  synergy the captures + pool favor.
- **Keep harvesting** (gold + bodies); convert off-comp captures to gold. Cross the first breakpoints.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a card matching the committed line.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** I am **gold-rich** (steady capture gold), so bid well on a key item or a
  Synergy Magic Crystal.
- **Leveling:** the gold lead funds steady leveling + rolling.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): cash the lead
- **The harvest's gold + chests fund a spike:** roll to star up, field the carries, take a payoff card.
  The 10-capture chests should have landed (gear for the carry).
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, more gear.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the board the harvest built, dump the gold into the last star-ups and items. Close before HP is the
  binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Gold-rich (steady capture gold + the exclusive's +2), so he can **level and roll** more freely than most.
The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`.
Level to the carry's tier and for capacity; the harvest funds the climb without sacrificing the interest
band.

## Best comps (concrete)

**Flexible**, shaped by the harvest:
- **A value-flex board** that commits to whatever synergy the captures + pool densest favor (often a
  frontline + one carry, the safe shell).
- **A go-wide board** (the random captures spread synergies, pairing with Mystic Meow / Solid Alliance).
- The constant: I do not force a comp at the lobby screen, I **let the harvest crystallize one** and
  commit by mid Stage II.

## Positioning

**Unusually important**, because the trap is positional. Each prep, place the trap on a tile enemies will
**step onto** (toward the contact line, on a diver's likely path, near an enemy carry that repositions).
Read the fight preview and re-place it to maximize captures. My own board positions normally (carry
back-center, wall front, spread vs AoE), but the trap placement is an extra, capture-focused decision the
other commanders do not have. Full detail: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## A worked line (one ideal game)

I-1 L3: place the trap toward the contact line, flexible board. I-2: take the exclusive. Stage I-II: I
capture an enemy hero most rounds + bank gold; I keep the ones that fit a forming frontline-and-carry
shell, sell the rest. By mid-II I am the richest at the table (capture gold) and have a flexible board.
Auction: I win a key item off the gold lead. III: the 10-capture chests land (gear for my carry); I roll
the gold lead to star up and spike. IV: the harvest-funded board closes it. Top placement off the lobby's
own heroes.

## Power-card deep dive

His **exclusive** (+2 gold per capture, 2 Equipment Chests at 10 captures) is the right pick, it monetizes
the harvest the kit already does and rewards consistent capturing with gear. Keep it. The **borrowable**
is a **trap on the owner** (it re-grants the Copy Trap he already has), it exists for *other* commanders
to borrow a trap. Use the 2 refreshes only if a synergy/capacity card clearly wins more.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fast aggro** ends the game before the harvest compounds, protect HP and bank the early captures + gold.
- **Boards that position to dodge the trap** lower my capture rate, re-place the trap and read their
  movement; the gold floor (+1 on a miss) keeps it from being dead.
- I am **draw-flexible** (I farm the lobby), so I rarely lose the pool war; my risk is **tempo and trap
  execution**.

## Contingency and pivots

- **Low capture rate (bad placement / dodging enemies):** re-read their board and re-place the trap on a
  likelier path; lean on the gold floor and the bought board until captures improve.
- **Captures all off-comp:** sell them for gold (fueling rolls) rather than cluttering the bench; the gold
  is the consolation.
- **Behind early:** the steady capture gold is a stabilizer; bank and let it compound, commit the comp the
  captures favor.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** stop harvesting for value, lock the board and dump the gold lead.

## The edge (the secret)

**He farms the lobby's own heroes for free, and never has a dead round.** The Copy Trap turns other
players' boards into my recruitment pool, and Hunter's Mark + the exclusive guarantee gold every round
(capture or not) plus gear at 10 captures. A flexible value/econ engine that out-resources the field by
stealing from it, paid for by good trap placement.

## Pitfalls

- **Taking the borrowable card** (redundant, re-grants the trap).
- **Lazy trap placement** (no captures = the engine underperforms; placement is the skill).
- **Hoarding off-comp captures** instead of selling them for gold.
- **Forcing a fixed comp** instead of flexing into what the harvest + pool open.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the harvest compounds. **Disciplined positioners** can lower his
capture rate by keeping heroes off the trap's reach (though the gold floor cushions it). He is strongest
in **long value games** where captures + gold + chests snowball; weakest in fast ones. A flexible
value/econ harvester whose floor is high (never a dead round) and whose ceiling is a board funded by
theft.

## Trap placement (read by read)

Placement is the capture rate, so read the enemy board each prep and place accordingly. Concrete reads
(in pencil):
- **vs a melee-forward board (Bruiser/Defender front):** their front-line units advance into contact, so
  place the trap **on the contact line, toward their advance**, the front bodies step into it reliably.
  Easy captures, often a tank or bruiser.
- **vs a Swiftblade/diver board (Ling, Saber, Helcurt):** the divers **jump to my back-row side**, so
  place the trap **near my back corners on the dive path**, I capture the assassin that lands (and blunt
  its dive). High-value captures.
- **vs a ranged/caster back-line:** their carries hang back and may not step, so place the trap on the
  **mid-line their front crosses**, accept I am catching front bodies, not the carry, the +1 gold floor
  covers a likely miss on the carry.
- **vs a spread board:** units fan out, so place the trap on the **densest movement lane** (often the
  center), maximizing the chance *someone* steps in.
- **General rule:** re-place every prep from the fight preview; a static trap on a board that already
  routed around it captures nothing. The trap is a per-round decision, not a set-and-forget.
The capture is **random within whoever steps in**, so I cannot pick the exact hero, but I *can* raise the
odds of catching *a* useful one (or the enemy's key unit) by where I place it. Good placement is the
difference between a value engine and a +1-gold-per-round trickle.
