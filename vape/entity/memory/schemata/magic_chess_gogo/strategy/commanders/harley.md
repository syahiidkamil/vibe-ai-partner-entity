# Commander — Harley (Mage Genius): the 5-cost shop flood

The deep, concrete playbook, round by round. **In pencil**, reasoned from
[[../../infos/commanders/resolved_commanders]] (the verbatim kit), [[../../infos/gogo_cards/power_cards]],
[[../../infos/encyclopedia/hero]] (the shop odds his kit bypasses), and the round calendar
[[../../infos/rounds/index]]; **not yet lived**, so verify by play and overwrite. Frame: [[index]];
spine: [[../general_strategy]]; the general (emergent) tiers: [[../tier_lists]]; the reasoning under it:
[[../../thinking_and_mental_model/index]].

## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** shop control / high-cost access. He fills the shop with 5-Gold heroes on demand, fielding
  legendaries the odds would deny.
- **Plays like:** a normal econ board through Stage I, then from II-2 he conjures 5-costs at will, buys the
  strongest, and out-powers the lobby's mid-game.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is a powerful on-demand active; the skill is having the *gold* to cash
  the flood and picking the right 5-cost for the comp.
- **Best into:** lobbies that climb to 5-costs slowly (he beats them there). **Worst into:** other
  high-cost rushers (Lancelot, a second Harley) contesting the 5-cost pool, and gold-poor spots where he
  cannot afford the heroes he conjures.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Harley, "Mage Genius." Skins: Mage Genius, Prodigy Mage. (There is no *hero* Harley in the
  S6 shop roster, so no commander/hero collision.)
- **Midas Touch!** [Active] (verbatim): *"Usable from Round II-2. Refresh the Shop 1 time(s) to fill it
  with 5-Gold Hero ×3. Gain 1 free Hero purchase this round."*
- **Gold! Gold! Gold!** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"The Shop refresh in the Active Skill now
  fills the Shop with 5-Gold Hero ×5."*
- **Power Card:** *Harley's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Gain 6 Gold. The Shop
  auto-refreshes once to show at least 4-Gold Hero ×2."*

Reading (in pencil): from **Round II-2** his active **floods the shop with 5-Gold heroes** (3, then 5 at
star-up) plus a **free buy**, so he sees and picks **5-cost legendaries when the normal odds are ~1-5%**
([[../../infos/encyclopedia/hero]]). That is enormous early-access: a 5-cost spike a full stage before
the lobby can roll into one. The card adds early **4-cost** access (auto-refresh showing 4-Gold x2) plus
+6 gold. The catch: 5-costs cost **5 gold each**, so the flood is only as good as the **gold** I have to
cash it, the free buy covers one, the rest I pay for.

## Win condition / identity

**Field 5-cost power before anyone, and pick the best one for my board.** Where Lancelot reaches high
costs by out-leveling, Harley reaches them by **flooding the shop**, so he gets first pick of the
strongest 5-cost (Odette, Lesley, Ling, Ruby, Bane, ...) and can even chase a 2-star of one (the flood
shows many copies). He is a **high-cost tempo** commander: spike a 5-cost mid-game, convert it to an HP
lead, and close before the lobby's economies mature into their own 5-costs. The whole plan rides on
having the **gold to buy** what he conjures.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Gold to cash the flood.** The active shows 5-costs but does not pay for them, so I must **bank a
   healthy economy** (the interest band, the card's +6, a clean streak) so that when I flood, I can
   actually buy 2-3 of the 5-costs, not just stare at them. A broke Harley wastes his kit.
2. **Which 5-cost, and how many.** The flood lets me **pick the strongest 5-cost for my synergy** (or
   chase a 2-star of one if the flood keeps showing it). Greedily buying every 5-cost dilutes my board;
   buy the **one or two that fit my comp** and bank the rest.

## The flood loop (his signature, concrete)

The ordered plan for the active (from II-2):
1. **Bank gold through Stage I-II-1** (interest band, the card's +6, a streak) so I have a war-chest when
   II-2 opens the active.
2. **At II-2, flood** (Midas Touch): the shop fills with 5-Gold heroes + a free buy. **Take the free one**
   (the best 5-cost for my line), then **buy 1-2 more** I can afford that fit.
3. **Pick for the comp, not the rarity:** a 5-cost that anchors my synergy (Odette for a mage core,
   Lesley for marksman, Ruby/Bane for a front) over a random legendary.
4. **At the star-up (Gold! Gold! Gold!), the flood shows 5 of them**, so I can chase a **2-star 5-cost**
   (normally near-impossible: 9 of 9 copies) by flooding repeatedly when rich.
5. **Convert the spike to HP:** the early 5-cost board should win rounds, press the lead before the lobby
   reaches its own 5-costs at L9-10.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): bank, the active is not online yet
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Play a normal early board toward an open line; the flood opens at II-2, so
  Stage I is for **economy and positioning**, not spiking. Creep: a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take his card** (+6 gold + early 4-cost access): it funds the war-chest the
  flood needs *and* gives me early 4-cost bodies to bridge to the 5-cost spike. Strong and on-kit; keep
  it. (Deviate only if a synergy/capacity card clearly wins more for a committed line.)
- **I-3 / I-4:** bank toward the interest band; build a board that holds while I save.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero opening my 5-cost-friendly line.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): the flood opens at II-2
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit a synergy a 5-cost can anchor.
- **II-2 (flood opens):** **Midas Touch** the shop full of 5-costs + a free buy. Take the best for my
  line, buy 1-2 more I can afford. I am now fielding 5-cost power the lobby cannot.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a card matching the 5-cost's synergy.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for the 5-cost carry's **best item** or a Synergy Magic Crystal; the
  card's gold helps.
- **Leveling:** I do not need to over-level for high-cost odds (the flood handles it), so level for
  **capacity** to field the 5-costs and a full board.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): press the high-cost lead
- **Flood again when rich** to add a second 5-cost or chase a 2-star of my carry (the star-up flood shows
  5). Spike: star up what I can, take a payoff card.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, more carry gear.
- HP is expensive now (base 7-11), so the 5-cost board must be winning rounds; press the lead.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- The lobby is reaching its own 5-costs at L9-10, so my early-access edge narrows, **close before it
  fully closes**. Dump gold into the last star-ups and items.

## Leveling cadence

Unusual: he does **not** need to rush levels for high-cost shop odds (the flood bypasses them), so he
levels for **capacity** (to field the 5-costs he conjures) and banks the rest for the flood. The upgrade
ladder, cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Level to
the capacity his board needs, and keep the war-chest, do not over-level and starve the gold the flood
requires.

## Best comps (concrete)

Built around an **early-fielded 5-cost carry**:
- **Mage core** ([[../mage_core]]): flood for **Odette** (5c Mage/Astro) early, anchor a Mage [4]/[6]
  with Kagura/Vexana/Lylia/Cecilion, Stargazer mana. The on-theme line (he is "Mage Genius").
- **Marksman carry** ([[../marksman_carry]]): flood for **Lesley** (5c) early, Marksman [4]/[6].
- **Flex best-5-cost:** take whichever 5-cost the flood + my synergy favor (Ling for assassin/Neobeasts,
  Ruby/Bane for a front, Claude for econ). His edge is *choice*, so pick the strongest fit.

## Positioning

Standard for the 5-cost carry (back-center for a ranged/caster carry, never cornered vs Swiftblade
divers; front for Ruby/Bane), wall front, spread vs AoE. The early 5-cost is a fat target, so protect it
like the carry it is. Full detail: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## A worked line (one ideal game)

I-1 L3: normal board, bank. I-2: take his card (+6 gold, early 4-cost access), grab a 4-cost mage to
bridge. Stage I-II-1: I hold the interest band, building a war-chest. II-2: I **flood** the shop full of

cannot touch. Auction: I win Odette's Glowing Wand. III: I flood again when rich, chase a 2-star of my
carry, press a win streak on the strongest mid-game board. IV: the lobby finally reaches its own 5-costs,
but I already converted my head start into an HP lead. 1st.

## Power-card deep dive

His card (**+6 gold**, auto-refresh showing **4-Gold x2**) is **strongly on-kit**: the gold funds the
war-chest the flood needs, and the early 4-cost access bridges Stage II before the 5-cost spike. Keep it.
Deviate only if a synergy-breakpoint or capacity card clearly wins more for a committed line. Use the 2
refreshes to weigh an econ card (Trust Fund) if I want to fund a bigger flood instead.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Other high-cost rushers** (Lancelot out-levels, a second Harley): they **contest the 5-cost pool** (9
  of 9 copies), so a shared 5-cost starves, race them or pick a different 5-cost.
- **Econ snowballs** (Aamon, Cyclops, Neobeasts) out-resource me late; I must convert my early 5-cost
  lead before their economies fund their own 5-costs.
- **Value engines** (Aurora, Diggie) are slower, my early spike beats them if I press.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **Gold-poor at II-2:** the flood is wasted without gold to buy, so **prioritize banking** in Stage I-II-1
  even at the cost of a weaker early board; the flood is the payoff.
- **My 5-cost is contested:** flood and pick a **different** strong 5-cost (the flood gives choice), do not
  fight the pool for a contested one.
- **Behind early:** I am normal in Stage I (the active is offline), so play a steady board and survive to
  II-2; the kit is mid-game.
- **Stage IV, edge narrowing:** the lobby has its own 5-costs now, so **close fast** while my board is
  still ahead; do not coast.
- **Flood whiffed my synergy (no fitting 5-cost shown):** take **Odette** (the most universally strong)
  or bank and re-flood next round, do not force an off-axis 5-cost just because it is a legendary.
- **Two rivals also on 5-costs:** the pool is 9 of 9 per 5-cost, so three players cannot all get the same
  one, **diversify** to a 5-cost they are not taking (my flood gives me the choice they lack).
- **Rich but board already strong:** flood to chase a **2-star** of my carry (repeat floods show 5
  copies) rather than adding a 4th scattered 5-cost; depth beats breadth once the board is set.

## The edge (the secret)

**On-demand 5-cost access, a full stage before the lobby.** The flood lets Harley field and *choose* the
strongest 5-cost legendary while normal odds are ~1-5%, and the star-up flood (5 shown) even makes a
2-star 5-cost reachable. He spikes high-cost power earliest, paid for by a banked economy, and turns that
window into an HP lead.

Contrast him with **Lancelot**, the roster's other high-cost-access commander, because the difference
sharpens how to pilot each. Lancelot reaches 4-5 costs by **out-leveling** (better odds sooner) but pays
in no-interest and no bank, so he is forced into the tempo he generates. Harley reaches them by
**flooding the shop** while keeping a normal economy, so he gets the **one thing Lancelot does not:
choice** (the flood shows several 5-costs, he picks the best fit and can chase a 2-star), at the cost of
needing gold *banked* to cash it. So Lancelot is "fastest, take what comes"; Harley is "a beat slower,
but pick exactly the 5-cost my comp wants." When both are in a lobby they contest the same 5-cost pool,
and the one with the better *fit* (Harley) usually out-values the one with the better *timing* (Lancelot)
if Harley banked enough to buy.

## Pitfalls

- **Flooding broke** (seeing 5-costs I cannot afford); bank first.
- **Buying every 5-cost** (dilutes the board); pick the 1-2 that fit my comp.
- **Over-leveling for odds** (the flood bypasses them); level for capacity, bank the rest.
- **Coasting into Stage IV** while the lobby catches up to its own 5-costs.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Other high-cost rushers** contest his 5-cost pool (Lancelot's level lead, a second Harley). **Econ
snowballs** out-resource him late, eroding his early-access window once the lobby funds its own 5-costs.
He is strongest into **slow lobbies** he can spike a 5-cost ahead of; weakest into a long, converged game
where everyone has 5-costs and his only edge (early access) is gone. A mid-game tempo commander who must
win on his head start.

## Numbers that matter (the flood, quantified)

- **The access gap:** normal 5-cost shop odds are **1% at L7, 5% at L8, 14% at L9, 25% at L10**
  ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/hero]]); Harley's flood shows **3 (then 5 at star-up)** 5-costs **on
  demand from Round II-2**, so he sees 5-costs at ~L6-7 timing when the lobby sees almost none.
- **The cost to cash it:** 5-costs are **5 gold each**, and the active gives **1 free buy**, so a flood
  realistically nets 1 free + 1-2 bought = needs ~10-15 gold banked to fully exploit. Hence the
  bank-first discipline.
- **The 2-star reach:** the star-up flood (5 shown) makes a **2-star 5-cost** (needs 9 of 9 copies)
  reachable by repeat flooding when rich, a spike nobody else can hit.

## Stage checkpoints (am I on pace?)

- **End of Stage I (~L5):** normal board, his card taken, **war-chest building** (the flood is offline
  until II-2). *Behind = overspending? Bank harder; the payoff is the flood.*
- **II-2 (flood opens):** I flood, take the free 5-cost + buy 1-2, suddenly high-cost. *Behind = broke?
  I banked too little; tighten and flood next round.*
- **Stage III (~L9):** 5-cost board pressing wins, maybe a 2-star 5-cost via repeat floods. *Behind =
  lobby catching up? Press the HP lead now.*
- **Stage IV (L10):** finalized, closing before the edge fully narrows.

## The 7-archetype read (who I face, and the adjustment)

- vs **high-cost rushers** (Lancelot, another Harley): contest the 5-cost pool, race or pick a different
  5-cost. My direct rivals for the same prize.
- vs **econ snowballs** (Aamon, Cyclops, Neobeasts): they out-scale late, convert my early 5-cost lead to
  HP before they fund their own.
- vs **value engines** (Aurora, Diggie, Kalea): slower, my mid-game spike beats them; press.
- vs **vertical** (Alice, Dyrroth): they manufacture power too; mine is *early* 5-costs, close before they
  finish their build.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong, Karina): I want gear for my 5-cost carry; contest only its
  best-in-slot.
- vs **synergy grant / free-hero** (Gusion, Lunox, Guinevere, Vale): they go wide/synergy; I go tall on a
  5-cost, different lanes unless we share the carry.
- vs **combat** (Kagura, Ling, Wanwan): their board is the threat; my 5-cost out-statures most, position
  and protect it.

## What to flood for (the 5-cost picks, ranked by what is open)

His edge is *choice*, so know which 5-cost the flood should hand me by the line I am building (rosters:
[[../../infos/heroes/cost_5]]). In pencil:
- **Odette** (Mage/Astro) - the **best generic flood target**: a top-damage mage capstone (the replay's
  carry) that anchors a Mage core *and* an Astro line. Default grab if mages are open.
- **Lesley** (Marksman/Mystic Meow) - the marksman capstone (45% crit, scales hard); grab for a
  Marksman/ATK-speed line.
- **Ling** (Swiftblade/Neobeasts) - an assassin carry + Neobeasts capstone; grab for a dive/Neobeasts
  line (mind the positioning, he is a diver himself).
- **Ruby** (Dauntless/Exorcist) - a tanky front-carry that anchors a wall and seeds Exorcist; grab when I
  want a sturdy front, not a glass carry.
- **Bane** (Defender/Northern Vale) - a front CC/control anchor; grab vs a carry-heavy lobby to lock them.
- **Claude** (Scavenger/Heartbond) - an econ body that keeps Scavenger online late; grab on an
  economy-flex board.
- **Alpha / Badang** (Weapon Master / Bruiser fronts) - sturdy bruiser-front carries; grab for a
  melee/Bruiser line.
The rule: **flood for the 5-cost that anchors my synergy**, not the flashiest legendary. Two that fit my
axis beat four scattered ones. If none fit, take Odette (the most universally strong) and bank the rest.
