## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** synergy grant / flex. He grants a chosen hero a **Role (class) synergy** and banks gold
  each time, buying class breakpoints directly.
- **Plays like:** a breakpoint-buyer, point the Sigil at the class I need, grant it to a keeper hero, and
  cross a [4]/[6] the roster would not otherwise let me reach, plus +7 gold per grant.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is a flexible grant with refreshes; the skill is granting the *right*
  class to a *keeper* hero at the right time.
- **Best into:** comps one class-count short of a breakpoint, and go-wide boards. **Worst into:** fast
  aggro before the grants pay, and lines where the granted class adds little.

  Drag a Hero onto the Sigil to grant them the Synergy. The Sigil then expires, granting 7 Gold. You have
  3 chance(s) to refresh the Role Synergy. When other players inherit Heroes from Gusion, Synergies
  granted by the Light Sigil will not be inherited."*
- **Sigil Resonance** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"After Stage II, the Sigil can be used one
  additional time."*
- **Power Card:** *Gusion's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Gain 1 Role Magic Crystal Chest
  and 3 Gold."*

Reading (in pencil): the Light Sigil lets me **grant a chosen hero a Role (class) Synergy** (a bought +1
to a class count), with **3 refreshes** to pick which class it shows, and the Sigil **pays +7 gold** when
used. The star-up adds **+1 use after Stage II** (so ~2 grants total). The card hands a **Role Magic
Crystal Chest + 3 gold** (another class-count grant). So he **buys class breakpoints and gets paid for
it**, a flexible synergy-grantor focused on the **class** (role) axis specifically. The grant is *not
inherited* if others copy my heroes, a minor anti-steal note.

Rethink (2026-07-10, first principles): synergy counts are the game's **denial-immune currency**, no
bench-holding or pool contention can stop a granted +1, and a bought [4]->[6] is a deterministic
fight-flip (the breakpoint changes what the whole class DOES, not just its stats). With 3 refreshes
the Sigil is effectively **chooseable**, not random, so he converts one flexible body into two class
memberships on demand, plus 7 gold for doing it. In the trick era this ranks HIGHER than before:
his payout is precisely the un-printable kind (counts + a Role crystal from the card), while the
gold half of his kit was never the point. Ceiling note: Role synergies cap at [6], so he buys
breakpoints, never the faction-[10] Ultra tier, the structural line between him and the crystal
printers (Aurora, Yu Zhong).

## Win condition / identity

**Cross class breakpoints the roster would not cheaply allow, and bank gold doing it.** By granting a
hero a second class synergy, Gusion reaches a class [4]/[6] with fewer "real" members (a key body counts
for two classes), and the +7 gold per grant funds the rest. He is a **flexible synergy-buyer**: not tied
to one comp, he grants whatever class his board is one short of, so he goes wide or fills a stubborn
class count on demand. The refreshes let him pick the *useful* class, not a random one.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Which class to grant, and to whom.** Grants are limited (~2), so each should **cross a real
   breakpoint** (a class I am exactly one short of) on a **keeper hero** (one I will field all game), not
   a filler. Use the 3 refreshes to land the class I actually need.
2. **Go-wide vs deepen.** The grant can either **add a new active class** (going wider, pairing with
   Mystic Meow / Solid Alliance) or **deepen a class toward [6]**. Read which buys more: a new [2] for a
   go-wide board, or the [6] breakpoint for a tall one.

## The grant plan (his signature, concrete)

The ordered way to use the Sigil:
1. **Identify the class breakpoint I am one short of** (e.g. Mage at 3, wanting [4]; or a new class for a
   go-wide board).
2. **Refresh the Sigil** (3 chances) until it shows that class.
3. **Grant it to a keeper hero** I will field all game (so the count sticks), crossing the breakpoint, and
   **bank the +7 gold**.
4. **Use the 2nd grant after Stage II** (Sigil Resonance) on the next needed class, or to push a [6].
5. **Stack the card's Role Magic Crystal Chest** (another class count) onto the same plan, two to three
   bought class counts is a lot of free breakpoint.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): build toward a class line
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Build a normal early board toward a class line a grant can complete. Creep:
  a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take his card** (a Role Magic Crystal Chest + 3 gold): a free class-count grant
  + econ, on-kit. Keep it.
- **I-3 / I-4:** bank toward the band; line up a keeper hero to receive the first grant.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a member of my class line.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): grant the first breakpoint
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count (stacks with the grant plan).
- **Grant the first class** (refresh to the one I need) onto a keeper hero, crossing a [4]; bank the +7
  gold. Commit the comp.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a synergy payoff or another class crystal.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for a Synergy Magic Crystal or the carry's item; the grant gold helps.
- **After Stage II:** Sigil Resonance unlocks the 2nd grant. Level toward the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): grant the [6] / a second class, spike
- **Second grant:** push a class [6] (and take Purest Power) or add a second active class for a wide board
  (Solid Alliance). Spike the board.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, more carry gear.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the breakpoint-rich board the grants built, dump the banked gold into star-ups and items. Close
  before HP is the binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

The +7 gold per grant (+3 from the card) keeps him **gold-healthy**, so he levels and rolls on a normal
interest-band cadence to the carry's tier and for capacity (more slots = more grantable breakpoints). The
upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`.

## Best comps (concrete)

**Flexible**, built around a class line a grant completes:
- **A class-tall line** (Mage [6], Marksman [6]) where a grant crosses the last count cheaply.
- **A go-wide board** (Mystic Meow / Solid Alliance) where each grant adds another active class for the
  per-synergy payoffs.
- The constant: I do not force a comp, I **grant the class my board is one short of**, so commit to
  whatever the pool opens and let the Sigil finish the breakpoints.

The tier list as it lives *here*, in Gusion's form of life (the general [[../tier_lists]] is only the

- **Heroes by phase:** **keeper heroes worth granting a class to are S** (a carry or a body I field all

## Power-card deep dive

His card (a **Role Magic Crystal Chest + 3 gold**) is **on-kit**, another class-count grant plus econ,
stacking with the Sigil's grants for a breakpoint-rich board. Keep it. Deviate only if a synergy payoff
(Purest Power) or a big econ card clearly wins more once my breakpoints are set. Use the 2 refreshes to
confirm.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fast aggro** ends the game before the grants build the board, protect HP and grant the first
  breakpoint promptly.
- **Lunox / Vale** (other synergy-committers): we may chase the same class; the grant lets me reach it
  with fewer real members, but a contested pool still tightens, prefer an open line.
- **Cyclops** wants someone to die early; do not be his first kill while the grants spin up.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **Sigil not showing the class I need:** use the 3 refreshes; if still off, grant the **best available
  class** that helps my board rather than wasting the use.
- **Comp shifts:** the grants are flexible, so grant the new class my board needs; I am not locked to one
  line.
- **Behind early:** the +7 gold per grant stabilizes; grant a breakpoint and bank.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** use the last grant to cross the breakpoint that flips a fight and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**He buys class breakpoints on demand and gets paid for it.** A grant crosses a class [4]/[6] with fewer
real members (a key body counts twice), the 3 refreshes ensure it is the *useful* class, and +7 gold per
use funds the rest. A flexible synergy-buyer who reaches breakpoints the roster would not cheaply allow,
shaping his comp around whatever class the board is one short of.

## Pitfalls

- **Granting a random class** instead of the one that crosses a real breakpoint (use the refreshes).
- **Granting to a filler** I will bench (the count leaves with the hero); grant to a keeper.
- **Forgetting the 2nd grant** unlocks after Stage II.
- **Forcing a fixed comp** instead of granting the class the open line needs.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the grants build the breakpoint-rich board. **A contested class line**
tightens the pool even though the grant helps. He is strongest in **flexible, breakpoint-hungry comps**
(go-wide, or a class one short of [6]) and weakest in fast finishes. A flexible synergy-buyer whose power
is mid-game breakpoint completion.

## Numbers that matter (the grants, quantified)

- **The grants:** ~**2 uses** (1 base + 1 after Stage II) of the Sigil + the card's **Role Magic Crystal
  Chest** = roughly **3 class-count grants** over a game, each crossing a breakpoint a body short.
- **The gold:** **+7 gold per Sigil use** (+3 from the card), so granting is also an econ engine, not just
  a breakpoint buy.
- **The refreshes:** **3 chances** to set the Sigil's displayed class, so I almost always land the class I
  actually need, the grant is reliable, not random.

## Stage checkpoints (am I on pace?)

- **End of Stage I (~L5):** card's class crystal granted, building toward a class line, banking. *Behind =
  no keeper to grant? Build one before granting.*
- **End of Stage II (~L7-8):** first Sigil grant crossed a [4], +7 gold banked, comp committed. *Behind =
  wrong class granted? Use the refreshes next time.*
- **Stage III (~L9):** 2nd grant pushed a [6] (or a 2nd class), Purest Power / Solid Alliance taken.
  *Behind = pool contested? Pivot to an open class.*
- **Stage IV (L10):** breakpoint-rich board locked, closing.

## The 7-archetype read (who I face, and the adjustment)

- vs **synergy-committers** (Lunox, Vale): we may share a class; the grant reaches it cheaply, but a
  contested pool still tightens, prefer an open line.
- vs **fast aggro / combat** (Lancelot, Ling, Lukas): they end it before the grants build, grant the first
  breakpoint promptly and protect HP.
- vs **vertical / copy** (Alice, Aamon, Paquito): they go tall on a hero, I go wide/deep on classes,
  little contention.
- vs **econ / value** (Aurora, Diggie, Chou): a value race; the +7-per-grant gold keeps me competitive,
  out-spike with the breakpoints.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong): they want gear, I want class members, little overlap.
- vs **free-hero / shop** (Guinevere, Harley): they generate bodies; I generate counts, we rarely contest
  the same heroes.
- vs **combat** (Kagura, Wanwan): their board is the threat; tech position, complete my breakpoints and
  out-power.
