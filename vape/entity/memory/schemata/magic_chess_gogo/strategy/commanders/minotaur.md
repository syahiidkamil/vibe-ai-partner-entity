## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** equipment economy / single over-geared carry.
- **Plays like:** the patient gear-hoarder who fields the best-equipped carry in the lobby, and abuses
  the lowest-HP-first reward order to pick equipment first.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is forgiving (gear is low-variance), but the lose-early line and the
  enhance-target choices reward judgment.
- **Best into:** gear-light lobbies, long games. **Worst into:** other equipment commanders, fast
  aggro, and boards that win without needing to out-gear me.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Minotaur, "Son of Minos." Class synergy shown: **Onslaught**. Skins: Son of Minos,
  Shadow Guardian. (Skill values below are the 3-star display, the level in the source screenshot.)
- **Molten Decree** [Active] (verbatim): *"Select an Equipment to enhance. Can be used 3 time(s).
  (Special Equipment and Enhanced Equipment cannot be enhanced.)"* -> enhance turns a Basic item into
  its Enhanced form (raises a passive's magnitude and/or adds a second passive).
- **Bloodline Resonance** [Passive] (verbatim): *"Obtain 1 Equipment Chest(s). Gain 2 Gold for every
  Equipment enhanced by the skill."* -> start the game with 1 Equipment Chest; +2 Gold per enhance.
- **Power Card:** *Minotaur's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - enhance an Equipment **2
  times**, and obtain **2 random Equipment**.

**Equipment the kit grants / affects:** he starts with **1 Equipment Chest**, his Power Card hands **2
random Equipment**, and his core power is **enhancing**, any Basic item becomes its **Enhanced** form
(raising a passive's magnitude and/or adding a second Unique Passive; per-item deltas in
[[../../infos/equipments/regular]]). Unlike Ruby or Karina he grants **no single fixed item**, his edge
is making *whatever* gear I funnel onto the carry stronger (he cannot enhance Special or already-Enhanced
items).

Reading (the wording is terse, held in pencil): he has **more gear and better gear** than the lobby. He
starts with a chest, turns Basics into Enhanced (the extra passive is a real power jump), and is paid
+2 gold for each enhance, so his enhancing *funds itself*. The scarce resource is **enhance charges**
(3 from the kit + 2 from the card = 5), so each enhance is a real decision.

Rethink (2026-07-10, first principles): equipment is the trick era's soundest currency, no shop
lock or gold farm prints an Enhanced passive, so his engine holds full value while pure-gold kits
deflated around him, and his kit is shop-independent, so he hosts the trick on top for free. The
structural read of the 5 charges: each enhance is a fixed-size, never-bricking power increment
whose value COMPOUNDS with concentration (Enhanced passives on one carry multiply with each other
and with the Astro Sovereign most-equipped bonus), so the charges want one hero, not fairness. His
cap is the other side of the same coin: the increments are bounded and he still must FIND the
items to enhance (a chest, the card's two, the creep drops), so he out-values steadily but never
spikes, the definition of a high floor with a modest ceiling.

## Win condition / identity

**Field the best-geared carry in the lobby, earlier, and let the gear gap win fights.** Everything
funnels to one carry (plus the front): all the enhanced damage items, ideally on an **Astro Power
Sovereign** (the most-equipped hero, which my over-geared carry becomes for free). The gear is the
plan; the comp is just the vehicle that carries it. He is a **placement-first, low-variance** commander:
gear has no recipe tree, so nothing bricks, and his floor is high.

## The core tension (every round)

Two tensions run the whole game:
1. **Which 5 items to enhance.** Enhance the **highest-value pieces on the one carry** first (its
   best-in-slot damage), then the front's key survival piece. Never spread enhances across many heroes,
   one fully-enhanced carry beats five half-enhanced bodies.
2. **How much HP to spend on reward priority.** Equipment is his economy, and the reward stages give
   equipment **first to the lowest-HP commander**, so **deliberately losing cheap early HP** to pick
   first is his signature line. The trade: lose *cheap* HP (Stage I-II, base DMG 3-6), never *expensive*
   HP (Stage IV), and never so low a leader's spiked board eliminates me (the Mythic floor:
   [[../../experience/ranked/general_mythic]]).

## Enhance priority (the 5 charges, in order)

The 5 enhance charges (3 from Molten Decree + 2 from the Power Card) are the scarcest thing I have.
Spend them in this order, all toward the one carry first:
1. **The carry's main damage item** (I-2 / II-1): the single piece that most multiplies output, a mage
   wand for an Odette Sovereign (Holy Crystal -> Enhanced for +30% Skill DMG), or Golden Staff for a
   physical carry.
2. **The carry's second damage item** (II): build toward **3 items on the carry**, which unlocks the
   3-equipment payoffs (Fortified Defense, Fully Loaded) and feeds the Astro Sovereign.
3. **The carry's third item or its mana piece** (II-III): complete the kit, Spellblade Talisman
   Enhanced (80% mana + skill DMG) on a caster so it casts faster.
4. **The front's key survival piece** (III): Guardian Helmet or Antique Cuirass Enhanced to keep the
   wall standing so the carry lives.
5. **Flex** (III-IV): whatever the spike needs, a second carry piece or a second front item.
Never enhance a Basic I will not field, and the kit cannot enhance Special or already-Enhanced items.

## The HP budget for the lose-early line (concrete)

The lose-early line trades HP for reward priority, and early HP is cheap. Losing **I-3 and I-4** (base
DMG 3 each + the winner's surviving star levels, ~4-6 HP each) plus a couple of light **Stage-II**
losses (base 5-6) is roughly **20-35 HP out of ~100** for **first pick** at the end-of-Stage-I (and
again end-of-III) reward selections. That is cheap for first crack at the gear that is my whole economy,
and it builds a **loss streak** (gold 1;1;2;3) on top. The hard rule: **stop the moment a loss would
cost expensive HP or drop me where the lobby's strongest board could close me out** (watch the leader's
Commander DMG); never lose-streak past Stage II, and never below the HP a Stage-IV hit (13-15 + stars)
could finish in one round.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): hoard gear, stay open, consider losing on purpose
- **I-1 (Creep round, no Commander DMG yet):** I start L3, 2 gold, with 1 Equipment Chest. The creep
  drops **3 of the 5 basics** (Inspire / Revitalize / Purify / Aegis / Retribution); take the one that
  fits my eventual carry, **Retribution or Inspire** for damage, **Aegis** if I expect to need HP. Since
  I-1 deals no Commander DMG, its result is free, so **do not overspend to win it**, just take the gear
  and the gold. Open the chest; begin the enhance loop on the best basic.
- **I-2 (Power Card selection):** **take Minotaur's Power** (enhance 2x + 2 random equipment): pure
  on-kit gear value, and the +2 gold per enhance starts paying. The only deviation: a generic **item
  bundle** (The Hunt: Golden Staff + Demon Hunter Sword + Sea Halberd + 6g; or Box of Holy Objects:
  Spellblade Talisman + Glowing Wand + Ice Queen Wand + 6g) if those specific pieces beat two random
  ones for my carry. Use the **2 refreshes** to compare. By now ~L4.
- **I-3, I-4 (combat, base DMG 3):** the **lose-early window**. Losing here costs only ~3 HP + the
  winner's surviving star levels, cheap. If I am committing to reward priority, I **field a deliberately
  light board and take the losses** (which also builds a loss streak: gold 1;1;2;3). Meanwhile I keep
  enhancing and banking. If the lobby is gentle I can instead win and streak, judge by the boards I see.
  Reach ~L5 on the free 2 EXP/round plus a little gold.
- **End of Stage I (Reward Selection, lowest-HP-first, 8 equipment + 3 heroes):** the payoff. If I
  lose-streaked I am **lowest HP and pick first**, grab the **best equipment** (a strong damage piece
  for the carry, or an Enhanced one) before anyone, or a hero that opens my comp. This single pick is
  why the lose-early line is correct *for Minotaur specifically*, equipment is his whole economy.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): kit the carry, commit, win the auction
- **II-1 (Blessing Shifter + Blessing system opens):** combine a 2-star to take a free **+1 synergy
  count**; aim it at the synergy I am committing (Astro Power if going the Sovereign line). Keep
  enhancing toward **3 items on the carry** (the 3-equipment payoffs and the Astro Sovereign both reward
  a fully-geared hero).
- **II-2, II-4, II-5 (combat, base DMG 5-6):** HP is getting less cheap, so **stop deliberately losing
  around here** and stabilize a real board, the gear lead should now let me win. Commit the comp; cross
  the first [4] breakpoint (Astro Power [4], or a Defender/Dauntless front [2]).
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + Creep):** take a card that pays the gear plan (an item bundle, Fortified Defense,
  or a synergy payoff) and the creep's 1-of-3 equipment.
- **End of Stage II (Auction, bid Gold):** **this is my stage.** Bid hard on **equipment lots and
  chests** (Enhanced items, an Equipment Chest, the gear my carry wants), they are my economy and
  worth more to me than to anyone. Use the +2-per-enhance gold cushion. Bid up to the lot's
  value-in-rounds; do not blow the interest band on a luxury I will not field. Level toward L7-8.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): spike the over-geared carry
- **III-1, III-2 (combat, base DMG 7):** my carry should now be enhanced and fielded; press round wins.
  If I am low HP from the early losses and the reward order still favors me, a *measured* loss before a
  reward stage can still be worth it, but only cheap HP.
- **III-3 (Go Go Card + Creep + Blessing Shifter):** take the gear/synergy card, the creep equipment,
  and re-point the Blessing onto a live breakpoint (the Shifter's value is flexibility).
- **III-4 to III-6 (combat, base DMG 11):** spike, star up the carry and front, deal real Commander
  DMG. The gear gap is widest now, others have not caught up on items, so this is the window to build
  an HP lead.
- **End of Stage III (Reward Selection, lowest-HP-first):** pick equipment/heroes; again the gear
  pieces are my priority.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- **IV-1 on (base DMG 13-15, Battle Acceleration forces results):** the board must be **finalized**. My
  over-geared carry should outscale everything; **dump the bank** (the enhance gold + interest) into the
  last star-ups and the final items. **IV-3** is a creep (1-of-3 equipment), take the carry's
  best-in-slot. Close fast while the gear advantage is largest, others catch up on items late, so my
  edge shrinks if the game drags.

## Leveling cadence (concrete)

Gold is cushioned by **+2 per enhance**, so I can level *and* roll more freely than a pure-econ
commander. Upgrade-cost ladder from L3 (gold-equiv EXP, minus 2 free/round): L4: 3, L5: 11, L6: 31,
L7: 63, L8: 103, L9: 157, L10: 231.
- **Stage I:** ride the free EXP to ~L5; do not rush levels while lose-streaking (bank the gold).
- **Stage II:** push **L7-8** to field the carry's tier (L8 = 18% 4-cost odds) and the capacity for a
  [4] + front.
- **Stage III:** **L8-9** to fish the carry and seat a 5-cost capstone (Odette at L9-10). Hold at the
  carry's tier and roll the enhance-gold rather than over-leveling.
- **Stage IV:** L10 for the last slot; then spend everything on board.

## Best comps (concrete)

- **Astro Power + Odette Sovereign (the signature line).** Astro members: Minotaur(hero), Hilda,
  Aurora, Odette, Helcurt, Martis. Funnel every enhanced item onto **Odette** (5c Mage/Astro); she
  becomes the **Sovereign** (most-equipped) automatically, gaining the extra DMG Increase + lifesteal,
  and the **Astro Judgment** card turbo-charges her. Front with Hilda/Minotaur-hero. This is the comp
  the kit is built for.
- **Any single-carry line with a gear sink.** A mage core ([[../mage_core]]) or marksman
  ([[../marksman_carry]]) where one carry soaks all the items works fine; Astro is just the best fit
  because the Sovereign rewards exactly what I do.
- **Kishin splash** (Franco, Karrie, Lancelot, Angela, Lunox, Dyrroth) for the free Kishin equipment, a
  second gear economy stacked on mine.

## Positioning (the geared carry)

My over-geared carry (the Astro Sovereign) **is** the board, so I protect it the way Alice protects hers:
all the value is on one unit, so a successful dive is catastrophic. Concretely:
- Seat the carry **back-center, never a back corner** (most Swiftblade heroes, Saber/Helcurt/Gusion/
  Ling, jump to the back-row side to assassinate it).
- **Corner-guard** the back row with a tank or a Heartbond body so the dive lands on the wall, not the
  carry; position a peeler to punish the landing.
- **Spread** the rest vs AoE and the Dragoncaller dragon (which hits the densest 9 tiles); do not cluster
  unless a row/end card pays for it.
- The Sovereign's gear does not change its seat, but the concentration of value means **peel hard** for
  it. Full positioning, and the dive counters: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## A worked line (one ideal game)

A clean Minotaur game reads: **I-1** take Retribution off the creep, open the starting chest, field a
light board, do not sweat the result (no Commander DMG yet). **I-2** take Minotaur's Power, enhance
Retribution onto my early carry, pocket the 2 random items. **I-3 / I-4** field light and lose cheaply,
banking a loss streak. **End of Stage I** I am lowest HP, so I **pick the best damage item first**.
**Stage II:** commit Astro Power, enhance toward 3 items on the Odette-to-be, **win the auction's gear
lots**, level to L7-8, then stabilize and stop losing. **Stage III:** seat Odette as the Sovereign,
enhance her core, spike a [4]/[6] Astro, and build an HP lead while my gear out-classes the lobby.
**Stage IV:** finalize, dump the bank, close before others catch up on items. The result is the lobby's
best-geared carry, paid for by enhancing and by the reward priority I bought with cheap early HP.

## Power-card deep dive

The **I-2 offer always includes Minotaur's Power**, plus borrowable power cards and generics, with 2
refreshes (one card at a time). His exclusive (enhance 2x + 2 random gear) is **on-kit and almost
always kept**, because it both gives gear *and* triggers his +2-per-enhance gold. The only competitor:
a **generic item bundle** (The Hunt / Box of Holy Objects) when those specific named pieces beat two
random ones for my carry, refresh to compare. Later card stages: take **Novel Equipment / Copycat** to
multiply the gear I already like, and **Astro Judgment** if I am on the Sovereign line. Avoid econ-only
cards that do not feed gear; my economy is already the gear.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Other equipment commanders (a rival Minotaur, Zilong, Ruby):** they split the gear pool and outbid
  me at the auction. If two of us hoard gear, the pieces run thin, contest the auction lots that
  complete *my* carry and concede the rest.
- **Aamon / Alice (copy / star-up verticals):** they out-tempo my slower gear advantage with a spiked
  carry; I cannot deny Alice's carry (no copies) so I must out-gear and out-position. (Enemy reading:
  [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].)
- **Aggressive early commanders (Lancelot, combat kits):** they punish my lose-early posture if I drop
  too low; keep the early losses *cheap* and stop before a leader can close me out.
- **Vexana:** she outbids me with +13 auction gold; bid earlier on the one gear lot I must have.
- Use the **lowest-HP-first reward order** as my weapon, but never let "lowest HP" become "dead."

## Contingency and pivots

- **Lose-early went too far (HP critically low at Mythic):** stop losing immediately, stabilize a real
  board with the gear I banked; the gear edge is worthless if I am eliminated. The intentional loss is a
  *cheap-HP-only* tool, abort it the moment HP gets expensive.
- **Gear spread too thin across heroes:** consolidate, re-enhance and re-slot onto the **one carry**; a
  half-geared board beats nothing.
- **Astro line contested (another Astro player):** pivot the gear onto a Mage or Marksman single carry
  instead, the kit does not need Astro, it just prefers it.
- **Behind on board mid-game:** the enhance-gold funds a roll-down; lean on the gear advantage to spike
  the carry now rather than banking.
- **Highroll a carry early:** commit and press; the gear makes an early carry untouchable.

## The edge (the secret)

Two edges only Minotaur has: (1) the **best-geared carry in the lobby, earlier**, because he enhances
Basics into Enhanced and the enhancing pays for itself (+2 gold each); and (2) the deliberate
**lose-early-to-pick-equipment-first** exploit of the lowest-HP reward order, which he abuses harder
than any commander because equipment *is* his economy. The **Astro Sovereign** synergy lands the
Sovereign buffs on his over-geared carry for free, so the comp and the kit point at the same hero.

## Pitfalls

- **Spreading enhances** across many heroes instead of kitting one carry (the single biggest mistake).
- **Over-losing HP** on the lose-early line, it has a hard floor; never spend expensive HP, never feed a
  leader.
- **Enhancing low-value items** (a niche stat stick) instead of the carry's best-in-slot.
- **Dragging the game** to Stage IV even, the gear edge shrinks as others catch up on items, so press
  the lead while it is largest.
- **Bidding the auction broke** on gear I will not actually field.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

- **Other equipment commanders** halve the gear pool and outbid him, his economy is contested directly.
- **Vertical / hyper-carry commanders (Alice, Aamon)** out-tempo the slower gear curve with a spiked
  carry, especially Alice, whose carry I cannot deny.
- **Fast aggro** ends the game before the gear advantage compounds, and punishes the lose-early posture.
- **Go-wide / multi-carry boards** win without ever needing to out-gear my single carry, sidestepping my
  whole plan.
He is **strongest** in gear-light, longer lobbies where his item lead compounds and the reward order
keeps feeding him; **weakest** when rushed, out-contested for gear, or facing a spike he cannot
out-gear. His skill ceiling is in the enhance-target choices and knowing exactly how much cheap HP the
reward priority is worth.

## Quick reference (TL;DR)

- **Plan:** gear is the win condition; **one carry soaks every enhanced item** (ideally the Astro
  Sovereign, Odette).
- **Enhances:** spend the 5 charges on the carry first (main damage -> 2nd -> 3rd/mana), the front last.
- **Lose-early:** spend ~20-35 cheap HP in Stage I-II for **first reward pick**; stop by Stage II, never
  feed a leader.
- **Auction:** win the **gear lots**, that is my economy; bid to value, not broke.
- **I-2 card:** take **Minotaur's Power** unless a better named item bundle (The Hunt / Box of Holy
  Objects) shows; refresh to compare.
- **Level:** L7-8 by Stage II, L9-10 for Odette; hold at the carry's tier and roll the enhance-gold.
- **Position:** carry back-center, corner-guard vs Swiftblade, spread vs AoE.
- **Threats:** other gear commanders, Alice/Aamon spikes, fast aggro. **Edge:** the best-geared carry,
  earlier, plus reward priority bought with cheap HP.
