## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** value / reward engine focused on the Go Go Box and auction. She takes an *extra* reward at
  the value stages and out-bids the auction.
- **Plays like:** a value player who simply gets *more* from every shared-reward stage, an extra pick each
  Go Go Box, a copied first pick, +13 auction gold.
- **Difficulty:** low-medium. Passive, schedule-locked value; the skill is picking the right extra rewards
  and flexing them into a board.
- **Best into:** value mirrors and long games (the extra picks compound). **Worst into:** fast aggro before
  the reward stages pay, and Cyclops (who profits if I die).

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Vexana, "Shimmer of Hope." Skins: Shimmer of Hope, Glimmer of Verdancy. (Note: the
  **commander** Vexana is the player avatar, distinct from the **hero** Vexana, a 4-cost Mage/Enchanted
  Tales unit in [[../../infos/heroes/cost_4]]. This file is the commander.)
- **I Want All of Them!** [Passive] (verbatim): *"For the first 3 Go Go Box round(s), after all Commanders
  have made their selections, select one additional reward from the remaining options. (Gain 13 extra Gold
  during the Go Go Auction round.)"*
- **Unrestricted** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"In the first Go Go Box round, copy the first item
  or Hero you select and gain 13 Gold."*
- **Power Card:** *Vexana's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Select one extra reward during
  the first 4 Go Go Box rounds. Gain 13 Gold in Go Go Auction."*

Reading (in pencil): at the **Go Go Box / reward stages** (end of Stage I, the auction, end of Stage III,
the gogo-card rounds), Vexana takes **one extra reward** (the leftover after others pick) for the first 3
boxes (4 with the card), **copies her first pick** in the first box (star-up), and gets **+13 gold at the
auction** (+13 more from the card = a big bidding edge). She is a **draw-independent value engine** whose
edge is concentrated at the **shared-reward stages**: she simply gets *more* from each than anyone, and
out-bids the auction.

## Win condition / identity

**Out-value the lobby at every shared-reward stage, then convert the surplus into a board.** Each Go Go Box
hands her an extra reward the others do not get, the first box doubles her best pick, and the auction
gives her a ~26-gold bidding edge (kit + card), so she accumulates more items/heroes/gold than the field
and flexes the haul into a spiked board. She is a **placement-first safe climb** like Aurora/Diggie, with
the value front-loaded onto the reward stages rather than every round.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Pick the right extra rewards.** Her extra Go Go Box pick comes from the *leftovers* after others
   choose, so I **prioritize the highest-value remaining** (a Synergy Magic Crystal, my carry's item, a
   needed hero) and the **copy** (first box) goes on my **best** first pick (a premium item or carry).
2. **Leverage the auction edge.** The +26 gold (kit + card) lets me **outbid** for a key lot, so plan the
   auction around winning the one lot that completes my comp, do not fritter the edge on junk.

## The value plan (her signature, concrete)

The ordered way to run the engine:
1. **Take the card** (extra reward for 4 boxes + another +13 auction gold), extending the kit's
   over-picking and doubling the auction edge.
2. **At the first Go Go Box (end of Stage I):** make my **best pick** (a premium item/carry) so the
   star-up **copy** doubles it, then take my **extra** pick from the leftovers (the next-best). +13 gold.
3. **At each subsequent box:** take the normal + extra pick, prioritizing breakpoint/carry pieces.
4. **At the auction (end of Stage II):** use the **+26 gold edge** to outbid for the one lot that
   completes my comp (a Synergy Magic Crystal, a 4-cost, my carry's Enhanced item).
5. **Flex the accumulated haul** into a spiked board.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): build to the first box
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Flexible early board. Creep: a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take her card** (extra reward for 4 boxes + 13 auction gold): extends her whole
  edge, on-kit. Keep it.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection (first Go Go Box):** make my **best pick** (the star-up copies it),
  then take the **extra** pick from the leftovers. +13 gold. A big early value swing.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): commit, out-bid the auction
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit a synergy.
- Flex the box rewards into the comp; cross the first breakpoints.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** use the **+26 gold edge** (kit + card) to **outbid** for the key lot that
  completes my comp. This is her signature stage, win the bidding war.
- **Leveling:** the value + auction wins fund a steady push to the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): more boxes, spike
- **End of Stage III, Reward Selection (another box):** normal + extra pick; finish breakpoints. The
  accumulated value funds a spike, roll to star up, take a payoff card.
- **III-3 / gogo rounds:** more extra picks (the card extends to 4 boxes).

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the value-built board, dump banked gold into the last star-ups and items. Close before HP is the
  binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Steady; her value comes from the reward stages, so level on the free EXP + the haul and push mid-game when
committing. The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3:
`L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Avoid the value-engine trap of banking
so long the board lags.

## Power-card deep dive

Her card (an **extra reward for 4 boxes + 13 auction gold**) is **on-kit**, it extends her over-picking to
a 4th box and *doubles* the auction edge (to ~+26 gold), more guaranteed value at the exact stages she is
built for. Keep it. Deviate only if a capacity or synergy card clearly wins more for a committed line. Use
the 2 refreshes to confirm.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fast aggro** ends the game before the late reward stages pay, protect HP and lean on the early box +
  auction value.
- **Other value engines** (Aurora, Alucard, Diggie, Kalea): a value mirror; I win it at the **shared-reward
  stages** (the extra pick + auction edge are unmatched there).
- **Cyclops** profits if I die; do not feed him on a soft early board.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]] (note: *enemy* Vexana is
  the one who outbids *you*, so I am that threat to others).

## Contingency and pivots

- **Bleeding HP early:** spend to hold a real board; survival outranks pure value.
- **Weak box leftovers:** take the best available (even a fodder hero / gold) rather than nothing; the
  extra pick is free value regardless.
- **Value-ahead, board-behind:** dump the banked value now (anti-greed).
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** convert all rewards + gold into the board and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**She simply gets more from every shared-reward stage.** An extra Go Go Box pick (from the leftovers), a
copied first pick, and a ~26-gold auction edge mean Vexana out-accumulates the field at the exact moments
value is handed out, then flexes the surplus into a board. A high-floor value climber who wins the
reward-selection and auction sub-games outright.

## Pitfalls

- **Wasting the first-box copy** on a mediocre pick (copy your *best* piece).
- **Squandering the auction edge** on junk instead of the one comp-completing lot.
- **Hoarding value** so long the board lags (the value-engine trap).
- **Playing too greedy early** on a soft board (spend some to survive).

## Threat assessment (who beats her, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the late reward stages pay. **Cyclops** profits if she dies. She is
strongest in **long value games** with many reward stages (the extra picks compound) and weakest in fast
ones. A low-variance value engine whose edge is concentrated at the Go Go Box and auction, so a game that
reaches those stages favors her.

- vs **fast aggro / combat** (Lancelot, Ling, Lukas): they end it before late reward stages, lean on early

- vs **combat** (Kagura, Wanwan): their board is the threat; tech position, out-resource to a spike.
