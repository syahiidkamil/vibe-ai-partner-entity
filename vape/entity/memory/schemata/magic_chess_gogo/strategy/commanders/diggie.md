## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** value / reward engine. A time-accumulating chest opens for escalating-rarity rewards,
  faster when I win, draw-independent value that floors placement.
- **Plays like:** a patient value player whose chest ticks up every round; win to accelerate it, harvest
  the (rarity-upgrading) opens, flex into a board.
- **Difficulty:** low-medium. Passive value; the skill is keeping a winning board (to speed the chest) and
  flexing the rewards.
- **Best into:** long games and value mirrors. **Worst into:** fast aggro before the chest pays, and a
  losing board (slower opens), though losses do tank-up the chest.

- **Alarm Chest** [Passive] (verbatim): *"Diggie summons an Alarm Chest on the battlefield. The Chest

- **Time Acceleration** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Round victory grants 10 extra Time Point(s).
  Round loss permanently increases Alarm Chest's Max HP by 300."*
- **Power Cards:** exclusive *Diggie's Power* - *"Gain 15 extra Time Points each round. The Alarm Chest
  opens 1 extra time for rewards worth 25 Gold."* **(Exclusive)**; borrowable *Diggie's Power* - *"Summon
  an Alarm Chest. Gain 15 Time Points/round, +10 on victory. At Point thresholds it grants rewards then
  upgrades rarity."*

Reading (in pencil): the Alarm Chest gains **15 Time Points/round** (**+10 on a win**), opens at **100
points** for **rewards that upgrade in rarity each open** (max 3 opens), so ~every 7 rounds normally,
faster if I win. Time Acceleration speeds opens on wins (and tanks the chest +300 Max HP on losses, it is
a battlefield object that can be attacked). The **exclusive doubles the rate** (+15 extra points/round =
~30/round, opens every ~3-4 rounds) **and adds an extra open** (25-gold reward). He is a **draw-independent
value engine** like Aurora/Alucard, accelerated by winning. The borrowable just re-grants the chest,
redundant on the owner.

Rethink (2026-07-10, first principles): two structural truths sharpen his read. (1) The +10-on-win
acceleration makes him a **win-more engine**: the value arrives fastest exactly when it is least
needed and slowest when a rescue is needed, so his "high floor" is really a high MIDDLE, the floor
belongs to kits that pay while losing (Chou, Nana, Cyclops). Pilot him like a tempo commander (a
board that wins early rounds IS the engine), never like a lose-and-bank engine. (2) The chest is a
**battlefield object with Max HP**, meaning enemies can hit it; whether destroying it costs the
rewards is unverified, CHECK live, because if it does, focused aggro counters his whole kit. Trick
era: reward contents unverified (gold-heavy = partially commoditized); the kit ignores the shop, so
the shop-lock rides on top freely.

## Win condition / identity

**Out-value the lobby passively, faster the more I win.** The chest pays escalating-rarity rewards
regardless of the shop, and winning accelerates it (a virtuous loop: a good board wins -> faster, better
opens -> a better board). He is a **placement-first safe climb** with a twist toward tempo: unlike a pure
lose-early value engine, Diggie wants to **win** (the +10/win speeds the chest), so a stable winning board
both protects HP *and* compounds his value. Survive, win where I can, and harvest the rarity-climbing
opens.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Win to accelerate (but do not over-spend).** Wins add +10 points (faster, better opens), so a
   winning board is doubly rewarded; but I still must bank the interest band and not over-roll for a
   marginal win. Balance tempo (for chest speed) against econ.
2. **Flex the rewards.** The opens give rarity-climbing rewards (random), so **convert** them: hero ->
   comp/fodder, item -> carry, gold -> roll. The later opens are best (higher rarity), so the engine
   rewards reaching them.

## The chest plan (his signature, concrete)

The ordered way to run the engine:
1. **Take the exclusive** (it doubles the point rate and adds an extra open), the single biggest
   accelerant.
2. **Win rounds where I can** (+10 points each) to speed the opens and climb the rarity ladder faster.
3. **At each open, flex the reward** into my board (the rarity rises each time, so later opens are
   premium, prioritize reaching the 2nd and 3rd).
4. **If I must lose**, the chest tanks up (+300 Max HP), so a losing streak is not pure waste, but prefer
   winning for the points.
5. **Protect the chest** (it is a battlefield object); a destroyed chest before it opens is lost value, so
   position the board to keep it safe.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): start the clock, build to win
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Build a board that can **win early rounds** (to feed the chest's +10/win).
  Creep: a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take the exclusive** (+15 points/round + an extra open), NOT the borrowable
  (which redundantly re-grants the chest). It doubles the engine; keep it.
- **I-3 / I-4:** win where I can, bank toward the band; the chest ticks toward its first open.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero opening the line the rewards favor.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): first opens, commit
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit a synergy.
- **The chest opens** (first, then second with the exclusive's rate); flex the rarity-climbing rewards
  into the comp. Keep winning to accelerate.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** value-rich, bid well on a key item or a Synergy Magic Crystal.
- **Leveling:** steady to the carry's tier; a stronger board wins more = faster chest.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): premium opens, spike
- **The late, high-rarity opens** + the bank fund a spike; roll to star up, take a payoff card. The extra
  open (exclusive) lands here-ish.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, gear the carry.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the reward-built board, dump banked gold into the last star-ups and items. Close before HP is the
  binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Steady; a stronger board wins more (faster chest), so level to the carry's tier and for a winning board.
The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`.
The chest's gold/rewards fund the climb; do not bank so long the board cannot win (which would slow the
chest).

## Best comps (concrete)

**Flexible**, shaped by the chest rewards, and tilted toward a **winning** board:
- **Frontline + one carry** ([[../frontline_tank]]): a stable winner that both protects HP and speeds the
  chest, the natural Diggie shell.
- **A committed synergy** the rewards thicken.
- The constant: a board that **wins** (for the +10/round) and flexes the rarity-climbing opens.

## Positioning

chest before it opens loses value). Otherwise carry back-center, wall front, spread vs AoE. Full detail:

## Power-card deep dive

His **exclusive** (**+15 points/round + an extra open**) is the right pick, it roughly doubles the chest's
rate and adds a premium open, accelerating the whole engine. Keep it. The **borrowable** is a **trap on
the owner** (it re-grants the chest he already has), it exists for *other* commanders to borrow a chest.
Use the 2 refreshes only if a capacity/synergy card clearly wins more.

## Contingency and pivots

- **Losing board (slow chest):** the +300 Max HP on losses keeps the chest alive, but prefer to
  **stabilize and win** (the +10/win is the accelerant); spend to hold a winning board.
- **Off-comp rewards:** flex them as fodder/gold rather than forcing them on.
- **Value-ahead, board-behind:** dump the banked value now (anti-greed).
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** convert all rewards + gold into the board and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**Draw-independent value that *accelerates when I win*.** Unlike a passive lose-early engine, Diggie's
chest speeds up on victories and climbs in rarity each open, so a good board compounds his value (win ->
faster, better opens -> better board), and the exclusive doubles the whole loop. A high-floor value
climber that rewards playing well, not just patiently.

## Pitfalls

- **Taking the borrowable card** (redundant, re-grants the chest).
- **Playing to lose** (it slows the chest; the +10/win is the accelerant, prefer winning).
- **Letting the chest die** before it opens (protect the battlefield object).
- **Hoarding rewards** instead of converting them.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the premium opens. A **board that cannot win** slows the chest (though
losses tank it up). He is strongest in **long games with a winning board** (the chest compounds);
weakest in fast ones or on a losing board. A high-floor value engine that rewards a strong, winning board.

## Numbers that matter (the engine, quantified)

- **Point rate:** **15/round** base (**+10 on a win**); the **exclusive adds +15/round** (~30/round, ~every
  3-4 rounds an open) **+ an extra open**. Wins compound the rate, so a strong board harvests faster.
- **Opens:** at **100 points**, **max 3** (4 with the exclusive's extra), each **upgrading in rarity**, so
  the later opens are premium, the engine rewards reaching them.
- **Loss insurance:** a round loss **+300 Max HP** to the chest (a battlefield object), so a forced losing
  streak does not destroy the engine, but winning is still better (points).
