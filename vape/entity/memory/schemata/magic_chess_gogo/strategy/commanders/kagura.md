> Note: the **commander** Kagura is the player avatar this file covers, distinct from the **hero**
> Kagura, a 4-cost Mage/Exorcist unit ([[../../infos/heroes/cost_4]]). The replay piloted this commander
> with a mage core (vs weak AI, so illustrative, not battle-tested).

## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** combat / carry-protector. She turns one fragile back-row carry into a sticky,
  hard-to-kill one.
- **Plays like:** a glass-cannon enabler, run the squishiest, highest-DPS carry the lobby has and make
  it survive the dive that would normally delete it.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is one decision (who gets the shield) plus a normal economy she must
  run herself, since the kit pays no gold.
- **Best into:** single-threat assassin / burst lobbies (the shield hard-counters the back-row dive).
  **Worst into:** heavy-CC boards (shield stops death, not a stun-lock), economy-snowball commanders who
  out-resource her, and go-wide boards that win without ever needing to kill the shielded carry.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Kagura, "Onmyouji Master." Skins: Onmyouji Master, Onmyouji Spring. (The Onmyouji Master
  skin grants +2% Hero Extra DMG, per the replay's commander-select screen.)
- **Shield Umbrella** [Active] (verbatim): *"During Preparation Stage, you can select a Hero to gain
  Shield. When that Hero's HP first drops below 50% in battle, they gain a Shield equal to 60% of their
  Max HP."*
- **Yin Yang Gathering** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Shield value increases to 135% of Max
  HP."*
- **Power Card:** *Kagura's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Select a Hero. At battle start
  they gain 20% Hybrid Lifesteal and 30% Hybrid ATK, and gain 9 Gold."* (The replay's I-2 pick.)

Reading (in pencil): she makes **one chosen carry extremely hard to kill**, a shield worth **60% Max HP**
(135% once her commander stars up) the instant it crosses 50% HP, exactly when a diver or burst would
finish it. The power card then makes that same carry **hit 30% harder and self-sustain** (20% lifesteal)
*and* hands +9 gold (rare econ on a combat kit). A combat commander built to keep a fragile carry alive
through the one death-window that kills carry comps.

## Win condition / identity

**A glass cannon that does not break.** Squishy back-row carries (mages, marksmen) do the most damage
but die to dives and burst; Kagura's shield turns that fragile carry into a sticky one, so it survives
long enough to out-DPS the lobby. She is the **answer to the assassin meta**: even a Swiftblade diving
the carry eats a 135%-Max-HP shield and the carry lives to keep casting. The replay's winning line:
commander Kagura + a 6-count mage core, carried by Odette and the hero Kagura.

## The core tension (every round)

The shield (and the card's buff) go on **one hero**. Pick the unit that both **does the damage** and
**most needs to survive**, the back-row carry, and stack everything (shield + card + items) on it. Her
kit is **combat-only**, so the standing tension is that **I must run a full economy alongside it** (the
interest band, a clean streak, leveling on pace) because the shield does not pay rent. A great shield on
a weak, under-econ'd board still loses.

## The shield-target decision (her signature, concrete)

Each Preparation Stage I re-select the Shield Umbrella target. The priority:
1. **The primary back-row carry** (the highest-DPS, squishiest unit), default, almost always.
2. **Switch only if** the carry is genuinely safe and a *second* threatened key unit (a fed secondary
   carry, or a load-bearing caster) is the one actually dying. Rare.
3. **Never** the front-line tank, it already survives; the shield is wasted there.

The shield triggers at **first drop below 50% HP**, so it rewards a carry that the enemy *focuses* (the
shield lands right as the burst arrives). Pair the target with the power-card buff and the items, all
three on the same hero, to make one overwhelming, unkillable threat.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): build toward a carry, run normal econ
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Build toward a **carry comp** (mage or marksman); buy the early bodies of
  that line and a starter front. Creep: a damage/ATK-speed basic earmarked for the eventual carry.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take Kagura's Power** (the replay's pick): +30% ATK + 20% lifesteal on the
  carry *plus* +9 gold is both a power spike and an econ tempo card, perfectly on-kit and rare for a
  combat commander. Keep it. (Refresh only if I have no carry direction yet and a synergy spike fits
  better.)
- **I-3 / I-4:** keep buying the carry line + front; bank toward the interest band (her edge is combat,
  not gold, so do *not* fall behind on econ).
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** grab the carry's best-in-slot item or a hero that opens the
  carry's synergy.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): lock the carry, shield it
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a free +1 count to a near breakpoint.
- **Lock the carry** and put the **Shield Umbrella** on it every prep. Cross the carry-class [4] and a
  frontline [2]. Reroll/level at the carry's tier.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a carry-item bundle or a synergy payoff.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for the **carry's best-in-slot Enhanced item** (the carry is the
  whole plan) or a Synergy Magic Crystal. The +9 gold from her card helps fund this.
- **Leveling:** push to L7-8 for the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): spike the shielded carry
- **Spike** the carry (star-up, the [6], a synergy payoff card like Purest Power) now that it is shielded
  and buffed. With the commander starred, the shield is **135% Max HP**, the carry is nearly unkillable;
  press the round wins.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, grab anti-CC tools (a Purify for the
  carry against a control lobby).

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Items maxed on the carry, shield + card + gear all stacked on it. A shielded, fed carry should outlast
  the lobby's burst. Close before HP becomes the binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Standard for the carry comp: sit at the tier the carry needs (**L8-9 for a 4-cost carry**), reroll for
the star-up and support, level for capacity. The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3:
`L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Nothing commander-specific forces the
pace; play the comp's clock, and use the card's +9 gold to stay on the interest band while doing it.

## Best comps (concrete)

**Squishy hyper-carry comps that need their carry to survive**, the shield solves their one weakness:
- **Mage core** ([[../mage_core]], the replay line): Kagura (hero, 4c) + Odette (5c) carries, Mage [6]
  via Cecilion/Lylia/Vexana/Julian, Stargazer mana (Aurora/Pharsa), Defender/Dauntless [2] front. Shield
  the primary carry (Odette).
- **Marksman carry** ([[../marksman_carry]]): Karrie/Irithel/Lesley back-row carry that needs ramp time;
  the shield buys the ATK-speed ramp.
- **Tanky-carry variant:** if no squishy carry opens, shield a **bruiser-carry** (Esmeralda, Ruby, Yu
  Zhong) instead, the shield makes an already-sturdy front-carry nearly immortal, a lower-ceiling but
  very forgiving line for a safe placement.
- Either way: front a Defender/Dauntless [2], protect the back, shield + buff + items all on the one
  carry.

**The hero-Kagura note:** the **hero** Kagura (4c Mage/Exorcist) is a natural fit on the **commander**
Kagura's mage core (it was a top-damage unit in the replay). Fielding the hero does not interact with the
shield mechanically, but it is a strong Mage [6] body and a clean carry to shield, so the
commander-and-hero pairing is a coherent, on-theme line, not a gimmick.

## Positioning

The shield buys *one* death-window, not invulnerability, so position still matters: carry **back-center,
never naked in a corner** (Swiftblade divers land on cornered carries). **Corner-guard** and add a
**peeler** so a diver trades into a dead assassin even with the shield. Spread vs AoE. The shield + good
position together are what make the carry truly unkillable; one without the other is not enough. Full
detail: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

I-1 L3: buy two mage bodies (Cecilion), take a magic/ATK-speed basic off the creep. I-2: take Kagura's
Power on Cecilion (+30% ATK, +20% lifesteal, +9 gold). Stage II: commit the mage core, field Odette as

Odette's Glowing Wand (Enhanced) off the card's gold cushion. III: spike Mage [6], take Purest Power,

## Tier lists (for Kagura)

The tier list as it lives *here*, in Kagura's form of life (the general [[../tier_lists]] is only the
upward aggregate, set it aside when piloting her). In pencil:
- **Synergies:** **squishy-carry lines rise** (Mage, Marksman, Stargazer), the shield makes their
  fragility safe, so they out-rank their general placement here. Raw tank synergies are *less* needed
  (the shield is the carry's survival), though a [2] front is still wanted.
- **Heroes by phase:** the **squishy back-row carries rise** (Odette, hero-Kagura, Karrie, Vexana,

- **Go Go Cards:** her **exclusive S** (buff + lifesteal on the carry + the rare combat-kit gold); carry

- **Equipment:** **stack damage on the carry** (the shield handles its survival, so go *greedy* on

  items go on the front. Synergy Magic Crystal / Mirror Device S as universal levers.
- **Stage I basics (the I-1 creep, pick 1 of 3):** a **damage basic (Retribution/Inspire)** on the carry
  (the shield covers survival, so go offensive); **Purify vs a CC lobby** (the shield stops *death*, but
  Purify stops the *stun-lock* that would freeze a shielded-but-CC'd carry, her one real weakness);
  **Aegis** if the board is thin; **Revitalize** lowest.

## Power-card deep dive

Her exclusive is **excellent and on-kit**: it buffs the same carry the shield protects (+30% ATK, +20%
lifesteal) *and* hands +9 gold, which is rare and valuable on an otherwise econ-less combat kit. Keep it
almost always. The only deviation: if I have no carry direction yet at I-2 and a generic synergy spike
wins the immediate fight more, fish with the refreshes; but for any carry comp, hers is a top-tier I-2
pick (the replay confirmed the feel, if not the difficulty).

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Assassins and burst** aimed at the carry: the shield buys it one life, but watch for **multiple
  divers or heavy AoE**, the shield saves one hero from one death window, not everything. Position still
  matters; corner-guard and peel.
- **CC commanders** (Lukas's statue stun, hard control): the shield stops death, **not a stun-lock**, a
  carry CC'd while shielded still does no damage. Purify the carry against them, her one true counter.
- **Economy commanders** (Aurora, Diggie, Lancelot, Neobeasts) out-resource me over a long game; I must
  convert my combat edge into HP pressure before they out-scale.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **Carry still dying** (multi-dive / AoE): re-position (back-center, corner-guard), add a peeler, and
  consider shielding a more survivable carry. The shield is one window, not a wall.
- **No clear carry yet:** the shield is wasted without one, so prioritize finding and committing the
  carry; until then the kit is dormant (play a normal tempo board).
- **Behind on econ:** her kit gives little gold (just the card's one-time +9), so tighten standard
  economy (streak + interest band) and do not over-roll early.
- **CC-heavy lobby:** tech Purify onto the carry and add control of my own; the shield alone does not
  beat a stun-lock.
- **Shield-bait read:** a sharp opponent may chip the carry to *trigger* the shield early, then burst it
  after the shield is spent. Counter by positioning so the shield triggers *during* their focus window
  (back-center + corner-guard delays the dive), not on a stray hit beforehand.
- **Two viable carries:** the shield is one target, so pick the one the enemy will *focus* (usually the
  highest-Power back-row unit); a second carry can take the lifesteal/ATK card instead, splitting the
  buffs across the two threats.

## The edge (the secret)

She gets the **upside of a glass cannon without the downside**: the highest-DPS, squishiest carry, made
survivable. In a meta full of back-row dives, a **135%-Max-HP shield** on the carry is a hard counter to
the exact thing that kills carry comps, and her card lets her stack damage greedily where another carry
would need defensive items. Her carry both deals the most and dies the least.

## Pitfalls

- **Shield on the wrong hero** (a tank that does not need it) instead of the damage carry.
- **No carry to protect:** the kit is dead weight on a board with no clear single carry.
- **Neglecting economy:** combat-only kit means I must still play the gold game; a strong shield on a
  weak, broke board still loses.
- **Forgetting Purify vs CC:** the shield stops death, not stun, so a control lobby walks through a
  shielded-but-locked carry.

## Threat assessment (who beats her, and why)

**Economy and value commanders** (Aurora, Diggie, Lancelot, Neobeasts) out-resource her over a long game
(she has almost no gold kit), so she must convert her combat edge into HP pressure before they
out-scale. **Heavy CC** (Lukas, control boards) neutralizes the shielded carry, alive but stunned = no
damage. **Go-wide / multi-carry boards** that win without ever needing to kill the shielded carry
sidestep her whole plan. She is strongest into **single-threat assassin / burst** lobbies (the shield is
a hard counter) and weakest into control-heavy or economy-snowball games.

## Numbers that matter (the shield, quantified)

- **Shield size:** **60% of Max HP** at base, **135% of Max HP** once her commander stars up (Yin Yang
  Gathering), applied **the instant the carry first drops below 50% HP**, exactly when a diver's burst
  would finish it. On a 4-cost carry with ~13-16k Max HP at 3-star, that is a **~9-22k** shield landing
  at the death-window.
- **The card on top:** +30% Hybrid ATK and +20% Hybrid Lifesteal on that same carry at battle start, so
  it both hits harder and heals itself behind the shield, plus the one-time +9 gold.
- **The limit (why Purify matters):** the shield stops **death**, not **control**. A carry that is
  stunned (Lukas, hard CC) while shielded does no damage, so against a CC lobby, **Purify on the carry**
  is the missing half; the shield alone is not enough.

## The 7-archetype read (who I face, and the adjustment)

- vs **single-threat assassin / combat** (Ling, Alice's one carry, Swiftblade lobbies): **my best
  matchup**, the shield hard-counters the back-row dive. Corner-guard and let them break on the shield.
- vs **CC / control** (Lukas stun, hard control): **my worst**, the shield stops death, not stun. Purify
  the carry, the shield alone loses here.
- vs **go-wide / multi-carry** (Neobeasts, Mystic Meow, Lunox [6]): they win without needing to kill my
  shielded carry; I must out-DPS, not just out-survive.
- vs **econ / value** (Lancelot, Aurora, Diggie, Neobeasts): they out-resource me late (I have almost no
  gold kit), so convert my combat edge into HP pressure before they scale.
- vs **vertical** (Aamon, Alice): I match them with a shielded carry of my own; the shield lets me play a
  squishier, higher-DPS carry than they dare.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong): I want the same damage items (I stack offense, the shield
  covers survival), so contest gear if it is my carry's best-in-slot.
- vs **free-hero / synergy** (Guinevere, Harley, Vale, Lunox): I do not contest the pool, I just need one
  carry line; let them have the wide edge.
