## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** combat / board expansion. A Hero Launcher summons a **Shadow** (an extra body) to the
  fight, and her card hands **+1 Hero Capacity**.
- **Plays like:** out-bodying the lobby, field a Shadow copy of a key hero (a 2nd carry or a front) plus an
  extra slot, so my board is effectively bigger.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is a build-and-launch plus a capacity card; the skill is choosing which
  hero to Shadow and using the extra body well.
- **Best into:** boards where an extra threat or front swings the fight. **Worst into:** fast aggro before
  the Launcher completes, and a Shadow on the wrong (low-impact) hero.

Reading (in pencil): the Hero Launcher (10 rounds base, **2 rounds** once she stars up) lets me **drag a
hero onto it to summon a Shadow** of that hero that **joins the battle**, an extra body that does not cost
a normal deploy slot, and the launched hero gains **+20% Hybrid ATK & HP** (starred). +4 gold on

she **out-bodies the lobby**: a Shadow (a duplicated threat or front) **plus** an extra slot means her
board is effectively bigger than capacity allows.

Research notes (2026-07-10, `../../research/fanny/`, two transcripts; the +20% launcher bonus
confirmed live on a Karrie): the launcher's quiet second use is **the free farming slot**. Normally
fielding Scavenger pieces weakens the fighting board; with Fanny the extra effective body absorbs
that cost, so **she farms without getting weaker** ("if you play Fanny and don't farm, you're wasting
the kit"). Scavenger + the shop-lock trick ([[../scavenger_tempo]]) ride on her more cheaply than on
anyone. One conflict to verify live: one research pilot skips her own card claiming it only wins the
early game and prefers Kagura's / Lancelot's / econ cards instead, while the other took Fanny's card
to a 1st place (6 Emberlord + 4 Kishin, double 2-star legends); the datamined +1-capacity text does
not obviously decay, so the skip may concern an older card version. Both stances in pencil.

## Win condition / identity

**Field more board than the lobby can, then win the numbers fight.** An extra Shadow (a copy of my carry =
two threats, or a copy of a tank = a fatter front) plus the +1 capacity gives me more damage, more
breakpoint bodies, and more board presence than the field, and a bigger board wins auto-battles. She is a
**combat board-expander**: flexible, the Shadow amplifies whatever I am building (double the carry, double
the wall, or fill a breakpoint).

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Which hero to Shadow.** The Shadow copies the dragged hero, so launch the **highest-impact** one, a
   carry (two threats), a key front (a fatter wall), or a breakpoint body, and the +20% (starred) makes
   the launched hero itself stronger too.
2. **Build the Launcher, run the economy.** It takes 2 rounds (starred) to complete, so start it early;
   and the kit is mostly combat (just +4 gold), so run a normal economy alongside.

## The shadow plan (her signature, concrete)

The ordered way to use the Launcher:
1. **Start the Launcher early** (it completes in 2 rounds starred; +4 gold on completion).
2. **Take her card** for **+1 capacity at L5** (an extra slot, near-universal value).
3. **Drag the highest-impact hero onto it** to summon a Shadow: my **carry** (two threats) when I want
   damage, or a **front body** when I want to hold, the launched hero also gains +20% ATK/HP.
4. **Position the Shadow** (the kit lets me adjust its deploy spot) to maximize it, a 2nd carry back, a
   2nd tank front.
5. **Combine the extra body + the +1 slot** so my fielded power exceeds the lobby's capacity, then win the
   numbers.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): start the Launcher, build a board
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Build a normal early board and **start the Launcher**. Creep: a flexible
  damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take her card** (+1 capacity at L5): an extra slot is near-universal value, and
  her own kit loves more board. Keep it.
- **I-3 / I-4:** complete the Launcher (2 rounds starred), Shadow a strong early unit; bank toward the band.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a breakpoint hero.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): out-body, cross breakpoints
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; the extra slot + Shadow help field more
  breakpoints.
- **Shadow the carry or a key front**, reach **+1 capacity at L5**, cross the first breakpoints with the
  larger board.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for the carry's item or a Synergy Magic Crystal.
- **Leveling:** to the carry's tier + capacity.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): spike the bigger board
- **The extra body + slot let me field a denser board** (two carries, or a fuller synergy); spike, take a
  payoff card.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, gear the carry.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the over-sized board (Shadow + the extra slot), dump banked gold into star-ups and items. Close
  before HP is the binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Reach **L5 for the capacity card** promptly (the extra slot is the payoff), then level to the carry's tier.
The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`.
The +4 gold (Launcher) helps; run a normal economy (the kit is combat-tilted).

**Flexible**, the Shadow + slot amplify any board; the research's lived default first:

  Marksman directly, you leak everywhere").
- **Double-carry:** Shadow the primary carry for two threats (a mage core with two casters, a marksman
  line with two shooters).
- **Fatter front:** Shadow a key tank for a wall the lobby cannot break.
- **Breakpoint-filler:** Shadow a synergy body + the +1 slot to reach a [4]/[6] cheaply; the launcher
  also hosts the Scavenger farm piece at zero board cost.
- The constant: **out-body the lobby**, play aggressive (her early game is strong when the combo is
  known: protect the win streak, level rather than break it), and roll calm while HP > 40 (interest
  20); panic-roll at interest 10 only below ~40 HP (the research pilot's rule).

## Power-card deep dive

Her card (**+1 Hero Capacity at L5**) is **excellent and near-universal**, an extra slot is value for
almost any comp, and her own board-expansion kit loves it doubly. Keep it. It is also **the best
borrowable card in the game**, so other commanders fish for it, on Fanny herself it is a clean keep. Use
the 2 refreshes only if a synergy spike clearly wins more. (One research pilot disputes the keep,
calling her card early-only and preferring Kagura's/Lancelot's; the other kept it to a 1st place.
Verify live.)

**Cross-card fishing (what to borrow when the keep is not clear-cut):**
- **Dyrroth's** (devour a 1-star every 3 rounds -> a +1-cost hero, +2 EXP): the best kit-fit in the
  pool. Her launcher + the farm slot keep spare cheap bodies flowing through her board, exactly the
  chaff the devour eats; the ladder converts it into 4-5 costs and the EXP feeds her L5 gate.
- **Ling's** (the same random 4-cost at L4/5/7): a free near-2-star 4-cost is the ideal **launch
  target**, the +20% launcher bonus wants a real hero, and the L7 third copy lands on her mid-game.
- **Yu Zhong's** (subsequent cards +1 quality): the compounding first-pick if the plan leans on the
  later card stages; the price is this window's guaranteed capacity card (re-offer at II-3/III-3
  unverified, so treat it as spending the shot).
- The rule stays: her exclusive is the default; borrow only when one of these clearly out-pays an
  extra slot for THIS run.

## Contingency and pivots

- **Launcher not done / Shadow on a weak hero:** start it earlier and Shadow the highest-impact unit; a
  Shadow of a filler is wasted board.
- **Behind early:** the +4 gold + the extra body stabilize; out-body a weak spot.
- **Capacity-rich but thin power:** Shadow a *carry* (not a filler) so the extra body is a threat, not just
  a chump.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** field the Shadow + the extra slot for max board and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**She fields more board than the lobby's capacity allows.** A Shadow (a duplicated carry or front, +20%
starred) plus the +1 capacity card means Fanny out-bodies the field, more damage, more breakpoints, more
presence, and in an auto-battle, more (good) board usually wins. A flexible combat expander whose extra
unit amplifies whatever she builds.

## Pitfalls

- **Shadowing a low-impact hero** (waste the extra body on the carry or a key tank).
- **Starting the Launcher late** (it needs to complete to pay).
- **Neglecting economy** (combat-tilted kit) or **missing L5** for the capacity.
- **Clumping the extra bodies into an AoE** (spread the larger board).

## Threat assessment (who beats her, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the Launcher + capacity compound. **Burst** that deletes the Shadowed
carry blunts the extra-threat plan. She is strongest in **board-state fights** where more (good) units win
and weakest in fast finishes. A flexible combat expander whose power is the numbers advantage.

## Numbers that matter (the expansion, quantified)

- **The extra body:** a **Shadow** that joins the fight without a normal deploy slot, effectively **+1
  unit** on the board (a duplicated carry or tank), and the launched hero gains **+20% Hybrid ATK & HP**
  (starred).
- **The slot:** **+1 Hero Capacity at L5** (her card), so combined she fields ~**2 more effective units**
  than a normal commander at the same level, a real numbers edge in auto-battle.
- **The Launcher:** **2 rounds** to build (starred), **+4 gold** on completion, start it early.
