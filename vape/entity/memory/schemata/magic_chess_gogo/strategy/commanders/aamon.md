# Commander — Aamon (Duke of Shards): free copies, pool-proof verticals

The deep, concrete playbook, round by round. **In pencil**, reasoned from

## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** economy + vertical hybrid. He prints free copies (Mirror Devices) and gold off the
  2-stars he was making anyway.
- **Plays like:** the patient pair-buyer who turns a normal star-up grind into Mirror Devices, then
  duplicates past the shared pool to complete a 3-star the lobby could not.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The loop is mechanical once it spins up; the skill is *where* the scarce
  Mirrors go and keeping a pair-buying tempo so the shards never stall.
- **Best into:** mid-length gold-fed grinds where his loop matures; contested verticals he can out-copy.
  **Worst into:** fast aggro that ends before the loop spins up, Alice (a faster single-carry), and a
  rival who contests his Mirror Devices in the auction.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Aamon, "Duke of Shards." Skins: Duke of Shards, Night's Edge.
- **Blade of Resonance** [Passive] (verbatim): *"Obtain 1 Mirror Device at the start of the match. When a
  Hero reaches 2-Star, you'll get 1 Shard(s). 7 Shard(s) will be automatically converted into 1 Mirror
  Device(s). You can obtain up to 2 Mirror Devices."*
- **Skybreaker Blade** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Gain 12 extra Gold each time a Mirror Device
  is created."*
- **Power Card:** *Aamon's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Gain 4 Gold each time a Hero
  reaches 2-Star (up to 20 Gold in total)."*

Reading (in pencil): a **Mirror Device duplicates a hero** ([[../../infos/equipments/special]]: "Copy 1-
to 5-Gold Hero"), and a free copy is the scarcest thing in a contested pool, a guaranteed step toward a
star-up. Aamon turns his **normal play (making 2-stars)** into **shards -> Mirror Devices -> free
copies + 12 gold each**, and the card adds +4 gold per 2-star (cap 20). The cap wording is ambiguous:
"up to 2 Mirror Devices" likely means at most 2 from the shard conversion (plus the 1 at match start),
so call it **2-3 Mirrors a game**, scarce, so each one matters. Each Mirror needs **7 two-stars** of
shards, so the engine demands a steady pair-buying tempo.

## Win condition / identity

**3-star a carry the pool would deny, funded by the gold his kit prints.** His loop: buy pairs -> 2-star
them -> shards + card gold -> a Mirror Device (+12 gold) -> a free copy toward a 3-star carry. He is an
**economy-plus-vertical hybrid**: he completes a *contested* vertical others cannot, and gets paid
(+12/Mirror, +4/2-star) for the exact 2-star grind the game already rewards. A Mirror-finished 3-star is
a power nobody who fought the pool fairly will match.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Mirror placement (scarce).** With only ~2-3 Mirrors a game, each must go on the **hardest-to-
   complete, highest-value copy**: a 4-cost I am one short of 2-star, or the copy that finishes a 3-star
   carry, **never** a throwaway 1-cost. A wasted Mirror is most of a game's edge gone.
2. **Pair-buying tempo (the shard fuel).** Shards only come from 2-stars, so I should always be buying
   **pairs** (two of a hero) rather than single bodies, or the shard engine and the card gold both
   stall. The shape of his shop habit is different from everyone else's: collect pairs.

## The Mirror-Device loop (his signature, concrete)

The ordered plan for the engine:
1. **Hold the starting Mirror.** Do not spend it on early tempo; save it for a committed carry's copy.
2. **Buy pairs, 2-star them.** Every 2-star = 1 shard (+4 card gold). Aim to keep pairs flowing so the
   7-shard counter climbs.
3. **At 7 shards, a Mirror is created (+12 gold).** Bank the gold, hold the Mirror for the highest-value
   duplication.
4. **Spend each Mirror on the single best copy:** the one that finishes a 3-star carry or 2-stars a
   contested 4-cost. Prefer duplicating a **higher-cost** hero (a Mirror copies any cost, so it is worth
   most on a 4-cost the pool starves you of).
5. **Convert when it wins rounds.** Do not hoard a Mirror so long that the spike comes too late;
   duplicate into a star-up the round it would flip fights.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): start the loop, hold the Mirror
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Buy toward a board of **pairs** I can 2-star (starts the shard loop) plus
  a starter front. **Hold the starting Mirror Device** for a committed carry. Creep: a damage basic for
  the eventual carry.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take Aamon's Power** (+4 gold per 2-star, up to 20): pure on-kit, it pays for
  the 2-stars I am already chasing and snowballs the econ. Keep. (Little reason to deviate, his engine
  wants exactly the 2-stars the card rewards.)
- **I-3 / I-4:** keep buying pairs; bank toward the interest band on top of the kit/card gold (he is
  gold-rich, so he can both bank and spend).
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** grab a **copy** of the committed carry or a Mirror-adjacent lot;
  else the carry's best item.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): run the loop hard
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a free +1 count (and that 2-star feeds a shard too,
  double value).
- **Run the loop:** keep 2-starring to farm shards -> a Mirror (+12 gold). Aim the Mirrors at a
  **committed carry's copies**. Cross the first breakpoints. Level to the carry's tier.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a card that feeds copies (Fateful Dice = a Mirror Device) or the
  carry's line.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** as Aamon I **want Mirror-related lots** (a Fateful Dice, a Mirror
  Device) and copies of my carry; my gold-rich econ lets me bid well. Mirror/copy lots are my premium.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): complete the 3-star
- **Complete the 3-star** with the Mirrors + rolls; the gold the kit printed funds the spike. A
  Mirror-finished 3-star is the payoff.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints or grab another copy/Mirror lot.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- The gold lead + the completed verticals close it. Dump the bank into the last star-ups and items.

## Leveling cadence

**Gold-rich, so he can level *and* roll** more freely than most. Level to the carry's tier, then roll
hard (the kit gold cushions it) to feed 2-stars (shards) and finish 3-stars. The upgrade ladder,
cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Do **not**
over-level past the tier where his target copies live, the Mirror still needs a *base copy from the
right tier* to duplicate, so a diluted shop hurts the loop.

## Best comps (concrete)

**Vertical comps that want 3-stars**, especially mid-cost carries the Mirror can complete:
- **A 3-cost vertical** (Miya, Saber, Helcurt, Masha) 3-starred with Mirror help, fielded into a
  frontline shell.
- **A contested 4-cost 2-star** (Kagura, Karrie, Vexana) the Mirror duplicates past the pool.
- **A cheap Mage vertical:** 3-star Cecilion (1c, 9 of 25 is easy) early for tempo, then aim Mirrors at a
  4-cost mage (Kagura/Vexana) to carry into the late game, the classic "cheap spike, then transition."
- **A Bruiser/Astro front-carry vertical:** Mirror-complete a 3-star Masha (3c Bruiser) or Hilda (3c
  Dauntless/Astro) for a sturdy carry-front that does not fold, forgiving if the loop is slow.
- He is **flexible**: the Mirror lets him **commit to a contested line** others cannot, so take whatever
  vertical is strong this patch and out-copy the lobby into it. Pair a Defender/Dauntless [2] front.
- **Mirror-priority within a comp:** spend the scarce Mirror on the **highest-cost / most-contested**
  copy on my board (a 4-cost 2-star over a 3-cost 3-star if the 4-cost is the real carry), since that is
  where the pool denies me most and the duplication is worth the most.

## Positioning

Standard: the 3-star carry sits back-center (never cornered vs Swiftblade divers), wall front with 2-3
tanks, corner-guard, spread vs AoE. Nothing Aamon-specific in positioning; his edge is in the build, not
the board shape. Full detail: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## A worked line (one ideal game)

I-1 L3: buy a pair of a 3-cost carry (Miya) and a front body, hold the starting Mirror. I-2: take
Aamon's Power. Stage I-II: I buy pairs relentlessly, 2-starring 5-6 heroes by mid-II, each a shard +4
gold; the shard counter hits 7, a Mirror is created (+12 gold). I now hold 2 Mirrors. I aim both at Miya
copies, finishing the **3-star Miya** the pool would never have given me. Auction: I win a Fateful Dice
(another Mirror) off my fat econ and a Miya copy. III: 3-star Miya + a Marksman [4] shell carries; the
+12/Mirror and +4/2-star gold funded the whole spike. IV: close on a board nobody could match for
copies. 1st or 2nd.

## Power-card deep dive

The exclusive (+4 gold per 2-star, cap 20) is **on-kit and snowballs his core loop**, every 2-star he
makes for shards also pays the card. **Keep it.** There is little reason to deviate, his gold engine
wants exactly the 2-stars the card rewards, and the early gold accelerates the whole Mirror loop. Only
consider a refresh if a once-a-game breakpoint card (a Synergy Badge/Blessing onto his exact axis) would
win more *and* his loop is already well-funded.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **As Aamon:** Mirror Devices and copy lots are my premium, grab them in the auction and from cards
  (Fateful Dice), and contest a vertical another player is grinding (I complete it faster with a Mirror).
- **Facing an Aamon** (the contest, [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]]): deny
  him **Mirror Devices** in the auction and the heroes his copies want to stack; his whole plan is free
  copies, so contesting the copy supply hurts him most.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **Loop not spun up (no 2-stars yet):** the kit is dormant, so prioritize **getting pairs to 2-star**
  to start shards; until then play a normal tempo board off the kit gold.
- **Behind:** the gold engine funds **aggressive roll-down stabilization**; use the bank (he is one of
  the richest commanders, so he can afford to roll out of trouble).
- **Carry contested:** the Mirror is my answer, **duplicate past the contention** instead of fighting the
  pool for the last copy. This is his signature pivot.
- **Mirror about to be wasted (no good target):** hold it rather than spend on a low-value copy; a Mirror
  saved is a future spike.
- **Both Mirrors spent, loop dry (cap reached):** I am now a normal vertical, so play the board I have and
  roll the kit gold for the last copies; do not stall waiting for a Mirror that will not come.
- **Pairs not coming (shop starving my line):** the shard engine needs 2-stars, so if my line is
  contested and pairs dry up, **pivot to an open line where pairs flow** (cheap if it shares a
  breakpoint), the loop matters more than any one carry.
- **Rich but loop slow:** the gold is piling up with no Mirror to spend it on, so just **roll it into the
  board directly** (star-ups, breakpoints), the kit gold is fuel even when the Mirror is not ready.

## The edge (the secret)

**Free copies make his verticals pool-proof, and he is paid for his normal play.** He 3-stars what
others cannot complete (a Mirror duplicates past the shared-pool cap), and prints gold (+12/Mirror,
+4/2-star) doing the exact 2-star loop the game already rewards. A flexible econ-vertical snowball that
beats the pool by duplicating it.

## Pitfalls

- **Wasting a Mirror** on a low-value copy instead of a key carry (the cardinal sin, Mirrors are scarce).
- **Not running pairs:** single bodies make no 2-stars, so the shard loop and the card gold both stall.
- **Hoarding Mirrors** too long instead of converting them into a spike when it would win rounds.
- **Over-leveling past the carry's tier**, diluting the base copies the Mirror needs to duplicate from.
- **Counting on the card past its cap:** Aamon's Power caps at **+20 gold** (5 two-stars), so the *card*
  gold flattens, the shards and Mirrors do not. Keep 2-starring for the **copies**, not the capped gold.
- **Neglecting the front:** the loop is absorbing, and it is easy to over-invest in the carry and field a
  paper wall. A Mirror-completed 3-star carry with no front still loses, always hold a Defender/Dauntless
  [2] alongside the vertical.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Alice** out-paces him on a single hyper-carry (she scales one carry off partial copies faster than his
Mirror loop completes a 3-star, and needs even fewer copies). **Fast aggressive boards** end the game
before his loop matures (it takes a few rounds of 2-starring to spin up the shards). A rival who
**contests his Mirror Devices** in the auction directly attacks his engine. His best games are
mid-length, gold-fed grinds; his worst are fast ones where the loop never got going. The throughline:
he is a **mid-game** commander, so any plan (mine or a rival's) that compresses the game into a fast
finish or stretches it into a pure-scaling marathon erodes his window, his power lives in the rounds
where the shard loop has spun up but the lobby has not yet hit its [6]/[10] ceilings.

## Numbers that matter (the loop, quantified)

- **Shards -> Mirror:** every **2-star = 1 shard** (+ 4 gold from the card, capped at +20 total). **7
  shards = 1 Mirror Device** (+12 gold, Skybreaker Blade), capped at ~2 Mirrors from conversion (+ the 1
  at match start). So **7 two-stars** yields roughly **+20 (card cap) + 12 (one Mirror) = ~+32 gold AND
  a free copy**, on top of normal income, for the exact 2-star grind I was doing anyway.
- **Why a Mirror beats a roll:** a 3-cost 3-star needs **9 of 18** pool copies ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/hero]]);
  a 4-cost 3-star needs **9 of 14** (often impossible if contested). A Mirror duplicates **any** cost, so
  spend it on the **highest-cost, most-contested** copy, that is where the pool denial is worst and the
  Mirror's value is highest.
- **The premium targets** (auction + rewards): a **Fateful Dice** (= a Mirror Device), a **Mirror Device**
  lot, and **copies of my committed carry**. Bid up to a lot's value-in-rounds; I am gold-rich, so I can.

## Stage checkpoints (am I on pace?)

Concrete benchmarks for the loop; the fix is below each. In pencil.
- **End of Stage I (~L5):** buying *pairs*, first 2-stars made (shards started), starting Mirror still
  held, card gold flowing. *Behind = no 2-stars? The loop is dormant, prioritize completing pairs.*
- **End of Stage II (~L7):** several 2-stars farmed, ~1 Mirror created (+12g), committed carry chosen,
  gold-rich. *Behind = Mirror spent on junk? Hold the next one for the carry.*
- **Stage III (~L8-9):** the **Mirror-completed 3-star** carry online, the kit gold funding a roll-down.
  *Behind = carry not 3-star? Aim every Mirror + roll at its copies.*
- **Stage IV (L10):** finalized, gold lead and completed verticals close it.

## The 7-archetype read (who I face, and the adjustment)

- vs **Alice** (faster single carry): she completes one carry quicker than my Mirror loop; I cannot out-
  copy her (she needs none), so out-place via a *broader* board, not a copy race.
- vs **fast aggro / combat** (Lancelot, Ling, Lukas, Wanwan): they end the game before my loop matures,
  protect HP and get pairs to 2-star ASAP to spin the engine.
- vs **another Aamon / vertical** (Paquito, Dyrroth): we contest the same copies and Mirror lots, deny
  his Mirror Devices in the auction; whoever loops faster wins.
- vs **econ snowball** (Lancelot, Chou, Cyclops): a gold race, but I am gold-rich *and* convert it to
  copies, so I usually win the conversion if my loop is spun up.
- vs **value engines** (Aurora, Diggie, Vexana): slower, I out-spike with the 3-star if I commit early.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong): they want gear, I want copies/Mirrors, little overlap, so I
  cede gear lots and grab Mirror/Fateful-Dice lots.
- vs **free-hero / synergy** (Guinevere, Harley, Vale, Gusion, Lunox): free heroes are pair fodder for me;
  I am happy to take the bodies they generate and 2-star them.
