## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** Go Go Card specialist. Every card he takes is **upgraded a quality tier**, so his
  augments are always a rarity stronger.
- **Plays like:** a card-quality player, build the run around augments, and take cards that are a tier
  better than anyone else's (a Purest Power I becomes II, etc.).
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is a passive card-upgrade; the skill is a comp the upgraded cards pay off
  and reading which card to take.
- **Best into:** comps that scale off card quality (tiered families like Purest Power, Barrier's Seal).
  **Worst into:** fast aggro before the cards pay, and a card-indifferent board.

Reading (in pencil): his cards are **upgraded a quality tier** (Blue -> Purple -> Orange) at II-3 (and
III-3 with the star-up), so a tiered card he picks is a rarity *stronger* than offered, a Purest Power I
becomes II (+20% -> +28% Hybrid ATK), a Barrier's Seal I becomes II (+650 -> +950 HP), etc. At max
quality (Orange), he gets a **Black Dragon Treasure** instead (a bonus reward), plus one at I-2 (star-up).
The card extends the upgrade to **subsequent** cards. He is a **card-quality specialist**: every augment
he takes punches a tier above its weight.

## Win condition / identity

**Take the same cards as everyone, but a tier stronger.** Card quality is his multiplier, so a comp built
around **tiered card families** (Purest Power, Solid Alliance, Barrier's Seal, Breath of Berserk) gets a
free rarity bump on every relevant pick, compounding into a board the field cannot match card-for-card.
He is a **card-leveraged flex** like Valir, but his edge is **quality** (each card stronger) where Valir's
is **quantity** (two cards). The skill is a comp those upgraded cards amplify and the Black Dragon
Treasures as bonus value.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Have a card-scaling comp.** The upgrade only matters if the card matters, so **commit a direction**
   (a synergy core for Purest Power, a front for Barrier's Seal, an econ/lifesteal stack) the upgraded
   cards pay off.
2. **Pick upgradeable (tiered) cards.** The bump helps **tiered families** most (I -> II -> III), so
   prefer those over flat one-off cards when the choice is close, and note that at max quality the bump
   converts to a Black Dragon Treasure (still value, but the card itself does not get stronger).

## The card-quality plan (his signature, concrete)

The ordered way to run the engine:
1. **Take his card at I-2** (upgrades subsequent cards a tier + a Black Dragon Treasure), turning on the
   quality engine early.
2. **Commit a card-scaling direction** by mid Stage II (a synergy core, a front, an econ stack).
3. **At II-3 (and III-3 starred), take a tiered card** that upgrades a rarity (Purest Power I -> II,
   Barrier's Seal I -> II), getting a payoff a tier above what the field can.
4. **Bank the Black Dragon Treasures** (from max-quality picks + the I-2 one) as bonus rewards.
5. **Convert the stronger cards into a board spike** (a bigger synergy buff, a tankier front, a fatter
   econ).

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): turn on the engine, commit a direction
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Build toward a card-scaling line. Creep: a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take his card** (upgrade subsequent cards a tier + a Black Dragon Treasure): it
  switches the quality engine on for the whole run, on-kit. Keep it.
- **I-3 / I-4:** bank toward the band; shape the comp the upgraded cards will amplify.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero opening my line.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): the upgraded card at II-3
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit the synergy/direction.
- **II-3:** take a **tiered card upgraded a rarity** (Purest Power I -> II, or a count card), a payoff
  stronger than the field's. Bank any Black Dragon Treasure.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for a key item or a Synergy Magic Crystal.
- **Leveling:** to the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): another upgraded card, spike
- **III-3 (starred):** another quality-upgraded card; the stronger augments pay off. Spike the board, roll
  to star up.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, gear the carry.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the card-amplified board, dump banked gold + Black Dragon Treasures into the last star-ups and
  items. Close before HP is the binding constraint.

## Best comps (concrete)

**Comps that scale off card quality (tiered families):**
- **A synergy core** (Mage [6], Marksman [6]) where Purest Power / Solid Alliance upgraded a tier is a
  bigger spike.
- **A front-heavy board** where Barrier's Seal / All-around Warrior upgraded is a tankier wall.
- **An econ/lifesteal stack** where Breath of Berserk / gold cards upgraded compound harder.
- The constant: **commit a direction whose tiered cards the upgrade amplifies.**

## Power-card deep dive

His card (**upgrade subsequent cards a tier + a Black Dragon Treasure**) is **on-kit**, it switches the
quality engine on for the whole run and hands a bonus reward. Keep it. Deviate only if a synergy-breakpoint
or capacity card clearly wins more for a committed line, but the engine-on card is the thematic and
practical pick. Use the 2 refreshes to confirm.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fast aggro** ends the game before the upgraded cards pay, protect HP and commit a direction early.
- **Valir** (the other card specialist): he doubles card *quantity*, I upgrade card *quality*; we both
  want strong card stages but rarely contest the same thing.
- **Synergy commanders** (Lunox, Vale) may share my payoff cards' synergy; prefer an open axis.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **No tiered card fits my comp:** take the best available (a flat card still helps), or a card whose
  upgrade converts to a Black Dragon Treasure (still value).
- **Behind early:** the kit is back-loaded to II-3/III-3, so survive with a normal board and commit a
  direction; the upgraded cards are the payoff.
- **Comp shifts:** flex; the upgrade helps whatever tiered card fits the new direction.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** convert the card power + treasures + gold into the board and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**Every augment a rarity stronger.** Yu Zhong takes the same cards as the field but a tier up (Purest
Power I -> II, Barrier's Seal I -> II), so his synergy spikes, walls, and econ stacks all punch above
their weight, and max-quality picks convert to bonus Black Dragon Treasures. A card-quality specialist
whose run is built a tier above everyone else's, when the comp scales off cards.

## Pitfalls

- **No card-scaling comp** (the upgrade is wasted on a card-indifferent board).
- **Picking flat one-off cards** over tiered families (the upgrade helps the families most).
- **Over-extending early** before the II-3/III-3 payoff.
- **Ignoring the Black Dragon Treasures** (they are real bonus value).

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the upgraded cards pay. A **card-indifferent board** wastes his edge.
He is strongest in **card-scaling comps** (synergy cores, tiered-card stacks) that reach II-3/III-3;
weakest in fast finishes. A niche-but-powerful card specialist whose power is card *quality*.

## Numbers that matter (the upgrade, quantified)

- **The bump:** **Blue -> Purple -> Orange** on cards at II-3 (and III-3 starred). Concretely, **Purest
  Power I -> II** is **+20% -> +28%** Hybrid ATK to the [6]; **Barrier's Seal I -> II** is **+650 -> +950**
  front-row HP; **Breath of Berserk I -> II** is **+8% -> +16%** Lifesteal. A free tier on every relevant
  pick.
- **At max quality:** the upgrade converts to a **Black Dragon Treasure** (a bonus reward), plus one at
  I-2 (star-up), so the kit is never wasted.
- **The card:** extends the upgrade to **subsequent** cards + another Black Dragon Treasure.

## The 7-archetype read (who I face, and the adjustment)

- vs **fast aggro / combat** (Lancelot, Ling, Lukas): they end it before II-3/III-3 pay, commit early and
  protect HP. My hard matchup.
- vs **Valir** (card quantity) and **synergy commanders** (Lunox, Vale): we lean on cards/synergy; my edge
  is quality, prefer an open payoff axis.
- vs **value engines** (Aurora, Diggie, Vexana): a value race; my upgraded cards spike mid-game, out-tempo.
- vs **vertical / copy** (Alice, Aamon): they spike a carry; I spike via stronger cards.
- vs **econ snowball** (Chou, Cyclops): upgraded econ cards keep me competitive.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong): upgraded item-bundle cards kit a carry; otherwise I cede gear.
- vs **synergy grant / free-hero** (Gusion, Guinevere): they shape comps; I shape mine with stronger cards.
