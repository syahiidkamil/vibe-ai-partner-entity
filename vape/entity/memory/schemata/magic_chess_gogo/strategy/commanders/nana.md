# Commander — Nana (Sweet Leonin): paid to take the hit

The deep, concrete playbook, round by round. **In pencil**, reasoned from
[[../../infos/commanders/resolved_commanders]], [[../../infos/gogo_cards/power_cards]],
[[../../infos/equipments/regular]], and the round calendar [[../../infos/rounds/index]]; **not yet lived**,
so verify by play and overwrite. Frame: [[index]]; spine: [[../general_strategy]]; the general (emergent)
tiers: [[../tier_lists]]; reasoning: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/index]]. Written to the standard in
[commanders_strategy_writing_guide](commanders_strategy_writing_guide.md).

## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** economy / survival; the damage-monetizer.
- **Plays like:** the one commander who *wants* to be hit, harvesting gold from the cheap early damage
  everyone else fears, with two survival cushions to catch the overshoot.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The line is simple (lose early, bank, stabilize) but the skill is the
  overshoot judgment, knowing exactly how low is too low, especially at Mythic.
- **Best into:** aggressive lobbies (their pressure is her income) and long games. **Worst into:**
  misreading the overshoot, and late out-scalers if she banks too long.

## The kit (exact)

- **Molina's Blessing (P):** **survive first death at 1 HP.**
- **Molina's Gift (P, the star-up):** **+8 Gold per 10 DMG taken** (up to **3x**); the **3rd trigger
  grants an Equipment Chest**.
- **Power Card:** *Nana's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - gain **1 Equipment Chest and 8
  Gold**, and the **Commander becomes immune to fatal DMG once**.

Reading (held in pencil, the wording is terse): **she is paid for HP loss.** Molina's Gift turns
Commander DMG taken into gold (+8 per 10, up to 3 triggers = +24 gold and an Equipment Chest). Molina's
Blessing is a survival cushion (a hero, likely the highest-value or a chosen one, survives its first
death at 1 HP each battle, the MLBB Molina-revive flavor; verify the exact target). The power card adds
gold, a chest, and a **one-time commander fatal-immunity**. Net: she profits from the exact thing other
commanders spend HP to avoid, and she carries safety nets so the overshoot is forgiving.

## Win condition / identity

**Out-economy the lobby by getting paid to take cheap early damage, then bank into a mid-game spike,
riding the cushions through the dips.** Where every other commander treats early HP as a cost, Nana
treats it as *income*: she leans into the lose-early line (gold + reward priority + the gift + a loss
streak), then stops bleeding and converts the fat wallet into board. Low-variance, high-floor, with a
free life or two built in.

## The core tension (every round)

1. **Harvest the gift, then stop.** Molina's Gift caps at **3 triggers** (~30 Commander DMG taken ->
   +24 gold + a chest). So I *want* to take ~30 cheap early HP to max it, then stabilize. Taking damage
   past the cap is just bleeding for nothing.
2. **Do not overshoot into elimination.** The cushions (hero survive-death + commander fatal-immunity)
   forgive a lot, but they are finite and one-time, so at Mythic, where opponents spike early and hit
   hard, a careless loss can chunk past them. The whole skill is **how low is too low.**

## The damage-for-gold harvest (Molina's Gift, the 3 triggers)

Concrete plan for the signature loop:
- Each **10 Commander DMG taken = +8 gold**, up to **3 times = +24 gold**, and the **3rd trigger also
  drops an Equipment Chest**. So the target is to **take ~30 HP of damage on purpose, as cheaply as
  possible.**
- Cheapest HP is Stage I-II (base DMG 3 / 5-6 + the winner's surviving star levels). So **field light
  and lose I-3, I-4, and 1-2 early Stage-II rounds**, which lands ~25-35 Commander DMG, enough to bank
  all three triggers plus the chest, while also building a **loss streak** (gold 1;1;2;3) and sitting
  **lowest HP** for the reward selections.
- **Stop the moment the 3rd trigger fires** (the gift is maxed) **and** the loss-streak/reward windows
  are banked; from there, stabilize and convert. Further losses pay nothing.

## The HP budget for the lose-early line (concrete)

Nana is the **best commander in the roster at the lose-early line**, because losing pays her three ways
at once: the **gift** (+24 gold + chest), the **loss-streak** gold, and **reward priority** (lowest-HP
picks first). Spending ~25-35 HP across Stage I-II to bank all of that is a bargain, and the cushions
(survive-death + the card's fatal-immunity) catch a misjudged loss. The floor is still real: **stop
before a leader's spiked board can chunk me past both cushions in one or two rounds** (watch the
strongest board's Commander DMG, 13-15 + stars by Stage IV). Never lose-streak into Stage IV; the late
hits are too big even for her.

## Double-dipping the reward order (the calendar edge)

Beyond the one-time end-of-I pick, Nana's low HP pays off **repeatedly** across the calendar. The Go Go
Box stages are all **lowest-HP-first**: the **end-of-Stage-I** and **end-of-Stage-III** Reward
Selections (8 equipment + 3 heroes each), plus the catch-up ordering at the **end-of-Stage-II Auction**.
Because Nana naturally sits low (the gift wants her low, and she has cushions to survive there), she can
be **first or near-first at every one of these**, double-dipping a priority other commanders only reach
when they are losing badly. So the lose-early line is not a one-shot, it is a **standing posture** that
keeps paying at each reward stage as long as the cushions hold. Shape the HP curve so I am lowest right
*before* each Reward Selection, and recovered enough not to be bled out between them.

## Reading whether the lose-line is safe

The lose-early line is safe in proportion to how **cheap** the HP is and how **forgiving** the lobby is.
- **Push it hard** when: early (base DMG 3-6), the lobby is not hyper-spiking, and my cushions are intact.
- **Pull back** when: opponents already field 4-5 costs (early Mythic spikes mean their winning boards
  deal more Commander DMG per loss), my cushions are spent, or I am near the HP a single Stage-III/IV hit
  could finish.
- **The read:** *what does the strongest board in the lobby deal per win right now?* If that number,
  times the couple of losses I plan, crosses my remaining HP plus cushions, stop. HP is income only while
  it is cheap.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

### Stage I (1-4): lean into losing, it pays
- **I-1 (Creep round, no Commander DMG yet):** L3, 2 gold. Take the creep basic that fits the eventual
  carry (a **damage basic**, Retribution/Inspire, see the tier note, she does *not* want Aegis, which
  would reduce her gift income). Field a light board; the result is free (no DMG this round).
- **I-2 (Power Card):** **take Nana's Power** (chest + 8 gold + a one-time commander fatal-immunity):
  gold, gear, and a free life, all on-kit. The fatal-immunity is a perfect safety net for her low-HP
  line. Deviate only for a huge econ generic. ~L4.
- **I-3, I-4 (combat, base DMG 3):** **lose on purpose.** Field light, take the losses, fire the first
  one or two **gift triggers** (+8 each), build the loss streak. HP is cheapest now, so this is pure
  profit. ~L5 on the free EXP.
- **End of Stage I (Reward Selection, lowest-HP-first, 8 equipment + 3 heroes):** I am lowest HP, so I
  **pick first**, grab the best item for the carry or a key hero. The early losses bought me the first
  pick.

### Stage II (5-10): finish the harvest, then stabilize
- **II-1 (Blessing Shifter):** combine a 2-star for a free **+1 synergy count**; aim it at my committing
  synergy. Keep taking measured damage if the **3rd gift trigger** has not fired yet.
- **II-2, II-3 (combat / card+creep, base DMG 5-6):** land the **3rd gift trigger** (+8 gold + the
  Equipment Chest), then **stop losing on purpose** and stabilize a real board with the fat wallet. Take
  the II-3 card (an econ or synergy card) and the creep equipment.
- **II-4, II-5 (combat):** commit the comp, cross the first [4] breakpoint, start winning (I have more
  gold than the lobby and a banked chest).
- **End of Stage II (Auction):** I am **gold-rich** (gift + streak + interest), so **bid well** on a lot
  that completes the comp (a Synergy Magic Crystal, the carry's item, a 4-cost). Level to L7-8.

### Stage III (11-16): spike, cushion the carry
- **III-1 to III-3 (base DMG 7, card+creep+blessing at III-3):** spike the carry; **Molina's Blessing
  (survive-death) protects it**, so seat the cushion on the carry. Take the III-3 card + creep + re-point
  the Blessing onto a live breakpoint.
- **III-4 to III-6 (base DMG 11):** press round wins, build an HP lead, deal Commander DMG. The banked
  economy should have me ahead on board now.
- **End of Stage III (Reward Selection):** pick the carry's best item or a star-up.

### Stage IV (17+): finalize and close
- **IV-1 on (base DMG 13-15):** finalize; dump the bank into the last star-ups and items. The commander
  **fatal-immunity from her power card is a free extra life** here, where a single loss is huge, so it
  effectively buys one more round of survival in the closing fight. **IV-3** is a creep, take the carry's
  best-in-slot. Close before the lobby catches up on economy.

## Leveling cadence (concrete)

Gold-rich from the gift, so she can **level *and* roll**. Upgrade ladder from L3 (cumulative, minus 2
free EXP/round): L4:3, L5:11, L6:31, L7:63, L8:103, L9:157, L10:231.
- **Stage I:** ride free EXP to ~L5; do not spend gold on levels while harvesting (bank it).
- **Stage II:** push **L7-8** once stabilized, funded by the gift gold.
- **Stage III-IV:** **L8-10** for the carry's tier and capacity; hold at the carry's tier and roll the
  banked gold for star-ups.

## Best comps (concrete)

Flexible, the banked economy funds whatever the pool opens. Two natural fits:
- **A single keeper carry + Dauntless/Defender front.** Put the **survive-death cushion on the carry**
  (it gets a free revive), wall it with Dauntless (team shields) or Defender (DEF) to extend the
  tank-and-bank, and feed the carry the banked items. A forgiving, high-floor build.
- **Any synergy line the econ funds.** Once the wallet is fat, commit the strongest open axis (mage,
  marksman, a faction line) and spike it; Nana does not force a comp, she funds one.

## Positioning

Standard, with one Nana-specific note: **put Molina's Blessing (the survive-death cushion) on the
carry**, so the most valuable unit gets the free revive. Seat the carry **back-center, never a back
corner** (Swiftblade heroes dive the back-row side), corner-guard with a tank, and **spread** vs AoE and
the Dragoncaller dragon. The cushion buys the carry one death, not invulnerability, so still peel for it.
Full positioning: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## The cushions, used well

Two finite safety nets, and timing them is the crux of her skill:
- **Molina's Blessing (a hero survives first death at 1 HP):** put it on the **carry**, so the most
  valuable unit gets the free revive, and position so it does not waste the revive on a throwaway trade
  (a protected back-row carry that dies last extracts the most from it). Verify whether it auto-targets
  or is chosen; if chosen, it is the carry, always.
- **The commander fatal-immunity (Nana's Power, one-time):** most valuable **late**, where a single lost
  round deals 13-15 + stars and would otherwise eliminate me, so it effectively buys one extra closing
  round. Do **not** burn it mid-game by overshooting the lose-line; **save it as the last life for Stage
  IV**, a one-time second wind for the final fights, not an early cushion.
- Together they let Nana sit lower than anyone safely, *but only if I do not spend both early*. The
  discipline is keeping at least the fatal-immunity in reserve for the close.

## A worked line (one ideal game)

I-1 take Retribution off the creep, field light, ignore the result (no DMG). I-2 take Nana's Power (chest
+ 8 gold + fatal-immunity). I-3/I-4 lose on purpose, banking the first two gift triggers and a loss
streak. End of Stage I I am lowest HP, so I pick the best item first. Stage II: land the 3rd gift trigger
(+8 gold + a chest), then stop losing, stabilize, commit the open synergy, and win the auction with my
fat wallet. Stage III: seat the survive-death cushion on the carry, spike a [4]/[6], build an HP lead.
Stage IV: finalize, and the commander fatal-immunity buys one extra round in the closing fight. Result:
an economy lead built by getting *paid* to take the cheap damage everyone else feared, with two safety
nets that made the line low-risk.

## Tier lists (for Nana)

The tier list as it lives *here*, in Nana's form of life (the general [[../tier_lists]] is only the
upward aggregate and can be set aside when piloting her). In pencil:

**Synergies (for Nana):**
- **S:** **Dauntless** (team shields extend the tank-and-bank and keep her alive while harvesting),
  **Defender** (DEF front, same role); plus whatever single-carry line the econ funds.
- **A:** **Mage / Marksman** (a clean carry to bank into), **Scavenger / Neobeasts** (stack a second
  economy on hers), **Stargazer** (mana for a caster carry).
- **B:** most damage factions (fine, but they do not amplify her kit).
- **C / off-plan:** nothing she hates, she is flexible; just avoid spreading thin below all breakpoints.

**Heroes by phase (for Nana):**
- **Early:** cheap glue + a future carry seed (Gatotkaca, Cecilion, Tigreal). She does not need
  early power (she is losing on purpose), so prefer **cheap bodies + a keeper carry** over expensive
  early units.
- **Mid:** the committed carry (Kagura/Vexana/Karrie) + a Dauntless/Defender front (Franco, Hilda,
  Esmeralda, Tigreal).
- **Late:** the carry's capstone (Odette, Lesley, Ruby) + Claude (a second econ body).

**Go Go Cards (for Nana):**
- **S:** **economy** cards (Trust Fund, Payday, Vault Upgrade+) and **Counteraction** (when HP < 45, gain
  3 Equipment Chests, she is *often* below 45, so it nearly always fires), Fanny's Power (+1 capacity).
- **A:** Shield of Fortune (gold + a buffer), Synergy Badge/Blessing (a breakpoint), her exclusive when
  no better econ card shows.
- **B:** raw combat buffs (fine once stabilized, but econ is her engine).

**Equipment (for Nana):** standard, plus the chests from the gift and the card.
- **S:** Synergy Magic Crystal (breakpoint), Mirror Device (copy).
- **A:** the carry's role items (mage wands / ATK-speed), Enhanced over Basic; front survival pieces
  (Guardian Helmet, Antique Cuirass, Winter Crown) to extend the tank-and-bank.
- **Stage I basics (I-1 creep):** **inverted for Nana**, she *wants* to take damage (the gift), so
  **Aegis and Revitalize fall** (they reduce incoming damage = less gift income); take a **damage basic
  (Retribution/Inspire)** for the carry instead, and lean on the cushions rather than shields for
  survival.

## Power-card deep dive

The **I-2 offer always includes Nana's Power**. Her exclusive (chest + 8 gold + a one-time commander
fatal-immunity) is **on-kit and almost always kept**: gold and a chest feed her economy, and the
fatal-immunity is a tailor-made safety net for a commander who lives at low HP. Deviate only for a
standout econ generic (Trust Fund / Vault Upgrade+) if the run is pure-greed and survival is assured.
Later, prioritize **Counteraction** (3 chests when HP < 45, she qualifies almost always) and econ cards.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Cyclops** wants someone to die to snowball, and Nana sits at low HP, so he eyes her, *but* her
  cushions (survive-death + fatal-immunity + the 1-HP survival) make her **hard to actually finish**, so
  she is more resilient to him than her HP bar suggests. Still, do not overshoot into his payout.
- **Aggressive commanders (Lancelot, combat kits, Wanwan):** I **welcome** their early pressure, it
  literally pays my gift; their aggression is my income. Let them hit me cheaply.
- **Late out-scalers (value engines, Neobeasts):** if I bank too long they overtake me; convert the
  econ lead into board on time.
- **Aurora / Diggie (value engines):** a clash of econ styles, but I get *paid* for the early damage
  their patient boards merely absorb, so I can out-tempo their passivity by being lower-HP for the reward
  picks and richer from the gift.
- **Minotaur (the other lose-early commander):** we both want the lowest-HP slot and the gear lots, so we
  **contest the reward priority and the auction**; whoever is lower picks first, and my cushions let me
  push the HP line a touch further to win that race when it matters.
- **Alice / Aamon (spikes):** they out-board me mid-game with a spiked carry, so I must **convert my econ
  lead into a real board before their spike lands**, never bank into oblivion.

## Contingency and pivots

- **Overshot (HP critically low, cushions spent):** stop losing immediately, stabilize with the banked
  gold and chests; the economy lead is worthless if I am eliminated. The cushions are finite, respect
  them.
- **Gift maxed but board still weak:** I banked, now **spend**, roll and level into a real board; do not
  keep sitting low (the anti-greed correction, the gift pays nothing past 3 triggers).
- **Highroll a carry early:** I can deviate from the full lose-line, take *some* gift triggers, then
  ride the strong board; the line is a tool, not a mandate.
- **Aggressive lobby chunking me faster than expected:** harvest fewer triggers, value survival over the
  last +8 gold; a live Nana with 2 triggers beats a dead one with 3.

## The edge (the secret)

She is **paid to take the cheap early damage every other commander spends HP to avoid**, and she carries
**two survival cushions** (a hero survive-death + a one-time commander fatal-immunity) that make the
overshoot forgiving. The lose-early / tank-and-bank line is *strictly best* for her, it pays three ways
(gift + streak + reward priority), so **the lobby's aggression funds her economy** while the cushions
catch the misjudgments. No commander monetizes HP loss like Nana.

## Pitfalls

- **Overshooting** (dying despite the cushions, especially at Mythic where spikes hit hard).
- **Not stopping after the 3rd gift trigger**, further losses pay nothing and just risk elimination.
- **Greed**, banking so long the late out-scalers pass her; convert on time.
- **Taking Aegis/Revitalize** at the I-1 creep, defensive basics *reduce her gift income*; she wants
  damage basics and the cushions instead.

## Threat assessment (who beats her, and why)

Her losses come from **her own overshoot** more than from any single commander, misjudging how low is
too low and dying past the cushions, which **fast, hard-spiking lobbies** (Mythic) punish. **Late
out-scalers** beat her if she banks too long without converting. She is **strongest** in aggressive
lobbies (more incoming damage = more gold) and longer games where her economy compounds behind the
cushions; **weakest** when she misreads the overshoot or sits greedy. Her skill ceiling is entirely in
the how-low-is-too-low judgment.

- **Stop** the moment the 3rd trigger fires and the windows are banked; further losses pay nothing.
- **Cushions:** seat Molina's Blessing (survive-death) on the carry; the card's fatal-immunity is a free
  late life. They forgive overshoot but are finite.
- **I-2 card:** take **Nana's Power** (chest + gold + fatal-immunity); later prioritize **Counteraction**
  and econ cards.
- **Basics:** take **damage** basics, not Aegis/Revitalize (those cut her gift income).
- **Level:** L7-8 by Stage II once stabilized, L8-10 late; roll the banked gold.
- **Threats:** her own overshoot, Mythic spikes, late out-scalers. **Edge:** paid to be hit, with two
  safety nets, so the lobby's aggression funds her.
