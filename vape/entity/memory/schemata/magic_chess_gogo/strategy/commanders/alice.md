## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** single hyper-carry / contention-resistant vertical. She over-scales one chosen hero on
  partial-copy boosts the normal 3-merge rule would never allow.
- **Plays like:** pick THE carry early, pour every Abyssal Gaze cast, the +45% card, and all items into
  it, wall it, and let one unkillable threat win fights.
- **Difficulty:** medium-high. The kit is simple but the carry-choice is the highest-stakes, hardest-to-
  reverse decision in her game, and the all-eggs-one-basket board punishes bad positioning.
- **Best into:** boards that cannot reach her back-row carry (slow frontline mirrors, no assassins).
  **Worst into:** Swiftblade divers / burst / hard CC aimed at the one carry, and go-wide multi-carry
  boards that out-body a single threat.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Alice, "Queen of Blood." Skins: Queen of Blood, Wings of Eclipse. (There is no *hero* Alice
  in the S6 shop roster, so no commander/hero name collision.)
- **Abyssal Gaze** [Active] (verbatim): *"Gain 1 use in Rounds I-1/II-1/III-1. After using it on a Hero,
  they no longer require three identical Heroes to Star Up. Instead, obtaining an identical Hero
  increases the Hero's attributes, up to 8 times. 1-Star Hero: Each identical Hero grants 50% of its
  2-Star attribute boost. 2-Star Hero: Each identical Hero grants 8% of its 3-Star attribute boost."*
- **Crimson Grace** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Use the skill to obtain a 1-Star version of the
  selected Hero. The Hero can receive 4 additional attribute boosts, gaining 10% Hybrid ATK and 10% Max
  HP each time."*
- **Power Cards:** exclusive *Alice's Power* - *"Gain 5 EXP. Heroes selected by Alice's skill gain 45%
  extra Hybrid ATK."* **(Exclusive)**; borrowable *Alice's Power* - *"Obtain a 1-Star version of a
  selected Hero. Additional copies auto-combine, granting partial Star-Up bonuses. Select 3 time(s)."*

Reading (in pencil): Abyssal Gaze converts a chosen hero from **hard 3-merge star-ups** to **incremental
per-copy boosts** (up to 8). For a **1-star** target, each copy = 50% of its 2-star stat gap (so ~2
copies ~= 2-star stats); for a **2-star** target, each copy = 8% of its 3-star gap (8 copies = 64% of
the way to 3-star). Crimson Grace adds a **free 1-star of the chosen hero** plus **4 flat boosts of +10%
ATK / +10% HP**. Net: she builds **one hero whose stats can exceed a normal 3-star** from **far fewer
copies than the pool would ever yield**, especially decisive on a **4-5 cost** carry (where 9 copies is
impossible for anyone). She still *wants* copies; she just needs a handful, not nine.

## Win condition / identity

**One hyper-carry the lobby cannot assemble.** Pick the carry, funnel three casts of Abyssal Gaze + the
+45% Hybrid ATK exclusive + all damage items + every copy I can find into it, protect it, and let it win
fights nearly single-handed. Her entire game is the **carry-choice decision** and then **protecting the
investment**. Because she scales a high-cost carry off partial copies, she fields an effective
3-star-plus 4-5 cost that rivals fighting the pool fairly cannot match.

## The core tension (every round)

Abyssal Gaze has **only three casts** (I-1, II-1, III-1) and the boosts cap at 8 per copy + 4 flat, so
**which hero I invest in is the highest-stakes, least-reversible choice in her game**. Concentrate every
boost on **one keeper carry that scales and stays relevant all match** (a 4-5 cost that does not fall
off), never a 1-cost that spikes round 3 and dies in relevance. Spreading casts or copies across heroes
dilutes the one thing that makes her strong. Secondary tension: she **does still chase copies** of the
chosen carry (each is a boost), so she rerolls like a vertical, just one that converts partial copies
into power instead of needing the full 9.

## The carry-investment plan (her signature loop, concrete)

The ordered decision for the three casts:
1. **I-1 (cast 1):** ideally on the **intended keeper carry** even at 1-star (each future copy = 50% of
   the 2-star gap, so early investment compounds). If I genuinely do not have the keeper yet, boost the
   strongest unit I will *keep*, not a throwaway 1-cost for round-3 tempo.
2. **II-1 (cast 2):** the keeper should be locked; pour it in. By now copies have been accruing partial
   boosts, so the carry is becoming the single strongest unit in the lobby.
3. **III-1 (cast 3):** the carry reaches its ceiling. From here the damage is *solved*; the game becomes
   **keeping it alive** (position + peel + a wall).
4. **Throughout:** buy every copy of the chosen carry I see (each one is a boost up to 8); reroll at the
   carry's tier to find them. Crimson Grace's free 1-star is a head start, not the whole plan.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): pick the carry, build its shell
- **I-1 (creep + first Abyssal Gaze):** L3, 2 gold. The tension is immediate: cast on the keeper carry
  if I have it (even 1-star), else on the strongest keeper unit. Creep: a damage basic (Inspire/
  Retribution) earmarked for the carry. Spend buys on the **frontline and board around** the carry, I do
  not need 9 of the carry, but I do want its copies as they appear.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take the exclusive** (+5 EXP, +45% Hybrid ATK to Alice-selected heroes): it
  buffs the exact carry the kit is pumping and the +5 EXP speeds capacity. Pure on-kit. (The borrowable
  Alice card is a weaker generic partial-star-up; prefer the exclusive.)
- **I-3 / I-4:** buy carry copies as they show (partial boosts), build the wall, hold gold toward the
  band.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection** (lowest-HP-first): grab the carry's best-in-slot item or a copy
  of the carry; do not need synergy-copy lots for *other* heroes.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): the carry becomes the lobby's strongest unit
- **II-1 (Blessing + second Abyssal Gaze):** pour the cast into the locked carry; combine a 2-star
  elsewhere for a free +1 synergy count to a near breakpoint.
- **Commit the shell:** the carry's class/faction gives the board its [4]; the rest exists to keep the
  carry alive and amplify it. Reroll at the carry's tier for more copies (boosts).
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a carry-item bundle (The Hunt / Box of Holy Objects) or a synergy
  payoff.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for the **carry's best-in-slot Enhanced item** or a wall piece. I do
  **not** need hero-copy lots for the carry as desperately as a normal vertical (partial copies + the
  kit cover me), but a copy of the carry is still a boost worth a modest bid. Prioritize gear and
  breakpoints.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): cap the carry, pivot to protection
- **III-1 (third Abyssal Gaze):** final cast; the carry hits its ceiling. **The damage is now solved.**
- **Spike the support:** the front, the synergy [6], the peel. The game from here is positioning and
  keeping the one carry alive (base DMG 7-11, HP expensive).
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, grab anti-dive tools (a CC body, a Purify
  for the carry).

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): protect and close
- Finalize the protection shell; the carry should out-scale everything. Close before a focused enemy
  board (assassins, CC) can neutralize the single threat. All eggs are in one basket, so the basket must
  be guarded.

## Leveling cadence

Steadier than a pure roll-down vertical, because she does not need the full 9 copies, but she **does
reroll** at the carry's tier to find boost-copies, so she is not as level-passive as Alice's v1
implied. Level for **capacity** (more wall + support around the carry) and to reach the **carry's tier**
(L8-9 for a 4-cost, L9-10 for a 5-cost), then roll for copies. The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3:
`L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. The +5 EXP from her exclusive helps
the climb. Do not over-level past the carry's tier (dilutes the copies she still wants).

## Best comps (concrete)

Built around **one expensive, scaling carry** with a **fat protective front** and a support synergy:
- **A 4-5 cost Mage carry:** Odette (5c Astro/Mage) or Kagura (4c Mage/Exorcist) pumped to absurd, with
  a Mage [4] shell + Stargazer mana, fronted by Defender/Dauntless [2].
- **A 4-5 cost Marksman carry:** Karrie/Irithel (4c) or Lesley (5c), with a wall and peel.
- **A tanky carry:** Esmeralda (4c Dauntless/Heartbond) or Ruby (5c) if I want the carry to also help
  hold the front, the most forgiving since the carry is harder to delete.
- The synergy is whatever the carry brings; the board exists to keep it alive.

## Positioning

The most positioning-dependent commander in the roster, because the whole board is one unit. Seat the
carry **back-center, never naked in a corner** (Swiftblade divers, Saber/Helcurt/Gusion/Ling, jump the
back-row side straight onto a cornered carry). **Corner-guard** with a tank or sacrificial body so the
dive lands on the wall. Add a **peeler** (CC/burst) near the landing spot to punish the diver. Spread
the rest vs AoE. If the carry self-peels (a Phasewarper), it tolerates exposure better. Full detail:
[[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## A worked line (one ideal game)

I-1 L3: I have a 1-star Odette in shop, buy it and cast Abyssal Gaze on it immediately (each future copy
= 50% of its 2-star gap). Take Inspire off the creep for it. I-2: take her exclusive (+5 EXP, +45% ATK
on Odette). Stage I-II: I buy every Odette copy I see (3-4 of them across the game, each a partial
boost), and Crimson Grace's free 1-star plus the 4 flat boosts stack on top. II-1: second cast, Odette
is now the strongest unit at the table. I wall with a Defender [2] and a Stargazer mana pair. Auction: I
win Odette's best-in-slot Glowing Wand (Enhanced). III-1: third cast caps her; I pivot fully to
protection, corner-guard against the lobby's Ling, add a peeler. III-IV: Odette deletes back lines while
the wall holds; nobody else could ever assemble a unit this statted. 1st, unless someone dives through
my peel.

## Power-card deep dive

The exclusive is a textbook "amplify my kit" card (**+45% Hybrid ATK straight onto the pumped carry**,
plus +5 EXP) and should almost always be kept. The **borrowable Alice card** (a 1-star copy, select 3x,
auto-combining for partial star-ups) is a weaker, generic echo of her own kit, prefer the exclusive,
which buffs the carry the kit already builds rather than duplicating the partial-star-up she already
does for free. Refresh past anything else; on Alice the exclusive is near-mandatory.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Her carry is contention-resistant, not immune:** rivals **can** slow her by buying the carry's
  copies (each denied copy is a denied boost), so a rich rival contesting the carry hero hurts more than
  the v1 claimed. But she needs *few* copies, so she usually wins that race; the bigger threat is the
  board.
- **Assassinate / neutralize:** the standard answer to a one-carry board, expect **Swiftblade divers**
  aimed at the carry and **CC/AoE** to lock it. **Kagura** (the commander) hard-counters dives with her
  shield; **Lukas** CC and Ling dives threaten Alice's carry directly.
- **Go-wide / multi-carry boards** can out-body her single threat or trade it away.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **Wrong carry pick (falling off / getting countered):** painful but recoverable, **re-aim the later
  Abyssal Gaze casts and items at a better keeper if I catch it by II-1**; the longer I wait, the more
  sunk the investment. The free Crimson Grace copy and partial boosts soften a re-pick.
- **Carry getting dove every fight:** the fix is **positioning and a peeler, not more damage**, re-seat
  back-center, corner-guard, add CC/Purify. (See [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].)
- **Carry copies contested:** I need few, so usually fine; if truly starved, lean harder on the flat
  Crimson Grace boosts + items and keep what partial boosts I have.
- **Behind early:** her power is back-loaded into the carry, so survive with the wall and let the carry
  come online; do not panic-spread the casts.

## The edge (the secret)

**A hyper-statted carry the shared pool would never let anyone assemble.** By converting hard 3-merges
into partial per-copy boosts (+ a free base copy + 4 flat boosts), Alice scales a **4-5 cost** carry
past a normal 3-star's stats from a *handful* of copies instead of the impossible nine. She is the
roster's cleanest single-carry engine, **resistant** (not immune) to the game's core denial mechanic.
Her whole skill is choosing the carry right and keeping it alive.

## Pitfalls

- **Investing in a fall-off hero** (a cheap unit for early tempo) instead of a lasting 4-5 cost carry.
- **Spreading casts or copies** across multiple heroes, diluting the one advantage.
- **Believing the v1 "needs no copies":** she *wants* the carry's copies (each is a boost), so reroll for
  them at the carry's tier; do not ignore the pool.
- **Under-protecting the single carry:** all eggs in one basket, so a dove or CC-locked carry is a lost
  board. Positioning and peel are not optional for her.

## Threat assessment (who beats her, and why)

**Assassins and burst** (Swiftblade lines, the commander Ling, anything that reaches and kills the
back-row carry) are her nightmare: neutralize the one carry and her board folds. **Hard CC** (Lukas
stun, control) that locks the carry for a fight does the same, alive but stunned = no damage.
**Go-wide / multi-carry** boards out-body her single threat. A **rich rival contesting her carry's
copies** can slow her scaling (the corrected, non-immune truth). She is strongest into boards that
cannot reach her carry and weakest into any that can; **protecting the investment is the entire
matchup.**

## Numbers that matter (the carry math, quantified)

- **Per-copy boost (Abyssal Gaze, up to 8):** on a **1-star** target, each identical copy = **50% of its
  2-star stat gap** (so ~2 copies ~= full 2-star stats); on a **2-star** target, each copy = **8% of its
  3-star gap** (8 copies = 64% of the way to 3-star). Plus **Crimson Grace**: a **free 1-star** of the
  carry + **4 flat boosts of +10% Hybrid ATK / +10% Max HP**. Net: a handful of copies + the flats puts
  one carry **past a normal 3-star's stats**, on a 4-5 cost the pool would never let anyone 3-star.
- **The exclusive on top:** *+45% Hybrid ATK* straight onto that same carry (+5 EXP for capacity). It
  stacks with the boosts, so the carry's effective ATK dwarfs the lobby's.
- **Why protection is the rest of the math:** all the power is in one unit, so a Swiftblade dive or a
  stun that removes Odette for a fight removes ~all my damage. Corner-guard + a peeler + Purify are
  worth more than another damage item once the carry is capped.
