## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** duplicate / cost-ladder value engine. He eats cheap 1-stars and returns higher-cost
  heroes, climbing toward 4-5 costs the shop would deny.
- **Plays like:** a patient upgrader, keep cheap fodder coming, devour on cadence to ratchet up the cost
  ladder, and cash the every-other-devour 2-hero synergy bonus.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is a paced active (every 3 rounds) plus a fodder-supply habit; the
  skill is timing devours toward a target tier and steering the synergy bonus.
- **Best into:** long, gold-fed games where the ladder climbs to 4-5 cost power. **Worst into:** fast
  aggro that ends before the climb pays, and a contested pool that starves the higher-quality returns.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Dyrroth, "Prince of the Abyss." Skins: Prince of the Abyss, Scalebore. (Note: the
  **commander** Dyrroth is the player avatar, distinct from the **hero** Dyrroth, a 1-cost Bruiser/Kishin
  unit in [[../../infos/heroes/cost_1]]. This file is the commander.)
- **Abyssal Devourer** [Active] (verbatim): *"From Round 1-4, Dyrroth can devour a 1-Star Hero every 3
  round(s) and return a Hero with higher quality (Gold cost +1, max 5-Gold)."*
- **Abyssal Favor** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Dyrroth gains 1 Commander EXP per devour. After
  every 2 devour(s), the next devour grants 2 Heroes, each sharing one Synergy as the devoured one. The 2
  Heroes are randomly selected from the Synergies of the devoured Hero, preferably 1 Role Synergy + 1
  Faction Synergy. If no such Heroes are available (sold out in the Shared Hero Pool or no higher quality
  Hero is available from the Synergy), grant higher quality Heroes from random Synergy instead (Gold cost
  +1, max 5-Gold)."*
- **Power Card:** *Dyrroth's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Every 3 rounds, devour a 1-Star
  Hero from your slot for a random Hero of +1 Gold cost (up to 5-Gold). Each devour grants 2 EXP."*

Reading (in pencil): he **trades a cheap 1-star for a higher-cost hero** (cost +1, capped at 5-Gold) on a
**every-3-rounds** cadence, so over a game he ratchets cheap fodder up into 4-5 cost heroes the shop
rarely hands out. The passive adds **+1 Commander EXP per devour** (a slow leveling assist) and, **every
2nd devour, returns 2 heroes** sharing the devoured one's synergies (a synergy-builder). The card stacks
**+2 EXP per devour** on top. He is a **value/upgrade engine** that bypasses the shop's high-tier odds.

## Win condition / identity

**Climb the cost ladder for free, then carry on the 4-5 cost heroes the ladder produced.** By feeding
cheap 1-stars he did not need, Dyrroth converts shop chaff into high-cost bodies and a built synergy
(the every-2nd-devour 2-hero bonus), reaching power the pool's odds would deny a normal commander. He is
a **draw-assisting value commander**: not a spike, a steady upgrade that compounds into a strong late
board if the game goes long enough to let the ladder climb.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Feed the devourer (the fodder supply).** The active needs **1-star fodder** every 3 rounds, so I
   should always keep cheap 1-stars on hand to eat, never starve the cadence. Buying a cheap body purely
   as devour-food is correct.
2. **Steer the synergy bonus.** Every 2nd devour returns 2 heroes of the devoured one's synergies, so
   **devour heroes whose synergies I am building** to aim that bonus at my axis (it prefers 1 role + 1
   faction). Devouring random off-axis heroes wastes the bonus.

## The devour cadence (his signature, concrete)

The ordered plan for the engine:
1. **Always hold cheap 1-star fodder** to feed the every-3-rounds devour, never miss a cadence (each
   missed devour is lost value + EXP).
2. **Devour toward a target tier:** to reach a 4-cost carry, devour a 3-cost (returns a 4-cost); to
   reach a 5-cost, devour a 4-cost. Climb deliberately, not randomly.
3. **Time the every-2nd-devour bonus** onto a hero of **my committed synergy** so the 2 returned heroes
   build my axis (1 role + 1 faction preferred).
4. **Bank the EXP** (+1 kit, +2 card per devour) toward a level when I am close.
5. **Keep the returned high-cost heroes** that fit; sell or re-feed the ones that do not.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): start feeding
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Build a normal early board and **keep cheap 1-star fodder** for the first
  devour (the active opens from Round I-4). Creep: a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take his card** (+2 EXP per devour on top of the kit's devour + EXP): it
  amplifies exactly the loop I am running and the EXP compounds the leveling. Keep it.
- **I-4 (first devour):** devour a cheap 1-star toward my intended tier; bank the EXP.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero that opens my line / feeds the ladder.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): climb and build the synergy
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit a synergy axis.
- **Devour on cadence** (every 3 rounds) toward a 4-cost carry, and **time the 2-hero bonus** onto my
  axis. Cross the first breakpoints with the returned heroes.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a card matching my axis.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for a carry item or a Synergy Magic Crystal; I generate bodies, so I
  rarely need hero lots.
- **Leveling:** the devour EXP assists; level for capacity + the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): cash the high-cost board
- **The ladder has climbed:** I should be fielding 4-cost (and reaching 5-cost) heroes the pool denied
  others, plus a built synergy from the 2-hero bonuses. Spike: star up what I can, take a payoff card.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, more carry gear.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the high-cost board the devourer produced; the EXP lead may have me at capacity early. Close
  before HP is the binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

The devour EXP (+1 kit, +2 card = up to +3 per devour) is a **passive leveling assist**, so he climbs
the ladder a touch faster than a normal commander on top of the 2 free EXP/round. The upgrade ladder,
cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Level to the
target carry's tier and for capacity to field the high-cost heroes he produces; the engine partly funds
the climb.

## Best comps (concrete)

Flexible, built around the **high-cost heroes the ladder produces** and the **synergy the 2-hero bonus
builds**:
- **A 4-5 cost carry line** reached by devouring up (e.g. climb to Kagura/Odette for a mage core, or
  Karrie/Lesley for marksman), since he accesses high costs the shop would deny.
- **A committed faction/class** fed by the every-2nd-devour bonus, devour heroes of that axis so the 2
  returned heroes thicken it (prefers 1 role + 1 faction).
- The hero **Dyrroth** (1c Bruiser/Kishin) is cheap fodder *or* a Bruiser/Kishin body if those are my
  axis.

## Positioning

Standard for whatever board the ladder produces: carries back-center (never cornered vs Swiftblade
divers), wall front, spread vs AoE. Nothing Dyrroth-specific, his edge is in the build (the high-cost
bodies), not the board shape. Full detail: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## Power-card deep dive

His card (+2 EXP per devour, devour for a +1-cost hero) is **on-kit**, it stacks EXP onto the exact loop
he runs and accelerates the level climb that lets him field the high-cost heroes the ladder produces.
**Keep it.** Deviate only if an EXP/level or synergy-breakpoint card clearly wins more for my specific
axis. Use the 2 refreshes to confirm.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fast aggro / burst** (Lancelot, Ling, combat kits): they end the game before my ladder climbs to 4-5
  cost power, protect HP early and devour efficiently to speed the climb.
- **Contested pool:** the higher-quality returns can be **starved** (the kit notes "sold out in the
  Shared Hero Pool" falls back to random synergy), so a heavily-contested axis weakens my returns, prefer
  an open one.
- **Cyclops** wants someone to die early; do not be his first kill with a slow early board.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **No fodder (cadence about to be wasted):** buy a cheap 1-star purely to feed the devour, never miss a
  cadence, the value/EXP is worth a coin.
- **Returns off-axis:** the bonus prefers the devoured hero's synergies, so **devour on-axis heroes**; if
  the pool starves the return, accept the random higher-cost body and slot or re-feed it.
- **Behind early:** the kit is back-loaded into the ladder, so survive with a normal board and let the
  climb pay; do not panic.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** stop devouring for value, lock the high-cost board you have and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**He manufactures the high-cost heroes the shop's odds would deny.** Feeding chaff he did not need,
Dyrroth ratchets up to 4-5 cost carries and a built synergy for free, on a steady cadence, while the
devour EXP keeps him a level ahead. A draw-independent value engine whose late board is stronger than its
gold should buy, if the game lasts long enough to climb.

## Pitfalls

- **Starving the cadence** (no fodder = a missed devour = lost value + EXP).
- **Devouring off-axis** (wastes the every-2nd-devour 2-hero synergy bonus).
- **Climbing aimlessly** (devour *toward a target tier/carry*, not randomly).
- **Over-relying on the ladder vs fast lobbies** (it is back-loaded; protect HP while it climbs).

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the ladder pays (his power is back-loaded into the climb). A
**heavily-contested pool** starves the higher-quality returns. He is strongest in **long, gold-fed**
games where the ladder reaches 4-5 cost power and the synergy bonus builds; weakest in fast ones where
the climb never finished. A mid-to-late commander whose value compounds with game length.

## Numbers that matter (the ladder, quantified)

- **The trade:** 1 cheap **1-star** -> a hero of **+1 Gold cost** (capped at 5-Gold), every **3 rounds**.
  Over a long game that is several free up-tiers, reaching 4-5 cost heroes the shop's odds (4-cost peaks
  ~26% at L9, 5-cost ~25% at L10) would rarely hand out.
- **The synergy bonus:** every **2nd** devour returns **2 heroes** of the devoured hero's synergies
  (prefers 1 role + 1 faction), so half my devours thicken my axis for free.
- **The EXP assist:** **+1** (kit) **+2** (card) = up to **+3 EXP per devour**, on top of the 2 free/round,
  a steady leveling lead that lets him field the high-cost bodies sooner.

## The devour schedule (round by round, worked)

The active fires every 3 rounds from Round I-4, and the **every-2nd-devour bonus** lands on devours **3,
6, 9** ("after every 2 devours, the next grants 2 heroes"). So plan the bonus devours onto my axis. A
worked cadence (rounds approximate; in pencil, verify the exact timing in play):
- **Devour 1 (~I-4):** eat a spare **1-cost**, return a **2-cost**. Start the ladder; bank the EXP.
- **Devour 2 (~r7):** eat a **2-cost on my axis**, return a **3-cost**. This is the 2nd devour, so the
  next one is the bonus, set it up by having an on-axis 1-star ready.
- **Devour 3 (~r10, BONUS):** eat a **3-cost on my committed axis**, return a **4-cost** *and* **2 heroes
  of that axis** (prefers 1 role + 1 faction). The single highest-value devour, it both up-tiers and
  thickens my synergy. Aim it deliberately.
- **Devour 4 (~r13):** eat toward a **5-cost** (devour a 4-cost), or up-tier a second carry.
- **Devour 5 (~r16):** another up-tier or fodder-to-4-cost.
- **Devour 6 (~r19, BONUS):** again on my axis, 2 more synergy heroes to finish a [4]/[6].
The rule the schedule encodes: **bonus devours (3, 6) go on my axis; filler devours (1, 2, 4, 5) climb
the cost ladder.** Always keep the right 1-star fodder staged for the next devour.

## Stage checkpoints (am I on pace?)

- **End of Stage I (~L5):** fodder on hand, first devour done (I-4), his card taken, EXP banking. *Behind
  = no fodder? Buy a cheap body to feed; never miss the cadence.*
- **End of Stage II (~L7-8):** ladder climbing toward a 4-cost carry, the 2-hero bonus thickening my axis,
  EXP a level ahead. *Behind = returns off-axis? Devour on-axis heroes.*
- **Stage III (~L9):** fielding 4-cost (reaching 5-cost) heroes from the ladder, a built synergy. *Behind
  = ladder stalled? Check the cadence and feed it.*
- **Stage IV (L10):** high-cost board locked, closing.

## The 7-archetype read (who I face, and the adjustment)

- vs **fast aggro / combat** (Lancelot, Ling, Lukas): they end it before my ladder pays, protect HP and
  devour efficiently. My hardest matchup.
- vs **vertical / copy** (Alice, Aamon, Paquito): we both manufacture power the pool denies; mine climbs
  cost, theirs copies, little direct contention unless we share a carry.
- vs **econ / value** (Aurora, Diggie, Chou): a value race; my ladder out-scales them late if the game
  lasts.
- vs **synergy grant** (Gusion, Lunox): they buy counts, I build a synergy via the devour bonus; if we
  share an axis the pool tightens (and starves my returns).
- vs **free-hero / shop** (Guinevere, Harley, Vale): they beat the wide pool; my returns can be starved by
  a contested axis, so prefer an open one.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong, Karina): they want gear, I want bodies/tiers, little overlap.
- vs **combat** (Kagura, Wanwan): their board is the threat; scout and tech position, survive to my
  late board.

## Fodder sourcing (where the 1-stars come from)

The engine starves without cheap 1-star fodder, so know the supply lines:
- **The cheap shop tiers** early: at L3-5 the shop is 50-60% 1-Gold ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/hero]]),
  so 1-star fodder is abundant in Stage I, the natural time to start the ladder.
- **The off-axis returns:** the every-2nd-devour bonus hands 2 heroes; the one I do not want becomes the
  *next* devour's fodder (the ladder partly feeds itself).
- **Bench leftovers:** any 1-star I bought but did not need (a bricked pair, an off-axis body) is food,
  not a sell, devour it for the up-tier + EXP instead of selling for a coin.
- **A deliberate cheap buy:** if I have no fodder when the cadence comes up, **buy a 1-Gold body purely
  to feed it**, the up-tier + 3 EXP is worth more than the gold spent. Never miss a devour for lack of
  food.

The discipline: I should *always* have at least one expendable 1-star staged for the next devour, and
ideally an **on-axis** one for the bonus devours (3, 6, 9). A starved cadence is the single biggest way
to underplay him.

## The leveling math (why he is a level ahead)

Free EXP per round is 2; Dyrroth adds **+1 (kit) +2 (card) per devour**. With a devour every ~3 rounds,
that is roughly **+1 extra EXP/round averaged**, modest but compounding: over a game it pulls him a level
or so ahead, which is *why* he can field the 4-5 cost heroes the ladder produces (capacity + odds arrive
sooner). The upgrade cost from L3 is cumulative `3 / 11 / 31 / 63 / 103 / 157 / 231`, so the devour EXP
shaves a meaningful chunk off reaching L8-9, exactly the tiers his returns need to be deployable.
