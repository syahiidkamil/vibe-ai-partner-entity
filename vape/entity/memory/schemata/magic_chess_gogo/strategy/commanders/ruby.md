## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** equipment / sticky front-carry. She starts with two strong fixed items and a board that
  refuses to stay dead.
- **Plays like:** the gear-ahead bruiser who funnels a marked-target burst onto one front-line carry and
  resurrects it mid-fight, low-variance and forgiving.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is two free items plus one pairing decision (who holds Cloak, who holds
  Scythe); the floor is high because she never bricks and the front never truly folds.
- **Best into:** gear-light lobbies and burst boards (the resurrect blanks an assassination). **Worst
  into:** other equipment commanders contesting gear, heavy true-damage / anti-heal, and go-wide boards
  that win without needing to kill her sticky front.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Ruby, "Little Red Hood." Skins: Little Red Hood, Verdant Huntress. (Note: the **commander**
  Ruby is the player avatar, distinct from the **hero** Ruby, a 5-cost Dauntless/Exorcist front-carry in
  [[../../infos/heroes/cost_5]], who is also a natural Cloak carrier on this commander.)
- **Red Hood's Rage** [Passive] (verbatim): *"Obtain 2 pieces of Equipment: Cloak and Scythe. Cloak: The
  Cloak carrier's Basic ATKs apply a Rage Mark to the current target. When the marked enemy dies, the
  carrier restores 10% HP. (Only 1 Rage Mark can exist at a time. It transfers to a new target when the
  carrier switches targets.) Scythe: Deal 280% Physical DMG based on Adaptive ATK to the enemy with the
  Rage Mark every 2s."*
- **Life from Death** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Cloak and Scythe both gain additional Passive
  effects: Cloak: Upon death, the Cloak carrier resurrects with 30% HP (once per round). Scythe: After
  the Cloak carrier resurrects, the Scythe carrier gains 55% Hybrid ATK."*
- **Power Cards:** exclusive *Ruby's Power* - *"Gain 2 Gold for each survived Cloak or Scythe carrier when
  battle ends. Max Gold: 35."* **(Exclusive)**; borrowable *Ruby's Power* - *"Obtain Raging Scythe (deals
  DMG at intervals) and Raging Cloak (restore HP & resurrect)."*

**Equipment the kit grants (the detail, from [[../../infos/equipments/commander_exclusive]]):** she
starts with a **Cloak** and a **Scythe**, which exist in 1-Star and 2-Star versions (the version scales
with her commander star), each with a Basic and Enhanced stat block:
- **Crimson Cloak** (1-Star): **+20% Max HP** (*Enh: +40%*). *Mastery:* Basic ATKs apply a Rage Mark;
  when the marked enemy dies, restore 10% HP.
- **Raging Cloak** (2-Star): **+20% Max HP** (*Enh: +40%*). *Mastery* + *Life from Death:* on death,
  resurrect with **30% HP** (once per round).
- **Hunting Scythe** (1-Star): **+20% Hybrid ATK** (*Enh: +40%*). *Hunt:* every 2s deal **280% Adaptive
  Physical DMG** to the Rage-Marked enemy.
- **Raging Scythe** (2-Star): **+20% Hybrid ATK** (*Enh: +40%*). *Hunt* + *Life from Death:* after the
  Cloak carrier resurrects, the Scythe carrier gains **+55% Hybrid ATK**.

Reading (in pencil): she is **gear-ahead from round 1** (two real items free) and her two items
**combine**: the Cloak marks a target, the Scythe punishes that marked target for a big repeating burst,
and at 2-star the Cloak resurrects its carrier (blanking a kill) which then *powers up* the Scythe (+55%
ATK). The exclusive pays her for keeping those two carriers alive, which her own resurrect makes easy.
The borrowable is **redundant** (it just hands the same Cloak + Scythe the kit already gives), so it is
the wrong pick on Ruby herself.

Research notes (2026-07-10, `../../research/ruby/`, two transcripts): the community describes the
revive-buff at **80% Hybrid ATK** where the datamine reads 55%, a possible live buff, verify in-game.
The lived doctrine on top of the kit:

  and for that exact reason **heavily contested**, expect another Ruby on your line). Gusion seats
  **center-back** so his knives reach both flanks, holds every damage item, and the community calls

- **The patience twin: Neobeast Ruby.** Her free items make her the roster's easiest Neobeast
  holder (the greedy line that knocks out impatient pilots); concede to the Go Go Box for the

- **Split the Cloak and Scythe onto two sturdy carriers** once the front holds: the exclusive pays
  per SURVIVING carrier, so two live carriers = +4g/round (confirmed in play: "separate the items

- Two field notes in pencil: **the Go Go Auction hands more gold to lower placements** (last seat

  Dragoncaller board, spread your units**, the dragon strikes the densest 9-tile area.

## Win condition / identity

**Field the stickiest, gear-ahead front-carry in the lobby and grind the enemy down on the marked-target
burst.** Her two free items mean she is ahead on equipment immediately, and the Cloak->Scythe loop turns
one front unit into a self-healing, target-deleting threat that resurrects when killed. She is a
**placement-first, low-variance** commander like Minotaur: the gear is the plan, nothing bricks, and the
floor is high because the board does not stay dead. The exclusive then converts her natural stickiness
into a steady gold trickle.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing decisions:
1. **Who carries Cloak, who carries Scythe.** The Cloak wants a **durable front unit that attacks**
   (it marks via basic ATKs and heals on the marked kill, and it is the one that resurrects), the hero
   Ruby herself, a bruiser, or a Dauntless front. The Scythe wants the **highest Adaptive-ATK unit**
   (the 280% scales with it). Two coherent builds: **both on one front-carry** (simplest, a bruiser that
   marks and punishes itself), or **Cloak on the front tank, Scythe on the back-row carry** (the tank
   marks, the carry's Scythe deletes the marked target). Pick by which carry I have.
2. **Protecting the two carriers (the exclusive's gold).** Up to **+35 gold** for surviving Cloak/Scythe
   carriers, and the resurrect helps, so positioning to keep both alive pays twice (board strength + the
   econ).

## The Cloak + Scythe pairing (her signature, concrete)

The ordered plan for the two free items:
1. **Equip the Cloak on a front unit that auto-attacks** (so it marks reliably) and survives (so it
   triggers the resurrect). The hero Ruby (5c, taunts + tanky) is the ideal carrier; else a bruiser or
   a Dauntless front.
2. **Equip the Scythe on the unit with the highest Adaptive Physical ATK** (the 280%/2s scales with it).
   Often the same unit early (one front-carry), or the dedicated back-row carry mid-late.
3. **Keep the Rage Mark flowing:** only one mark exists at a time and it follows the Cloak carrier's
   target, so the Cloak carrier should be hitting the unit I want deleted (front-to-front it marks the
   enemy tank; if I want the enemy carry marked, I need the Cloak carrier reaching it, harder, so the
   mark usually lands on the nearest enemy, which is fine, the Scythe burst plus the heal-on-kill is the
   value).
4. **Enhance the items** when offered (Enhanced doubles the stat: +40% Max HP / +40% Hybrid ATK), and
   **chase the 2-star versions** (her commander star-up) for the resurrect + the +55% Scythe ATK, the
   real power jump.
5. **Stack more gear** on the carry on top of the free two (she is an equipment commander, so funnel
   normal drops onto the Cloak/Scythe carrier).

## The HP economy (sticky, not lose-early)

Ruby is **not** a deliberate-lose commander; her front is *sticky* (the resurrect + the Cloak heal), so
she should **win rounds and protect HP**, leaning on the resurrect to survive burst that would kill a
normal board. She only takes cheap early losses if a genuine lowroll leaves her unable to win (then lose
*cheap* Stage I-II HP, base DMG 3-6, same as anyone). Her edge is durability, so she banks a clean win
streak behind the sticky front rather than farming the lowest-HP reward priority.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage (HP cost of a loss): **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15**
([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]). Calendar from [[../../infos/rounds/index]].

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): gear-ahead from round 1
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. I already hold Cloak + Scythe, so I am gear-ahead immediately. Equip both
  on my best early front unit (one carry to start). Buy toward a Dauntless/Bruiser front. Creep pick: a
  **damage or Adaptive-ATK basic** for the Scythe carrier, or Aegis to harden the front.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take the exclusive** (+2 gold per surviving Cloak/Scythe carrier, max 35), NOT
  the borrowable (which redundantly re-grants the items I already have). The exclusive monetizes my
  natural stickiness; keep it. (Deviate only if I genuinely will not keep both carriers alive, then a
  generic econ/synergy card.)
- **I-3 / I-4:** build the front, hold gold toward the interest band. The sticky front should be winning
  early rounds (gear advantage).
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** grab an **Enhanced** item for the Cloak/Scythe carrier or a
  front piece; I am usually not lowest HP (sticky board), so I pick later.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): split or stack, cross breakpoints
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a free +1 count, place on a near breakpoint (Dauntless
  or Exorcist if I am leaning those).
- **Decide the build:** keep both items on one front-carry, or split Cloak (front tank) / Scythe
  (back-row carry). Cross a frontline [2]/[4] (Dauntless/Defender) and a carry-class [2].
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** an item bundle (The Hunt / Box of Holy Objects) to stack the carry, or a
  synergy payoff.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for **Enhanced damage/HP items** for the carriers and a Synergy
  Magic Crystal. As an equipment commander I value gear lots; the exclusive's gold helps fund bids.
- **Leveling:** to the carry's tier (L7-8).

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): hit the 2-star items, spike
- **Chase the 2-Star Cloak + Scythe** (her commander star-up): the **resurrect** + **+55% Scythe ATK**
  is the real spike, the front now blanks a kill and the Scythe burst jumps. Enhance both.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, grab more carry gear.
- Press round wins on the sticky, gear-ahead board (HP now expensive, base 7-11).

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Items maxed (Enhanced 2-star Cloak + Scythe + stacked drops on the carry). The resurrect makes her
  board outlast burst in the Battle-Acceleration window. Close before HP is the binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Standard for a front-carry comp: level for capacity and the carry's tier (L7-8 for a 3-4 cost carry),
reroll for the carry's star-up and front bodies. The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3:
`L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. The kit's gear lead means her board
is strong per-level, so she can level steadily on the interest band rather than rushing; the exclusive's
gold cushions it.

## Best comps (concrete)

Built around a **sticky front-carry holding the Cloak + Scythe**; the researched meta lines first:
- **6 Dragoncaller + 4 Swiftblade, Gusion hyper (the community default).** Gusion center-back with
  the damage items, dragons striking the enemy clump, Ling/Hanzo/Saber filling Swiftblade; the
  strongest push line and the most contested one, scout for the mirror Ruby before committing.
- **Neobeast Ruby (the patience line).** Her free gear holds the early rounds the greedy Neochest
  demands; concede toward Go Go Boxes for pieces, break the chest high, convert.
- **Dauntless front** ([[../frontline_tank]]): Balmond, Franco (Dauntless+WM), Hilda, Esmeralda,
  Silvanna + the hero Ruby (5c Dauntless/Exorcist) as the Cloak carrier. Dauntless [4] = team-wide
  shields on top of the resurrect, brutally hard to kill.
- **Exorcist commitment:** the hero Ruby seeds Exorcist (with Saber, Kagura, Granger, Yu Zhong, Phoveus);
  Exorcist's dying-heroes-become-Phantoms pairs with Ruby's own resurrect (death-payoff stacking). A
  higher-ceiling line.
- **Cloak-tank + Scythe-carry split:** a Defender front tank holds the Cloak (marks + resurrects), a
  back-row Marksman/Mage holds the Scythe (deletes the marked target). Use when my carry is squishy.
- The hero **Ruby** is the on-theme keeper: tanky, taunts (pulls focus so the resurrect matters), and
  carries the Cloak naturally.

## Positioning

The Cloak carrier goes **front** (it needs to attack to mark, and tank to trigger the resurrect); seat
it where it will reach enemies and soak focus (the hero Ruby's taunt helps). If the Scythe is on a
back-row carry, seat that carry **back-center, never cornered** (Swiftblade divers). Spread vs AoE. The
resurrect means the Cloak carrier can take a more exposed, focus-pulling seat than a normal unit, that
is a positioning freedom her kit grants. Full detail: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

maxed, the resurrect blanks the lobby's burst in Battle Acceleration, close. 1st or 2nd off a board
nobody could keep dead.

## Power-card deep dive

Her **exclusive** (+2 gold per surviving Cloak/Scythe carrier, max 35) is the right pick: it pays her for
exactly what her kit already does (keep two gear-carriers alive, which the resurrect guarantees), a
steady econ on a board built to not die. Keep it. The **borrowable** Ruby card is a **trap on Ruby
herself**: it just re-grants the Cloak + Scythe she already starts with, so it is wasted, the borrowable
exists for *other* commanders to borrow her items, not for Ruby to pick. Use the 2 refreshes only if a
once-a-game breakpoint card (a Dauntless/Exorcist Badge/Blessing) would win more.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Other equipment commanders** (Minotaur, Zilong, Karina): we contest the same gear lots/chests; if
  gear is my bottleneck, bid against them, else cede and lean on my free Cloak + Scythe.
- **True-damage / anti-heal boards** (Glowing Wand burn, healing-reduction): they blunt the Cloak heal
  and burn through the resurrect's 30% HP; respect that her stickiness has a counter.
- **Go-wide / multi-carry** boards win without needing to kill my sticky front, I must deal damage, not
  just survive.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **Behind early (lowroll):** the free gear means even a weak board is gear-ahead; hold the interest band
  and let the sticky front stabilize, do not panic-roll.
- **Squishy carry, no front yet:** put **both items on whatever front body I have** until I find the
  keeper Cloak carrier (the hero Ruby or a bruiser); the kit works on any front.
- **Items contested by another equipment commander:** I do not need the gear pool the way Minotaur does
  (my two key items are free), so cede contested gear lots and just stack generic drops on my carry.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** double down on the carry's items and the 2-star Cloak/Scythe; the
  resurrect is a strong all-in anchor.

## The edge (the secret)

**Two strong items free from round 1, and a board that will not stay dead.** The Cloak->Scythe loop
turns one front unit into a self-healing, target-deleting threat that **resurrects** when killed and then
*powers up its own damage* (+55% Scythe ATK), and the exclusive pays her for the survival her kit
guarantees. She is the lobby's gear-ahead, lowest-variance front, and an assassination just buys the
enemy one wasted kill.

## Pitfalls

- **Taking the borrowable card** (redundant, re-grants items she already has), the classic Ruby misplay.
- **Scythe on a low-ATK unit** (the 280% Adaptive scales with ATK, so a tank-Scythe wastes most of it).
- **Cloak on a non-attacking / dying-instantly unit** (it needs to attack to mark and survive to
  resurrect).
- **Over-relying on the resurrect vs anti-heal / true damage**, which counters her stickiness.
- **Spreading gear** instead of funneling onto the Cloak/Scythe carrier(s).

## Threat assessment (who beats her, and why)

**Other equipment commanders** (Minotaur out-gears the lobby harder over a long game, Zilong/Karina
contest gear) can match or beat her item edge. **Anti-heal / true-damage** boards blunt the Cloak heal
and burn the resurrect. **Go-wide / multi-carry** boards sidestep her by winning without killing the
sticky front. She is strongest into **burst / assassin** lobbies (the resurrect blanks the kill) and
gear-light fields; weakest into sustained true-damage and wide boards. Her floor is high (free gear,
no bricks, a sticky front), so she rarely places badly, her ceiling is capped by not being a pure
hyper-carry.

  30% HP** once per round, a whole free death blanked every fight.

  resurrect makes near-automatic.
