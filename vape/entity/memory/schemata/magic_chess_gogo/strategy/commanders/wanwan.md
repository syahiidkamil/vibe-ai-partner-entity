# Commander — Wanwan (Agile Tiger): the finisher

The deep, concrete playbook, round by round. **In pencil**, reasoned from

## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** combat / finisher. When an enemy is chipped below 30% HP, she rapid-fires the lowest-HP
  enemy, cleaning up and snowballing gold from kills.
- **Plays like:** a cleanup engine, build a board that softens enemies, then Wanwan's burst finishes the
  low ones, and each kill pays gold.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is a triggered finisher + kill-gold; the skill is a board that chips
  enemies into her trigger and capitalizing on the kill snowball.
- **Best into:** boards that get chipped (most) and kill-heavy games. **Worst into:** high-sustain enemies
  she cannot push below 30%, and a board of my own with no real damage to soften them.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Wanwan, "Agile Tiger." Skins: Agile Tiger, Azure Tiger. (There is no *hero* Wanwan in the S6
  shop roster, so no commander/hero collision.)
- **Tiger Pounce** [Passive] (verbatim): *"The first time an enemy Hero's HP drops below 30%, Wanwan
  attacks the enemy Hero with the lowest HP 20 time(s), dealing 180/310/430/550 Physical DMG depending on
  the current stage."*
- **Reinforcement** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Gain 1 Gold when killing an enemy Hero, up to 3
  times."*
- **Power Card:** *Wanwan's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"50% chance to gain 1 Gold each
  time you kill an enemy (Max Gold: 35)."*

Reading (in pencil): when the fight chips an enemy **below 30% HP**, Wanwan **rapid-fires the lowest-HP
enemy 20 times** (**180/310/430/550** by stage, so it scales late), a **finisher** that cleans up softened
targets and can chain through a weakened back line. The star-up + card add **gold per kill** (a kill
snowball, up to caps). So she is a **cleanup/finisher**: not a primary carry (flat damage), but a
board-wide closer that rewards a board which **softens enemies** and a **kill-heavy** game.

## Win condition / identity

**Soften the enemy, then clean them up and snowball kills.** Wanwan's burst triggers on a sub-30% enemy
and deletes the lowest-HP one, so a board that chips the enemy down (AoE, a carry, DoT) feeds her a clean
finish, often chaining as the back line crumbles, while each kill pays gold. She is a **combat finisher**
add-on: build a normal damage board, and Wanwan converts "almost dead" into "dead" plus an economy. Best
where the board already wins fights (she makes them win *harder and richer*).

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Feed the trigger (soften enemies).** Tiger Pounce needs an enemy **below 30%**, so my board must
   **chip the enemy down** (a carry, AoE, DoT); on a board that cannot soften anyone, the finisher rarely
   fires.
2. **It is a finisher, not the carry.** The flat damage (180-550) is a closer, not a primary carry, so
   **build real damage carries** and let Wanwan clean up; do not rely on her burst as the main damage.

## The finish plan (her signature, concrete)

The ordered way to run the finisher:
1. **Build a damage board** that softens the enemy (a carry + AoE/chip), so enemies dip below 30%.
2. **Wanwan's burst finishes the lowest-HP enemy** (and the kill snowball can chain as the line crumbles).
3. **Bank the kill-gold** (star-up + card) on a kill-heavy board, an econ bonus for winning hard.
4. **Take her card on a killy board** (50% gold/kill); a generic if my board is not kill-heavy.
5. **Lean late** (the burst scales to 550 in Stage IV), so she closes harder as the game goes.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): build a damage board
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Build toward a damage board (a carry + chip). Creep: a damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take her card** (50% gold/kill) *if* my board is kill-heavy; a generic econ/
  synergy card if not. Situational, lean keep on a killy board.
- **I-3 / I-4:** the finisher cleans up softened enemies; bank toward the band.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a damage hero.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): soften + finish, cross breakpoints
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit the damage synergy.
- **My carries chip, Wanwan finishes**; the kill snowball pays gold. Cross the first breakpoints.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for the carry's item or a Synergy Magic Crystal; kill-gold helps.
- **Leveling:** to the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): spike the carries, finish harder
- **Spike** the carries (star-up, the [6], a payoff card); the finisher (now 430+) cleans up the softened
  enemy faster. Press the round wins.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, gear the carry.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- The finisher peaks at **550** (Stage IV); a chipped enemy is deleted. Close before HP is the binding
  constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Standard for the damage comp; the kill-gold (star-up + card) helps the economy on a winning board, so
level to the carry's tier and for capacity. The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3:
`L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`.

## Best comps (concrete)

**A damage board that softens, with Wanwan finishing:**
- **A marksman/mage carry + AoE/chip** (so enemies dip below 30% for the finisher).
- **An execute-adjacent board** (the chip + finisher both punish low-HP enemies, pairs with the idea of
  Karina, though that is a different commander).
- The constant: **real damage carries** that soften the enemy; Wanwan is the closer, not the carry. Best on
  a **kill-heavy, winning** board (for the gold snowball).

## Power-card deep dive

Her card (**50% gold/kill, max 35**) is **an econ engine on a kill-heavy board** (a winning, killy board
banks a lot), but weak if my board does not get kills. Take it on a killy comp; **deviate to a generic**
econ/synergy card if my board is not kill-heavy. Situational. Use the 2 refreshes to weigh it.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **High-sustain enemies** (lifesteal, heals, shields) that I cannot push below 30% starve the finisher,
  bring anti-heal / more burst.
- **My own board must soften** the enemy; against a tanky board I need chip that actually dents their HP.
- **Economy commanders** out-resource me unless my board is killy (then the kill-gold competes).
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]] (note: enemy *Nana*
  profits from being hit and survives her first death, so she is awkward to finish).

## Contingency and pivots

- **Finisher not triggering (no softening):** prioritize a real damage carry + AoE/chip so enemies dip
  below 30%; the kit is dead without softening.
- **High-sustain enemies:** tech anti-heal (Glowing Wand burn, Sea Halberd) to push them into trigger
  range.
- **Behind early:** the finisher + a damage board stabilize; build the carry.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** spike the carry; the 550 finisher cleans up; close.

## The edge (the secret)

**She turns "almost dead" into "dead," and gets paid for it.** Wanwan's triggered burst deletes a chipped
enemy (chaining as the line crumbles) and snowballs gold from kills, so a board that already softens the
enemy finishes harder and richer, scaling to a 550 closer in Stage IV. A combat finisher that makes a
winning board win more decisively.

## Pitfalls

- **No softening** (the finisher needs sub-30% enemies; build real damage).
- **Relying on the finisher as the carry** (it is flat damage, a closer, not the main DPS).
- **Taking her card on a non-killy board** (the kill-gold starves).
- **No anti-heal vs high-sustain** (cannot push them into trigger range).

## Threat assessment (who beats her, and why)

**High-sustain enemies** she cannot chip below 30% starve the finisher. A **low-damage board of my own**
that cannot soften anyone wastes the kit. **Nana** (survives her first death, profits from chip) is
awkward to finish. She is strongest on a **kill-heavy, winning** board into chippable enemies and weakest
into sustain or on a weak board. A finisher add-on whose value rides on the board doing the softening.

## The 7-archetype read (who I face, and the adjustment)

- vs **chippable / squishy boards** (most): **good**, my board softens, the finisher cleans up + banks gold.
- vs **high-sustain** (lifesteal/heal boards, Weapon Master): tech anti-heal to push them below 30%.
- vs **Nana** (survives first death, profits from chip): awkward to finish; focus other targets.
- vs **fast aggro / combat** (Lancelot, Ling, Lukas): a damage race; my finisher + kill-gold reward winning
  fights.
- vs **value / econ** (Aurora, Diggie, Chou): a killy board's kill-gold competes; out-tempo with damage.
- vs **vertical / copy** (Alice, Aamon): they spike a carry; my board softens it and Wanwan finishes it.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong): I want damage gear for the carries; the finisher is kit.
