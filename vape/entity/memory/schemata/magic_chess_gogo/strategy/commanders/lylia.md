## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** duplicate / vertical via a win-fed copy engine. At capacity 8 she turns the shop off and
  free-copies her highest-cost deployed hero on every win.
- **Plays like:** build a strong, near-complete board by L8, lock a 5-cost carry as the top unit, flip the
  shop off, and snowball free copies of it toward a 2-3 star the pool would never allow.
- **Difficulty:** medium-high. The kit's commitment (no more shop after L8) and the "highest-cost
  deployed" targeting make the *setup before activation* the whole skill.
- **Best into:** boards she can keep winning with at L8 (the copies are win-fed). **Worst into:** a board
  that cannot win at L8 (the engine stalls, and the shop is off), and burst that deletes the one carry the
  copies are building.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Lylia, "Lovable Witch." Skins: [not legible in the source screenshot, fill on a cleaner
  capture]. (Note: the **commander** Lylia is the player avatar, distinct from the **hero** Lylia, a

- **Go, Tharz!** [Active] (verbatim): *"Can be used after Capacity reaches 8: The Shop can't be used later
  on and you immediately gain 1 Hero(es) from the highest-cost Heroes deployed. Upon winning a round, gain
  1 more. If that Hero is sold out in the Shared Hero Pool, gain the next highest cost Hero instead."*
- **Party Time!** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Upon losing, grant 15% Hybrid ATK to the
  highest-cost Hero deployed. If tied, prioritize the one with more Equipment."*
- **Power Card:** *Lylia's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"At Commander Lv.7, receive a
  5-Gold Hero with a matching Equipment."*

Reading (in pencil): once **Capacity reaches 8** (L8), she can flip a switch, the **shop turns off
permanently**, and she **immediately gains a free copy of her highest-cost deployed hero**, then **one more
per round won**. So she stops buying and instead **prints free copies of her top carry on wins**, building
a star-up the shared pool would deny (a 5-cost 3-star = 9 of 9 copies). Party Time! means even a *loss*
buffs that carry (+15% ATK). The card hands her a **free 5-cost + matching item at L7**, the perfect carry
to then free-copy at L8. The big commitment: **no shop after activation**, so the board must be set first.

## Win condition / identity

**A free 2-3 star high-cost carry the pool would never allow, fed by wins.** She builds a strong board by
L8, locks a 5-cost as the highest-cost deployed unit, flips the shop off, and snowballs free copies of it
on every win toward a star-up, while losses buff it. She is a **commitment vertical**: the setup before
activation is everything, and once flipped she rides the win-fed engine to a carry nobody else can
assemble. If her L8 board can keep winning, the snowball is brutal; if it cannot, the engine sputters.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions, both about the activation:
1. **When to flip the shop off.** Activating too early (a weak L8 board) means no more shop *and* few
   wins to feed copies, the engine stalls. Activating with a **strong, near-complete board** means the
   free copies pour in. So **set the board fully before flipping**: the right carry deployed as the
   top-cost unit, the front and synergies in place, gold spent (the shop is about to close anyway).
2. **Highest-cost deployed = the copy target.** The engine copies whatever my **highest-cost deployed
   hero** is, so I must ensure that is the carry I *want* to star up (deploy the intended 5-cost; do not
   leave a stray higher-cost off-carry on the board stealing the copies).

## The activation decision (when to flip the shop off, concrete)

The single skill that defines her. A pre-flip checklist:
1. **Capacity 8 reached** (L8) and **gold spent down** (the shop closes, so unspent gold past a small
   buffer is wasted, do a final roll-down for the front and any last bodies *before* flipping).
2. **The intended carry is the highest-cost deployed hero** (a 5-cost, ideally the one from the L7 card),
   so the copies build *it*.
3. **The board can keep winning** at L8 (a real front, active synergies), because copies are **win-fed**,
   a losing board flips into a stalled engine.
4. **Then flip Go, Tharz!**: gain the immediate free copy + 1 per win. From here, every win is a step
   toward a 2-star (and eventually 3-star) of a carry the pool could never give.
The anti-pattern: flipping on a mediocre board "to start the engine" and then losing, no shop, no wins,
no copies. Patience before the flip is the whole game.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): build normally toward the eventual carry
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Normal early board toward a line a 5-cost can anchor (mage/marksman/front).
  Creep: a flexible damage basic for the eventual carry.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take her card** (a free 5-cost + matching item at L7): it hands her the exact
  high-cost carry the L8 engine will free-copy, perfectly on-kit. Keep it. (Deviate only if a synergy
  card clearly wins more for a committed line.)
- **I-3 / I-4:** bank toward the interest band; build a board that holds.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero opening my line.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): set the board, hit L7 for the card
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit the synergy.
- **Reach L7** to trigger the card's **free 5-cost + item**, that is the carry the engine will copy.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a card matching the carry's synergy.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for the carry's **best item** or a Synergy Magic Crystal (the shop
  closes later, so banked items matter more than usual).
- **Leveling:** push toward **L8 (Capacity 8)**, the activation gate, but **do not flip yet**, finish the
  board first.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): flip the shop off, snowball
- **At L8, do the final roll-down** (front, last bodies), then **flip Go, Tharz!** with the carry as the
  top-cost deployed unit. The free copies start (immediate + 1/win).
- **Win rounds to feed copies** toward a 2-star (and chase 3-star) of the carry; losses buff it (+15% ATK).
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, more carry gear (no shop, so rewards/auction
  are now my only new items).

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- The win-fed copies should have produced a starred high-cost carry nobody else could. Close on the
  snowball before HP is the binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

She **rushes to L8 (Capacity 8)** to unlock the engine, but **times the flip** carefully (board first).
The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10:
231`. After flipping, gold no longer buys shop heroes, so **levels (capacity) and items** are where late
gold goes (a bigger board to deploy the free copies, and gear since the shop is closed). She can keep
leveling to L9-10 for capacity even with the shop off.

## Best comps (concrete)

Built around **one 5-cost carry the engine copies**, on a board strong enough to keep winning at L8:
- **A 5-cost mage/marksman carry** (Odette, Lesley) from the L7 card, anchoring Mage/Marksman, fronted by
  Defender/Dauntless, set fully before the flip.
- **Neobeasts/Mage** (her hero Lylia's synergies) if I field her too, a go-wide board that wins at L8.
- The constant: a **clear single highest-cost carry** to be the copy target, plus a board that does not
  need the shop after L8 (so it must be near-complete at the flip).

## Positioning

Standard for the carry (back-center for ranged/caster, never cornered vs Swiftblade divers; front for a
tanky carry), wall front, spread vs AoE. Because the engine builds *one* carry, protect it like the
investment it is, a dead carry both loses the round and stalls the win-fed copies. Full detail:
[[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## Power-card deep dive

Her card (a **free 5-cost + matching item at L7**) is **excellent and on-kit**: it hands her the exact
high-cost carry the L8 engine will free-copy, *and* an item for it, a free 5-cost is huge value on its
own. Keep it almost always. Deviate only if a synergy-breakpoint card clearly wins more for a committed
line and I already have a clear 5-cost carry. Use the 2 refreshes to confirm.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Boards that beat my L8 board** are the real danger: the copies are **win-fed**, so a rival who can
  out-fight me right after I flip starves my engine. I must flip only when I can keep winning.
- **Burst / assassins** that delete my one carry both lose me the round and stall the copies, protect it.
- **My carry's pool can sell out** (the kit copies "the next highest cost" if so), so a rival contesting my
  5-cost can shift my copies, deploy the carry I actually want as the clear top-cost.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **L8 board too weak to flip:** **wait**, keep using the shop to strengthen it; flipping a losing board
  is the cardinal Lylia error. Patience before the flip.
- **Flipped, now losing (engine stalled):** lean on Party Time! (losses buff the carry +15% ATK) and the
  rewards/auction for items; try to stabilize the board with positioning and the copies I do get.
- **Wrong copy target** (a stray higher-cost hero on board): bench it so my intended carry is the
  highest-cost deployed.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** the shop is off, so deploy the free copies and Party-Time buffs onto the
  carry and close; there is no rolling out of it.
- **L8 arriving but board not ready:** delay the flip and keep shopping, even at the cost of a round or
  two; an unready flip is worse than a late one. The active waits for me, the engine does not have to fire
  the instant capacity hits 8.
- **Carry pool drying up before the flip:** the engine copies "the next highest cost" if my 5-cost is sold
  out, so if rivals are buying my carry, **lock it deployed early** and consider flipping a touch sooner to
  start claiming copies before the pool empties.
- **Highrolled an early strong board:** I can flip closer to on-time and snowball harder; press the win
  streak immediately, the engine rewards a board that was already winning.

## The edge (the secret)

**A 5-cost star-up the shared pool could never give, fed by her own wins.** By turning the shop off and
printing free copies of her top carry on every win (with losses buffing it), Lylia assembles a 2-3 star
high-cost carry that bypasses the pool entirely, the rarest achievement in the game, off a board she set
before flipping. The whole edge is paid for by patience: set the board, then snowball.

## Pitfalls

- **Flipping too early** (a weak L8 board: no shop + no wins = a dead engine). The defining mistake.
- **Wrong highest-cost deployed hero** (the copies build the wrong unit).
- **Not spending gold before the flip** (the shop closes, so unspent gold past a buffer is wasted).
- **Under-protecting the one carry** (its death stalls the win-fed copies).
- **No front at L8** (the board must win without the shop's help after flipping).

## Threat assessment (who beats her, and why)

**A board that out-fights her right at L8** starves the win-fed engine (the copies need wins). **Burst /
assassins** that delete the one carry stall the snowball. A **rival contesting her 5-cost** can shift her
copy target (pool sold out). She is strongest when her **L8 board is already winning** (then the snowball
is unstoppable) and weakest when she flips onto a board that cannot hold. The activation timing is her
whole skill and her whole vulnerability.

## Numbers that matter (the engine, quantified)

- **The gate:** the active unlocks at **Capacity 8** (L8); the upgrade cost to L8 is **103 gold
  cumulative from L3** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]), so plan the climb.
- **The copy rate:** **1 immediate** + **1 per round won**. A 5-cost 3-star needs **9 copies**, so ~8 wins
  after the flip (plus what I held) builds it, a star-up the pool (9 of 9 total) would never allow.
- **Party Time!:** **+15% Hybrid ATK** to the top carry **per loss**, so even a losing round is not pure
  waste, the carry gets stronger.
- **The cost:** the shop is **off** after the flip, so all my power afterward is the free copies + Party
  Time + rewards/auction items, hence the "set the board first" discipline.

- **L7 (the card):** free 5-cost + item received, my carry chosen. *Behind = no carry yet? Build toward

- **Stage III-IV (post-flip):** winning a streak, copies building the carry to 2-3 star. *Behind = engine

- vs **strong L8 boards** (anyone spiked by mid-game): the real threat, I must out-fight them right after
  flipping or the engine starves; flip only when I can win.
- vs **burst / assassin** (Ling, Swiftblade): they delete my one carry, protect it; its death stalls the

- vs **vertical / copy** (Alice, Aamon, Dyrroth): we both assemble pool-denied carries; mine is win-fed,

- vs **econ / value** (Aurora, Diggie, Chou): slower, my snowball out-scales them if my L8 board wins.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong): post-flip I cannot shop, so I value reward/auction gear more;

- vs **synergy grant / free-hero** (Gusion, Lunox, Guinevere, Vale): they go wide; I go tall on one
  win-fed carry.
- vs **combat** (Kagura, Lukas, Wanwan): their board is the threat at L8; tech position and keep winning.

## The two phases of a Lylia game (the strategic frame)

She is the one commander whose game splits cleanly into **two phases at the flip**, and holding that frame
is most of piloting her well:

- **Phase 1, pre-flip (rounds 1 to ~L8): build the engine's chassis.** This phase plays like a normal
  econ-into-board commander, *but every decision is in service of the flip*. Bank the interest band, reach
  L7 for the free 5-cost (the card), commit a synergy, and assemble a board that will **win at L8 without a
  shop**. Gold here is for **board + levels**, and it has a deadline: it must be **mostly spent by the
  flip** (the shop closes). Think of Phase 1 as loading the spring.
- **Phase 2, post-flip (L8 onward): ride the snowball.** The shop is gone; my only inputs are **free
  copies (on wins)**, **Party Time buffs (on losses)**, and **rewards/auction items**. The game is now
  "win to compound the carry, position to protect it, and deploy the copies." There is no rolling out of a
  bad spot, so Phase 2 is pure execution on the chassis Phase 1 built.

The single biggest Lylia skill is **not rushing the transition**: a Phase-1 board that is not ready makes
Phase 2 a stalled engine. The patience to finish loading the spring before releasing it is the whole
commander. (This two-phase shape is unique in the roster, every other commander keeps the shop all game.)
