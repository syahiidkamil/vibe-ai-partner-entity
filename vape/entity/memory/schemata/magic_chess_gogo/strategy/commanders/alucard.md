## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** value / reward engine. A Demonic Altar pays random rewards at fixed rounds, draw-
  independent value that floors placement.
- **Plays like:** a calm value player, harvest the Altar rewards on schedule, flex them into a board, and
  out-resource the lobby without depending on the shop's mood.
- **Difficulty:** low-medium. The kit is passive value; the skill is flexing random rewards into a
  coherent comp and not playing too greedy on the early board.
- **Best into:** value mirrors and long games. **Worst into:** fast aggro before the Altar value compounds,
  and Cyclops (who profits if I die).

- **Demonic Altar** [Active] (verbatim): *"Alucard summons a Demonic Altar beside the battlefield,
  containing 6 Rewards. Each time you activate the Altar, discard 1 reward and randomly receive 1 from the
  remaining rewards. You can activate the Altar once in Rounds I-3 and II-3."*
- **Plunder Expansion** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Gain 1 additional activation in Round
  III-3."*
- **Power Card:** *Alucard's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Obtain Demonic Altar (6
  Rewards). In Rounds I-2, II-2, III-2, discard 1 reward and receive 1 from the remaining."*

Reading (in pencil): the Altar holds **6 rewards**; each activation I **discard 1 (the worst) and randomly
get 1 of the rest**, so the pool narrows and the odds of a good pull rise each time. Activations at
**I-3, II-3** (kit) **+ III-3** (star-up) = 3, and the card adds an **Altar at I-2/II-2/III-2** (3 more),
so up to **6 reward pulls** over a game. He is a **draw-independent value engine** like Aurora and Diggie:
the rewards come regardless of the shop, flooring placement, paid for by a weaker early board (he is not
spending hard early).

## Win condition / identity

**Out-value the lobby passively, then convert the banked rewards into a board.** The Altar pays on a
schedule no matter what the shop hands me, so my resources outpace the field while I play a calm early
game; then I flex the rewards (items, heroes, gold, whatever the Altar gives) into a spiked mid-late
board. He is a **placement-first safe climb**: reliable top-4 because the value is guaranteed, with the
usual value-engine risk of a soft early board that must survive to cash in.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Discard smart.** Each activation discards 1 of the 6 then pulls randomly from the rest, so **discard
   the reward I least want** to bias the pull toward the good ones, and consider activation *timing* (the
   pool narrows, so later pulls are more reliable, but I want value flowing early too).
2. **Flex the random rewards.** The Altar gives whatever it gives, so **convert it**: use a reward hero
   in my comp or as star-up fodder, an item on my carry, gold to roll. Do not let off-comp rewards sit
   idle.

## The altar plan (his signature, concrete)

The ordered way to run the engine:
1. **Take the card** for the extra Altar (pulls at I-2/II-2/III-2 on top of the kit's I-3/II-3/III-3).
2. **At each activation, discard the worst of the 6** and take the random pull; discard to **bias toward
   the rewards my comp wants** (keep the synergy/carry pieces in the pool, discard the off-comp ones).
3. **Flex each reward** immediately: item -> carry, hero -> comp/fodder, gold -> roll/bank.
4. **Survive the soft early board** (value engines are weak before they cash in) by spending *some* to
   stay competitive, the Mythic anti-greed rule.
5. **Spike mid-game** once the rewards have funded a real board.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): start the value, stay alive
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Flexible early board. Creep: a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take his card** (an extra Altar, pulls at I-2/II-2/III-2): doubles the reward
  cadence, on-kit. Keep it. The first card-Altar pull is now.
- **I-3 (kit Altar):** activate, discard the worst, take the pull. Flex it.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero opening the line the rewards favor.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): cash the rewards, commit
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit a synergy.
- **II-2 (card Altar) + II-3 (kit Altar):** two activations this stage, discard smart, flex the pulls into
  the comp. Cross the first breakpoints.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** value-rich, bid well on a key item or a Synergy Magic Crystal.
- **Leveling:** the banked value funds a steady push to the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): spike
- **III-2 (card Altar) + III-3 (kit Altar, the star-up activation):** two more pulls; the accumulated
  rewards fund a spike, roll to star up, field the carries, take a payoff card.
- **End of Stage III, Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, more carry gear.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the reward-built board, dump banked gold into the last star-ups and items. Close before HP is the
  binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Steady; the Altar value (not the shop) is his edge, so he levels on the free EXP plus the banked rewards
and pushes mid-game when committing. The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3:
`L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Avoid the value-engine trap of banking
so long that capacity lags the board.

## Best comps (concrete)

**Flexible**, shaped by the Altar rewards:
- **A frontline + one fed carry** ([[../frontline_tank]]) pairs with his safe value style.
- **A go-wide board** if the rewards spread synergies.
- The constant: **let the rewards crystallize the comp**, commit by mid Stage II, and convert every pull.

I-1 L3: flexible board. I-2: take his card, first card-Altar pull. I-3: kit-Altar pull. Stage I-II: I
activate the Altar at I-2/I-3/II-2/II-3, discarding the worst each time and flexing the pulls (an item to

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fast aggro** ends the game before the Altar value compounds, protect HP and spend *some* early to stay
  competitive.
- **Cyclops** profits if I die early; do not let a soft early board make me his first kill.
- I am **draw-independent**, so I rarely lose the pool war; my risk is **tempo**.

## Contingency and pivots

- **Bleeding HP early:** spend to hold a real board for a few rounds; survival outranks pure value.
- **Off-comp rewards:** flex them as fodder/gold rather than forcing them onto the board.
- **Value-ahead, board-behind:** dump the banked value now (anti-greed correction).
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** convert all rewards + gold into the board and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**Guaranteed, draw-independent value on a schedule, doubled by the card.** The Altar pays regardless of
the shop, and the discard-then-pull mechanic biases toward the rewards I want, so Alucard out-resources
the field with low variance and flexes the haul into a board. A high-floor placement-first climber.

## Pitfalls

- **Discarding carelessly** (discard the *worst* to bias the pull toward good rewards).
- **Hoarding value** so long the board lags and I die before cashing in (the value-engine trap).
- **Letting off-comp rewards sit idle** instead of converting them.
- **Playing too greedy early** on a soft board (spend some to survive).

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the Altar value compounds. **Cyclops** profits if I die. He is
strongest in **long value games** where the rewards snowball; weakest in fast ones. A low-variance value
engine whose floor is high and whose only real risk is the soft early board.

## Numbers that matter (the engine, quantified)

- **Activations:** **I-3, II-3, III-3** (kit) **+ I-2, II-2, III-2** (card) = up to **6 reward pulls** over
  a game, each from a 6-reward Altar.
- **The discard math:** each pull **removes 1 of 6 then draws randomly from the rest**, so discarding the
  worst raises the odds the random pull is something I want, and a narrowed pool late is more reliable.
- **Value type:** rewards are mixed (items, heroes, gold), so each is multi-purpose, the engine is
  draw-independent value, not a fixed payout.
