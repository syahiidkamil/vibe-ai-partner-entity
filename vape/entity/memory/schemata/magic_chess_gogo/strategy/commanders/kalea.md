## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** value / reward engine with a push-your-luck timing. Roll a dice toward 8 without busting,
  then cash the Trove for a points-scaled reward.
- **Plays like:** a value gambler, accumulate dice points each round, decide when to lock in a Trove
  activation for a big reward vs risk a bust, flex the haul into a board.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is passive value plus a real **when-to-activate** decision (the
  push-your-luck call); the skill is timing the cash-out and flexing the rewards.
- **Best into:** long games and value mirrors. **Worst into:** fast aggro before the Trove pays, and a
  greedy bust that wastes accumulated points.

Reading (in pencil): from **Round I-4** I tap the Lucky Dice each round for **1-4 points**; if the running
total **exceeds 8 it resets to 0** (a bust), and I **activate the Trove** to claim a reward **scaled to my
current points** (max **2 activations**, **3** starred). So it is **push-your-luck**: bank points toward 8
for a big reward, but cash out before a high roll busts me. The card adds **gold rolls (21-35 total)** now
+ at Stage III. He is a **draw-independent value engine** like Aurora/Diggie, with a gambling-timing
twist, the *when to activate* is the skill.

## Win condition / identity

**Out-value the lobby via well-timed Trove cash-outs, then convert the haul into a board.** The Trove pays
regardless of the shop, scaled to how high I bank the dice, so disciplined cash-outs (lock in ~6-8 points,
do not bust) net big rewards a couple times a match. He is a **placement-first safe climb** with a
decision layer: the value is guaranteed, the *size* depends on playing the push-your-luck well. Survive
the soft early board, cash the Trove, spike mid-late.

## The core tension (every round)

The signature tension is **cash out vs push**:
- At a **low total** (1-5), keep rolling (low bust risk, small reward if I cashed now).
- At a **high total** (6-8), the reward is big but the next roll risks a **bust to 0** (at 6, a 3-4 busts;
  at 7-8, most rolls bust). So **activate the Trove at a high total before pushing into bust range**,
  unless I have activations to spare and want to gamble for a max-points cash-out.
- With only **2-3 activations**, each cash-out should be at a **high, safe-ish total**, do not waste an
  activation on a low total or lose points to a greedy bust.

## The dice plan (his signature, concrete)

The ordered way to run the Trove:
1. **Tap the dice every round from I-4** (free points; never skip a tap).
2. **Bank toward ~6-8** without busting; track the bust risk (at 6, a roll of 3-4 busts; at 7, only a 1
   stays; at 8, any roll busts unless it caps).
3. **Activate the Trove at a high total** (ideally 7-8) to claim a big points-scaled reward, **before** a
   greedy push wipes the points.
4. **Spend the 2-3 activations wisely** across the game (e.g. one mid-Stage-II, one Stage-III, one
   Stage-IV with the star-up), each at a high total.
5. **Flex each reward** into the board, and take the card's gold rolls (21-35) as bonus econ.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): start tapping
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Flexible early board. Creep: a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take his card** (gold rolls now + at Stage III, 21-35 total): on-kit econ that
  pairs with the dice engine. Keep it.
- **I-4 (dice starts):** begin tapping the Lucky Dice each round; bank points.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero opening the line.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): first Trove cash-out, commit
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit a synergy.
- **Bank the dice to ~7-8 and activate the Trove** for a big reward; flex it into the comp. Cross the first
  breakpoints.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** value-rich, bid well on a key item or a Synergy Magic Crystal.
- **Leveling:** the rewards + card gold fund a steady push to the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): second cash-out, spike
- **The card's Stage-III gold roll + a second Trove activation** (at a high total) fund a spike; roll to
  star up, take a payoff card.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, gear the carry.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): third activation (starred), close
- The **3rd Trove activation** (Wave of Fortune) lands a final big reward; lock the board and dump banked
  gold into the last star-ups and items. Close before HP is the binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Steady; the Trove value (not the shop) is his edge, so level on the free EXP + the rewards + the card gold
and push mid-game when committing. The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3:
`L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Avoid the value-engine trap of banking
so long the board lags.

## Contingency and pivots

- **Bleeding HP early:** spend to hold a real board; survival outranks pure value.
- **Dice near a bust:** **cash out the Trove** at a high total rather than greedily pushing (a bust wastes
  the banked points).
- **Off-comp rewards:** flex as fodder/gold.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** use the 3rd activation + all gold to spike and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**Big, draw-independent rewards scaled to a push-your-luck dice, played well.** Disciplined cash-outs
(banking near 8 without busting) net Kalea premium Trove rewards a few times a match, value the shop does
not give, and the card's gold rolls fund the conversion. A high-floor value climber with a real skill
layer (the cash-out timing) that separates a good Kalea from a busted one.

## Pitfalls

- **Greedy busts** (pushing past 8 and resetting the points, the cardinal Kalea error).
- **Wasting an activation** on a low total (each of the 2-3 should be at a high total).
- **Hoarding value** so long the board lags (the value-engine trap).
- **Playing too greedy early** on a soft board (spend some to survive).

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the Trove compounds. **Cyclops** profits if I die. He is strongest in
**long value games** where the cash-outs snowball; weakest in fast ones or if I bust away my points. A
low-variance value engine *if* the dice are played with discipline, the bust risk is the only self-inflicted
threat.

## Numbers that matter (the dice, quantified)

- **The dice:** **+1-4 points/round** (avg ~2.5) from I-4; **bust (reset to 0) if total > 8**. So the
  sweet spot to cash is **~7-8** (a high reward, before a near-certain bust on the next roll).
- **Bust risk by total:** at **6**, a 3-4 busts (~50%); at **7**, only a 1 stays (~75% bust); at **8**,
  any positive roll busts, so **activate at 7-8**, do not push.
- **Activations:** **2** (kit), **3** (starred), each a points-scaled reward, so spend them at high totals,
  spread across the game.
- **The card:** **21-35 gold** total across two rolls (now + Stage III), bonus econ on top.

## Stage checkpoints (am I on pace?)

- **End of Stage I (~L5):** card taken, dice tapping from I-4, banking points, flexible board surviving.
  *Behind = bleeding? Spend some to hold a board.*
- **End of Stage II (~L7-8):** first Trove cash-out (at ~7-8 points) flexed in, comp committed. *Behind =
  busted the dice? Cash earlier next time.*
- **Stage III (~L9):** card gold + 2nd cash-out funding a spike. *Behind = capacity lagging? Level.*
- **Stage IV (L10):** 3rd activation landed, finalized, closing.
