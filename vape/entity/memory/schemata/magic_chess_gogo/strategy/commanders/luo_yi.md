## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** equipment / flex-conversion. She turns heroes into stat-and-synergy *items*, expanding
  her effective capacity and synergy density.
- **Plays like:** a high-skill toolbox, store a synergy count or a fed hero's stats in a gear slot, free
  a board slot, and run more power than capacity should allow.
- **Difficulty:** high. The kit is the most fiddly in the roster (a transform-or-keep decision per
  hero), and its payoff (count compression, stat funneling) rewards a player who plans the board as a
  system.
- **Best into:** go-wide synergy boards and capacity-starved spots she can relieve. **Worst into:** fast
  aggro that ends before her conversion engine pays, and burst that deletes the carry holding the Cores.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Luo Yi, "Mistveil Mage." Skins: Siren Priestess, Mistveil Mage. (Note: the **commander**
  Luo Yi is the player avatar, distinct from the **hero** Luo Yi, a 3-cost Stargazer/Northern Vale unit
  in [[../../infos/heroes/cost_3]]. This file is the commander.)
- **Duality Transformation** [Passive] (verbatim): *"Summon a device beside the battlefield. Dragging a
  Hero into the device transforms their power into a piece of Equipment, Essence Core. The Essence Core
  inherits a portion of the Hero's attributes. Reset the device to transform the Core back into the Hero.
  The Essence Core inherits 40% of the Hero's Hybrid ATK and HP, and 40% of the base attributes from the
  Hero's Equipment. If a Hero with the Blessing effect is transformed, the Synergy granted by the Blessing
  still counts, but the Essence Core carrier does not inherit the Blessing effect."*
- **Harmony of Essence** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Based on the Role Synergy of the Hero that
  provides the power, the Essence Core gains extra enhancement effects: Enhancement Effects"* (the
  specific per-synergy effects are truncated in the source, see the gap note above).
- **Power Card:** *Luo Yi's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Obtain random 3-Gold Hero ×1 now,
  then the same one at each round start, up to 6 total."*

**Equipment the kit grants ([[../../infos/equipments/commander_exclusive]]):** the **Essence Core**
("Hero's Power Inherited"), **+10% Hybrid ATK, +10% Max HP** at Basic (*Enh: +20% / +20%*), plus the
inherited **40% of the transformed Hero's Hybrid ATK and HP** and **40% of that Hero's equipment base
stats**, and (by the exclusive Core text) it *"counts the Blessing's Synergy without the carrier gaining
the Blessing."*

Reading (in pencil): she **converts a Hero into a wearable item**. The Core takes a **gear slot** on a
carry and gives: a flat stat block (+10%/+10%, Enh +20%/+20%), **40% of the transformed hero's ATK/HP +
40% of its gear**, a **synergy enhancement** (Harmony, exact bonus unknown), and, critically, if the
transformed hero was **Blessed**, it **keeps that Blessing's synergy count** while the hero is off the
board. So she can **store a synergy count + real stats in a gear slot**, freeing a board slot, an
effective **capacity and synergy-density expansion** no other commander has.

## Win condition / identity

**Run more power and more synergy counts than my capacity should allow.** By transforming heroes into
Essence Cores, she holds a synergy count (especially a *blessed* one) and a fed hero's stats in a gear
slot instead of a board slot, so her on-board units can all be carries/breakpoint-fillers while the
"glue" lives as gear. She is a **flexible value/optimization** commander: the floor is a normal board
plus a free stat item; the ceiling is a board that out-densities the lobby (extra synergies + funneled
stats). High skill, high optimization ceiling.

## The core tension (every round)

The standing decision is **transform or keep**, per hero, and it is genuinely hard:
1. **Keep on board** when the hero's *body* (its skill, its position, its full stats) matters more than a
   freed slot.
2. **Transform into a Core** when I want its **synergy count or stats but not its body**, the canonical
   use: a hero whose synergy I need (especially blessed) but whose body is weak, becomes a gear-slot
   count + stats, and a stronger unit takes the freed board slot.
3. **Which carry wears the Core** matters (the inherited stats land on it), funnel onto the main carry.
The transform is reversible (reset the device), so the tension is recurring, not one-shot.

## The conversion plan (her signature, concrete)

The ordered way to use the device:
1. **Identify a "glue" hero** I want for its synergy/stats but not its body (a benched breakpoint-filler,
   or a fed spare).
2. **Bless it first if possible** (the II-1/III-3 Blessing system), so the Core **keeps the blessing's
   synergy count** off-board, that is the highest-value transform (a count in a gear slot).
3. **Transform it into an Essence Core** and **equip the Core on my main carry** (the inherited 40%
   ATK/HP + 40% of that hero's gear lands on the carry).
4. **Fill the freed board slot** with a stronger unit (another carry, a wall body, a new breakpoint).
5. **Reset when the comp shifts** (the transform is reversible), so the Core always holds the
   right hero for the current board.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): build normally, learn the device
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Play a normal early board; the device is a mid-game optimization, not an
  early spike. Buy toward an open line. Creep: a flexible damage/ATK-Speed basic for the eventual carry.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take her card** (a free 3-gold hero now + the same one each round, up to 6):
  six free 3-cost bodies is strong value, fodder for star-ups *and* for Essence Cores (transform the
  spares). Keep it unless a breakpoint/capacity card clearly wins more.
- **I-3 / I-4:** accumulate bodies and gold; the free 3-costs pile up.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero that opens my line.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): start converting, free slots
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count, **and plan to transform a blessed hero**
  into a Core later to keep that count off-board.
- **Begin transforming:** turn a glue hero (or a fed spare) into a Core on my carry, freeing a board slot
  for a stronger unit. Cross breakpoints, using Cores to hold counts.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a card matching the line I am building.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for a carry item or a Synergy Magic Crystal; the free-hero card means
  I rarely need hero-copy lots.
- **Leveling:** for capacity (more board slots = more to convert from) and the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): out-density the lobby
- **Spike:** with Cores holding counts + stats, field a board denser than my capacity (extra synergies +
  a funneled carry). Star up the carry; Enhance the Cores (+20%/+20%).
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, more carry gear, more blessings to bank in
  Cores.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the densest board: carries on board, glue + counts in Cores, items maxed. Close before HP is the
  binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

Level for **capacity** (more board slots feed more transforms and more on-board carries) and the carry's
tier. The upgrade ladder, cumulative from L3:
`L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Her free 3-cost card supplies bodies,
so she leans **value/level** over heavy rolling; the conversion engine is her power, not the reroll.

## Best comps (concrete)

Flexible, the device suits any board that wants **more density than capacity**:
- **Go-wide / many-synergy:** transform glue heroes into Cores to hold their (blessed) synergy counts in
  gear slots, fielding more *active* synergies than capacity should allow, pairs with Mystic Meow
  (HP per active synergy) and Solid Alliance (ATK per active synergy).
- **Single fed carry + stat-funnel:** transform fed spares into Cores on the one carry, concentrating
  40%-of-each into it, a hyper-statted carry built from leftovers.
- The hero **Luo Yi** (3c Stargazer/Northern Vale) fits a Stargazer/caster board if I want to field her
  too; not required.

## Positioning

Standard for the on-board units (carries back-center, wall front, spread vs AoE); the Essence Cores are
**items**, so they have no position, they just sit in the carry's gear slots. The one positioning note:
because the device frees board slots, I often have *more* on-board carries than a normal commander, so
protect the multiple threats and do not over-cluster. Full detail:
[[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## A worked line (one ideal game)

I-1 L3: normal board, take her card (free 3-cost each round). Stage I-II: the free 3-costs pile up; I
2-star a couple and bench the spares. II: I want a Bruiser [4] but cannot fit the last body, so I bless a
glue Bruiser and **transform it into an Essence Core** on my carry, the Bruiser count still counts
(blessed), the carry gets +40% of that hero's stats, and I field a stronger unit in the freed slot. III:
I run **two carries + a Core-held synergy count + Mystic Meow** for a board denser than my capacity; I
Enhance the Cores. IV: the densest board at the table closes it. 1st, if I piloted the conversions well.

## Tier lists (for Luo Yi)

The tier list as it lives *here*, in Luo Yi's form of life (the general [[../tier_lists]] is only the

- **Synergies:** **go-wide / many-synergy lines rise** (Mystic Meow, Solid Alliance shapes, Neobeasts

- **Heroes by phase:** **fed / high-stat heroes and blessed glue rise as transform targets** (40% of

## Power-card deep dive

Her card (a free 3-cost hero now + the same one each round, up to 6) is **strong, flexible value**: six
free bodies feed star-ups *and* the Essence Core engine (transform the spares), and "the same one"
repeated builds toward a 3-star of that 3-cost. Keep it unless a **capacity card (Fanny's Power)** or a
**synergy breakpoint card** clearly wins more for the wide board, both are reasonable deviations given
her density plan. Use the 2 refreshes to weigh them.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fast aggro / burst** (Lancelot, combat kits, Ling): they end the game before my conversion engine
  pays (it is a mid-game optimization), protect HP and set up the density early.
- **Capacity-card / go-wide rivals** compete for the same wide-board space and synergy pool; my Cores
  give me an edge in density, but a contested wide line still tightens.
- **I am draw-flexible** (the free-hero card), so I rarely lose the pool war; my risk is **tempo and
  execution** (the kit is fiddly).
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **Engine not paying (early game):** that is expected, play a normal board and bank the free 3-costs;
  the device is a mid-game tool, do not force it turn 1.
- **Mis-transformed (wrong hero in the Core):** **reset the device** (the transform is reversible) and
  re-convert the right hero, low-cost to fix.
- **Capacity-starved:** this is exactly her strength, transform a glue/blessed hero into a Core to free
  the slot rather than dropping a breakpoint.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** lock the densest board, stop fiddling; commit the Cores and carries.

## The edge (the secret)

**Capacity and synergy-density expansion no one else has.** By storing a hero's stats and (blessed)
synergy count in a gear slot, Luo Yi fields more *active* power and more breakpoints than her capacity
should allow, building a hyper-statted carry from leftovers or running an extra synergy for free. The
ceiling is a board that simply out-densities the lobby; the cost is the highest skill floor in the roster.

## Pitfalls

- **Forcing the device too early** (it is a mid-game optimization; early it just removes a body).
- **Transforming a hero whose *body* you needed** (its skill/position) for a marginal slot.
- **Not blessing the hero first** when a count-in-a-slot was available (the highest-value transform).
- **Putting the Core on a non-carry** (the inherited 40% stats want to land on the unit doing the work).
- **Over-fiddling** instead of just playing a strong board (the kit can distract from fundamentals).

## Threat assessment (who beats her, and why)

**Fast aggro / burst** ends the game before her mid-game conversion engine matures. **Go-wide rivals**
contest her density space and the synergy pool. The kit's **high skill floor** is its own enemy, a
mispiloted Luo Yi is just a normal board with a free stat item, while a well-piloted one out-densities
the table. She is strongest in **long, optimization-rewarding games** and capacity-starved spots; weakest
in fast ones where the device never pays. Her ceiling is high; her floor is "fine," and the gap between
them is execution.

## Numbers that matter (the conversion, quantified)

- **Essence Core stats:** **+10% Hybrid ATK, +10% Max HP** base (**Enh +20% / +20%**), **plus 40% of the
  transformed hero's Hybrid ATK and HP** and **40% of that hero's equipment base stats**. So a *fed*
  hero (3 items, high stats) makes a *strong* Core, the better the hero you transform, the better the
  item.
- **The count trick:** a **blessed** hero transformed **keeps the Blessing's synergy count** while
  off-board (the carrier does not gain the Blessing, but the count still counts), so a gear slot can hold
  a +1 synergy count, effectively a free board slot for that breakpoint.
- **Reversible:** reset the device to turn the Core back into the hero, so conversions are not one-way.
- **Harmony enhancement:** a Role-Synergy-flavored buff on the Core (exact bonus unresolved in the
  source, see the gap note); structurally, transforming a hero of a given role tunes the Core's effect.

## The 7-archetype read (who I face, and the adjustment)

- vs **fast aggro / combat** (Lancelot, Ling, Lukas): they punish my slow engine, protect HP early and
  set up density fast; do not over-fiddle while bleeding.
- vs **go-wide rivals** (Neobeasts, Mystic Meow, Lunox [6]): we contest the wide space, my Cores give a
  density edge, but a contested synergy pool still tightens, prefer an open axis.
- vs **econ / value** (Aurora, Diggie, Chou): a value race, I match with the free-hero card and out-
  density them mid-game.
- vs **vertical** (Alice, Aamon): they go tall on one carry, I go wide+dense; different lanes, little
  direct contention.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong, Karina): we all want gear, but my "gear" is partly free
  (Cores from heroes), so I cede some gear lots.
- vs **synergy grant** (Gusion, Lunox): they buy counts directly, I hold counts in Cores; if we share an
  axis the pool tightens.
- vs **free-hero / shop** (Guinevere, Harley, Vale): they beat the pool wide, my card already feeds me
  bodies; commit my density plan and ignore their lane.
