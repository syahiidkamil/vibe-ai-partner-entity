## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** shop control / synergy commit. He refreshes the shop to show *exactly* the synergy he is
  building, with a free buy, on a charge at almost every level-up.
- **Plays like:** pick a synergy, then fish for it with surgical precision, targeted refreshes hand him
  the members the pool would make him roll blindly for.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is a powerful targeted-refresh active; the skill is committing the right
  synergy and spending the charges (and free buys) at the right moments.
- **Best into:** uncontested synergy lanes he can stack fast. **Worst into:** a rival contesting his
  chosen synergy (the targeted refresh still draws from a drained pool), and fast aggro before he stacks.

## Win condition / identity

**Commit one synergy and reach its breakpoints faster and cheaper than anyone.** The targeted refreshes
mean he is never fishing blind, he sees his synergy's members on demand, and the free buys (kit + card,
one each per level-up) grab them for nothing. So he stacks a [4]/[6] ahead of the pool and on schedule
with his leveling. He is a **shop-control synergy snowball**: pick the axis, then let the kit feed it. His
edge is *finding* the line; the comp is whatever synergy he commits.

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Which synergy to commit.** Updraft is strongest pointed at **one** line (every charge feeds the same
   axis), so the high-stakes choice is *which* synergy, ideally one **uncontested** and **6-stackable**
   (the targeted refresh still draws from the shared pool, so a contested line starves even his fishing).
2. **When to spend charges + free buys.** Charges come at level-ups (7 of them), so **leveling is the
   clock**, each level is a targeted refresh + 2 free buys (kit + card). Spend them to cross the next
   breakpoint, not randomly; bank a charge if the shop is already feeding me.

## The charge cadence (his signature, concrete)

Charges arrive at **Commander Levels 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10** (one per level from the kit + star-up). Each
is a targeted Updraft (synergy refresh + 1 free buy), and the card adds another free buy + 1 gold per
level. So:
- **Treat every level-up as a synergy-fishing event:** level, then Updraft for my axis, grab the members
  (one free from the kit, one free from the card, buy the rest cheap).
- **Level on pace** to fire charges through the game (do not sit under-leveled hoarding gold, the charges
  *are* the value, and they need level-ups to arrive).
- **Point Updraft at a hero of my committed synergy** so the refresh shows that synergy's members; switch
  the target only if I pivot the axis.
- **Bank a free buy** if the shop already has what I need, but do not waste a charge, fire it the round I
  want to cross a breakpoint.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): read the open synergy, take the first charges
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Read which synergy the early shop + lobby leave open. Build toward it.
  Creep: a flexible damage basic for the synergy's carry.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take his card** (a free buy + 1 gold per level-up): it doubles the free-buy value
  of his leveling kit, perfectly on-kit. Keep it.
- **I-4 (first charge):** at L4, the first Updraft, target my chosen synergy, refresh, grab members with
  the free buys.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a member of my synergy.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): stack the synergy with charges
- **L5/L6 charges:** Updraft each level-up for my axis, cross **[4]** cheaply (targeted refresh + free
  buys). Combine a 2-star (II-1) for a +1 count.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** a synergy payoff or Badge/Blessing for my axis.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for a Synergy Magic Crystal (another +1 count) or the carry's item.
- **Leveling:** push steadily (each level = a charge); aim L7-8 by end of II.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): spike the [6]
- **L7/L8/L9 charges:** Updraft to cross **[6]** and star up the core; the targeted refresh makes the
  6-stack reachable a round before the pool would allow. Take Purest Power (the [6] payoff).
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish the count, more carry gear.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- **L10 charge:** a final targeted refresh for the last star-ups. Lock the spiked synergy board and close
  before HP is the binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

**Leveling is his engine** (charges at every level 4-10), so he levels *on pace* rather than hoarding,
each level is a targeted refresh + 2 free buys (kit + card) + 1 gold. The upgrade ladder, cumulative from
L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Do not under-level (it strands the
charges) or wildly over-spend (he still needs gold to buy the non-free members and to ride the interest
band); steady, charge-paced leveling is the cadence.

## Power-card deep dive

His card (a **free buy + 1 gold per level-up**) is **strongly on-kit**: he is already a leveling-paced
commander (charges at every level), so the card doubles the free-buy value of each level and adds econ.
Keep it. Deviate only if a synergy-breakpoint card clearly wins more for a committed line. Use the 2
refreshes to weigh a Badge/Blessing for the axis.

  Updraft cheaply).
- **Fast aggro** ends the game before I stack the [6], protect HP and level on pace.
- **Lunox** (another synergy-committer) may share my axis, read it and take a different open line.

## Contingency and pivots

- **Synergy contested (pool drying):** **re-point Updraft at a different open synergy**, the kit makes a
  pivot cheap (just target a new hero and the refreshes follow); do not fish a drained pool.
- **Under-leveled (charges stranded):** level up, the charges *are* the value and they need level-ups;
  do not hoard gold past the point of stalling the engine.
- **Behind early:** the free buys + targeted refreshes stabilize toward a coherent board; commit the axis
  and let the charges compound.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** fire the L10 charge for the last star-up and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**Surgical synergy-fishing on a per-level cadence.** Where everyone else rerolls blind, Vale refreshes the
shop to show *only his synergy's members*, with a free buy, at every level from 4 to 10, so he stacks a
[4]/[6] faster and cheaper than the pool should allow. The cleanest, most reliable synergy-committer in
the roster, his only ceiling is contention on the line he picks.

## Pitfalls

- **Spreading Updraft across synergies** (it wants one committed axis; scattering wastes the charges).
- **Committing a contested line** (the targeted refresh still pulls from a drained pool).
- **Under-leveling** (the charges arrive at level-ups; hoarding gold strands them).
- **Wasting a charge** on a refresh I did not need instead of saving it for a breakpoint round.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**A rival contesting his chosen synergy** is his main counter, the targeted refresh cannot conjure heroes
the pool has run dry. **Fast aggro** ends the game before the [6] stacks. He is strongest into an
**uncontested, 6-stackable** lane (where he is unmatched at committing) and weakest into a crowded one or
a fast finish. A synergy snowball whose power is the *reliability* of the stack, gated only by pool
contention.

## Numbers that matter (the kit, quantified)

- **The charges:** one at **each of L4-L10** (4/6/8/10 kit + 5/7/9 star-up = **7 charges**), each a
  **targeted refresh + 1 free buy**. So leveling to L10 = 7 surgical synergy-fishes.
- **The card stacks it:** **+1 free buy + 1 gold per level-up**, so each level-up is **2 free buys** of my
  synergy's members + gold, the pool-beating engine.
- **Targeted vs blind:** a normal reroll shows random heroes at the level's odds; Updraft shows **only my
  synergy's members**, so my effective hit rate on the line is far higher, the whole edge.

  *Behind = synergy contested? Re-point Updraft to an open line.*

## The 7-archetype read (who I face, and the adjustment)

- vs **synergy-committers** (Lunox, another Vale): we contest the same axis, take a *different* open line;
  the targeted refresh is wasted on a drained pool.
- vs **fast aggro / combat** (Lancelot, Ling, Lukas): they end it before the [6], level on pace and protect
  HP.
- vs **vertical / copy** (Alice, Aamon, Paquito): they go tall on a hero, I go tall on a synergy, little
  contention unless we share a carry.
- vs **econ / value** (Aurora, Diggie, Chou): a value race; my targeted stack spikes mid-game, out-tempo
  them.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong): they want gear, I want synergy members, little overlap.
- vs **free-hero / shop** (Guinevere, Harley): we both beat the pool; mine is *targeted* (a synergy),
  theirs is *broad/high-cost*, so we rarely want the exact same heroes unless our lines overlap.
- vs **combat** (Kagura, Wanwan): their board is the threat; tech position, stack my [6] and out-power.
