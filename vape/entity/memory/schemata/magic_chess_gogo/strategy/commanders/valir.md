## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** Go Go Card specialist. He picks *two* cards at II-3 and gains EXP from the card layer,
  doubling a key run-shaping moment.
- **Plays like:** a card-engine player, build the run around the augment layer, take two cards at the big
  II-3 stage, and convert the card power into a spike.
- **Difficulty:** medium. The kit is a card-double plus a niche EXP assist; the skill is having a comp the
  two cards can pay off and reading which two to take.
- **Best into:** comps that scale hard off cards (synergy payoffs, econ stacks). **Worst into:** fast aggro
  before II-3 pays, and games where no two strong cards fit my line.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Valir, "Son of Flames." Skins: Son of Flames, Son of Emberlight. (There is no *hero* Valir in
  the S6 shop roster, so no commander/hero collision.)
- **Blazing Inferno** [Passive] (verbatim): *"Valir can choose Go Go Cards twice in the Go Go Card Stage of
  Round II-3. (Skill effect adjusted in Magic Crystal: Rune mode: Gain 15 Commander EXP at the start of
  Round II-3.)"*
- **Incinerate** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"Gain extra Commander EXP based on Card Quality in
  Round II-3: 16 EXP for purple card. 26 EXP for blue card. (Skill effect adjusted in Magic Crystal: Rune
  mode: Gain 15 extra Commander EXP at the start of Round II-3.)"*
- **Power Card:** *Valir's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Gain 4 Gold. Obtain a random
  Commander Power Card."*

Reading (in pencil): Valir **picks two Go Go Cards at II-3** instead of one, doubling that run-shaping
moment, and **gains Commander EXP by the card's quality** (16 for a purple, **26 for a blue**, the lower
rarity gives *more* EXP, a tradeoff: a weaker card for a bigger level assist). The card adds **+4 gold + a
random Power Card** (a third card). He is a **card-layer specialist**: his power is in the augments, so he
wants a comp those cards pay off and a read on which two to take.

## Win condition / identity

**Over-power the card layer, then convert it into a spike.** Two cards at II-3 (plus the card's bonus
card) means more run-shaping than anyone, two synergy payoffs, or a payoff + an econ stack, or a payoff +
an item bundle, landing at the mid-game commit stage. He is a **card-leveraged flex**: the cards bend his
run harder than the field's, and the EXP assist keeps him on level. The skill is building a comp the
doubled cards amplify (a synergy core for Purest Power x-effects, an econ board for stacked gold cards).

## The core tension (every round)

Two standing tensions:
1. **Have a comp the cards pay off.** Two cards are only as good as the board they buff, so **commit a
   direction by II-3** (a synergy core, an econ stack) that the cards amplify, rather than taking two
   strong-but-off-comp cards.
2. **EXP-vs-quality tradeoff.** Incinerate pays *more* EXP for a *blue* (weaker) card, so occasionally a
   blue card + the level assist beats a purple, weigh the card's power against the EXP when leveling
   matters.

## The card plan (his signature, concrete)

The ordered way to run the engine:
1. **Take his card at I-2** (+4 gold + a random Power Card), banking a bonus card and econ.
2. **Commit a card-scaling direction** by mid Stage II (a synergy core, an econ stack, an item-bundle
   carry).
3. **At II-3, take two cards** that **both pay my comp**: ideally two synergy payoffs (Purest Power +
   Solid Alliance), or a payoff + an econ/level card, or a payoff + an item bundle. This is his power
   spike, two augments at once.
4. **Use the EXP assist** (16 purple / 26 blue) to hit a level breakpoint if I am close, taking a blue
   for the EXP when the level matters more than the marginal card power.
5. **Convert the stacked cards into a board spike** (a buffed synergy, a funded roll-down).

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage: **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]).

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): bank a card, commit a direction
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold. Build toward a card-scaling line (a synergy core or an econ stack). Creep:
  a flexible damage basic.
- **I-2 (power card):** **take his card** (+4 gold + a random Power Card): a bonus card + econ, on-kit.
  Keep it.
- **I-3 / I-4:** bank toward the band; shape the comp the II-3 cards will amplify.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection:** a carry item or a hero opening my line.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): the double-card spike at II-3
- **II-1 (Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a +1 count; commit the synergy/direction.
- **II-3 (the big stage):** **take two Go Go Cards** that both pay my comp (two synergy payoffs, or a
  payoff + an econ/level card); gain the EXP assist. This is his signature double-spike.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** bid for a key item or a Synergy Magic Crystal; the +4 gold helps.
- **Leveling:** the card EXP keeps me on level for the carry's tier.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): convert the cards, spike
- **The stacked II-3 cards pay off** (a doubly-buffed synergy, a funded roll-down); spike the board, take
  the III-3 card too.
- **III-3 / end-of-III Reward Selection:** finish breakpoints, gear the carry.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close
- Lock the card-amplified board, dump banked gold into the last star-ups and items. Close before HP is the
  binding constraint.

## Leveling cadence

The card EXP (16 purple / 26 blue at II-3, or +15 in Rune mode) is a **leveling assist** on top of the 2
free EXP/round, so he hits the carry's tier a touch sooner if he leans into it. The upgrade ladder,
cumulative from L3: `L4: 3 · L5: 11 · L6: 31 · L7: 63 · L8: 103 · L9: 157 · L10: 231`. Use a blue-card EXP
pick to cross a level breakpoint when leveling matters more than the marginal card.

## Best comps (concrete)

**Comps that scale off cards:**
- **A synergy core** (Mage [6], Marksman [6]) where two synergy payoffs (Purest Power + Solid Alliance, or
  Purest Power + a Badge/Blessing) double the spike.
- **An econ stack** where stacked gold/refresh cards compound a wallet into a big roll-down.
- **An item-bundle carry** where two item-bundle cards (The Hunt + Box of Holy Objects) kit a carry fast.
- The constant: **commit a direction the doubled cards amplify**, not a card-less board.

## Power-card deep dive

His card (**+4 gold + a random Power Card**) is **on-kit** for a card specialist, a free extra augment +
econ. Keep it. Deviate only if a synergy-breakpoint or capacity card clearly wins more for a committed
line, but a free card on the card-doubler is thematically and practically strong. Use the 2 refreshes to
confirm.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fast aggro** ends the game before the II-3 double-spike pays, protect HP and commit a direction early.
- **Other card-layer players** (Yu Zhong upgrades card quality): we both lean on cards; my edge is
  *quantity* (two), his is *quality* (upgraded), little direct contention.
- **Synergy commanders** (Lunox, Vale) may share my payoff cards' synergy; prefer an open axis.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **No two strong cards fit my comp:** take the best one + an econ/level card (or a blue for the EXP);
  do not take two off-comp cards just because I can take two.
- **Behind early:** the kit is back-loaded to II-3, so survive with a normal board and commit a direction;
  the double-card spike is the payoff.
- **Comp shifts:** flex; the cards are flexible, take the two that fit the new direction.
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** convert the card power and gold into the board and close.

## The edge (the secret)

**Double the run-shaping at the key commit stage.** Two Go Go Cards at II-3 (plus a bonus card from his
own) means Valir bends his run harder than anyone exactly when comps lock in, two synergy payoffs, or a
payoff + an econ stack, compounding into a spike the single-card field cannot match, with an EXP assist to
stay on level. A card-leveraged flex whose ceiling is high when the comp scales off augments.

## Pitfalls

- **No card-scaling comp** (two cards on a card-less board underdeliver).
- **Taking two off-comp cards** just to use the double (pick two that *pay my line*).
- **Ignoring the EXP tradeoff** (a blue card's EXP can cross a level breakpoint).
- **Over-extending early** before the II-3 payoff.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

**Fast aggro** ends the game before the II-3 double-spike. A game where **no two strong cards fit my line**
blunts him. He is strongest in **card-scaling comps** (synergy cores, econ stacks) that reach II-3 and
III-3; weakest in fast finishes or a card-indifferent board. A niche-but-powerful card specialist whose
power is the mid-game augment double.

## Numbers that matter (the kit, quantified)

- **The double:** **2 Go Go Cards at II-3** (vs the field's 1), plus the card's **+1 random Power Card**, so
  up to **3 augments** more than a normal commander gets, concentrated at the commit stage.
- **The EXP assist:** **16 EXP (purple) / 26 EXP (blue)** at II-3 (or **+15** in Rune mode), a leveling
  boost, with the quirk that a *weaker* (blue) card pays *more* EXP.
- **Refreshes:** each Go Go Card stage has 2 refreshes (one card at a time), so I can dig for the *two*
  that best fit my comp.

- vs **fast aggro / combat** (Lancelot, Ling, Lukas): they end it before II-3 pays, commit early and
  protect HP. My hard matchup.
- vs **card / synergy players** (Yu Zhong, Lunox, Vale): we lean on cards/synergy; my edge is two cards,
  prefer an open payoff axis.
- vs **value engines** (Aurora, Diggie, Vexana): a value race; my card double spikes mid-game, out-tempo.
- vs **vertical / copy** (Alice, Aamon): they spike a carry; I spike via doubled cards.
- vs **econ snowball** (Chou, Cyclops): a gold race; stacked econ cards keep me competitive.
- vs **equipment** (Minotaur, Ruby, Zilong): item-bundle cards let me kit a carry; otherwise I cede gear.
- vs **synergy grant / free-hero** (Gusion, Guinevere): they shape comps; I shape mine with two cards.
