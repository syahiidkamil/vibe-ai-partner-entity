## Snapshot

- **Archetype:** economy / self-leveling tempo. He converts gold into Commander Level, not interest.
- **Plays like:** the lobby's fastest leveler, fielding 4-5 cost heroes and high capacity before anyone
  else, racing to convert that head start into an HP lead before rivals catch up at L10.
- **Difficulty:** medium-high. The kit is simple but the spend-vs-level pivot and the "no bank" auction
  weakness punish autopilot; he is a tempo commander, and tempo asks for good timing.
- **Best into:** slow interest-greed lobbies, value/econ commanders who pay off late (Aurora, Diggie,
  Neobeasts) that he can beat before they mature. **Worst into:** other rushers (Harley, a second
  Lancelot) contesting his high-cost access, and any board that drags the game to a converged-L10 Stage
  IV where his only edge has evaporated.

## The kit (exact)

- **Title:** Lancelot, "Blade of Roses." Skins: Blade of Roses, Blade of Fall. (Note: the **commander**
  Lancelot is the player avatar, distinct from the **hero** Lancelot, a 5-cost Phasewarper/Kishin unit
  in [[../../infos/heroes/cost_5]]. This file is the commander.)
- **Golden Legacy** [Passive] (verbatim): *"No longer earns Interest. Starting from I/II/III and
  subsequent stages, gain 2/3/3 bonus Gold each round. At the end of each round, all Gold is consumed to
  upgrade Lancelot."*
- **Golden Blade** [Passive, the star-up] (verbatim): *"From Stage I/II/III onward, bonus Gold increases
  to 4/5/5."*
- **Power Card:** *Lancelot's Power* (the I-2 offer always includes it) - *"Gain 2 Gold per round upon
  reaching Commander Lv.5."* He has only this one card (no borrowable second).

Reading (in pencil, the wording is terse and carries a real ambiguity): he **earns no interest** and
the kit consumes gold into Commander XP. The load-bearing question is *how much* gold converts. Literal
reading: **all** gold sitting at round end becomes XP, so he **cannot bank a war-chest at all**, he must
spend it the moment he has it (buy, reroll, or it auto-levels him). Softer reading: only the *unspent
remainder* converts. Either way the identity holds: **no interest, no hoarding, fastest leveling**.
Play it as "spend in the moment or it becomes a level," and verify the exact timing in play.

Rethink (2026-07-10, first principles): the trick era is quietly kind to him. Scavenger shop-lock
gold ([[../scavenger_tempo]]) on a normal commander competes with interest banking; on Lancelot
every farmed coin **auto-converts to EXP**, so the trick becomes a guaranteed-tempo engine, and
tempo is his entire thesis, he is the structural version of the edge Aurora only draws by lottery
(her 9-EXP pulls). Two structural dings stand unmoved: the **expiry** (level advantage is a
depreciating asset, worth its maximum the round it is gained and zero at L10 convergence, so every
round of early lead NOT converted into HP damage and streak gold is value destroyed), and the
**auction handicap** (no war-chest means the end-of-stage auctions belong to others; his bids must
come from same-round income). Pilot him as a countdown: the game he wins ends before it converges.

## Win condition / identity

He trades the entire interest economy for **raw leveling speed**, and leveling buys two compounding
edges: **earlier Hero Capacity** (more breakpoints fielded sooner) and **earlier high-tier shop odds**
(4-5 cost heroes appear for him before the lobby). So he wins by **spiking a strong board in the
early-to-mid window and converting it into an HP lead**, before opponents' interest economies mature and
everyone converges on L10. His clock is the *earliest* in the lobby; the whole game is racing that head
start into eliminations before it expires. A Lancelot who is still merely even at Stage IV has already
lost his only structural edge.

## The core tension (every round)

Gold not spent becomes levels, and he earns no interest, so the standing decision is **spend-on-board vs
let-it-level-me**, with a hard asymmetry no other commander has: **there is no third option of
banking**. The skill is timing the pivot:
- **Early:** let the drift level you. Rerolling at L3-5 fishes a shop that is 50-60% 1-cost, so reroll
  gold is near-wasted; let it ride into levels (which is exactly what the kit wants).
- **At the comp's target level:** *stop feeding levels and dump gold into rerolls*. Past the tier your
  carry lives in, more levels only **dilute the shop** for the heroes you want, and you have no interest
  to protect, so reroll-to-hit is strictly correct. The mistake is auto-leveling past target out of
  habit.

## The level-vs-roll pivot (his signature mechanic, concrete)

The one decision that defines piloting him. The trigger to **stop leveling and start rolling** is:
*Commander Level == the level where my carry's cost tier peaks.* From the shop clock
([[../../infos/encyclopedia/hero]]):
- **3-cost carry plan:** peak odds at **L6 (40%)**. Drift to L6, then roll, do not over-level (L8+
  dilutes 3-cost hard).
- **4-cost carry plan:** peak at **L9 (26%)**, strong at **L8 (18%)**. Push to L8-9 fast (his edge), then
  roll there.
- **5-cost capstone:** opens L7 (1%), real at **L9-10 (14-25%)**. Rush to L9-10 and roll.

Because his gold auto-converts, the danger is **overshooting the target level**. The rule: once at
target, deliberately *spend down to ~0 each round on rerolls* so nothing is left to auto-level past it.
He is the one commander where "I have leftover gold" is a mistake, not a cushion.

## The HP economy (he protects, he does not farm losses)

Unlike the lose-early commanders (Minotaur, Nana, the value engines), Lancelot is a **tempo** commander:
his early board is *strong* (he out-levels into capacity and high-cost heroes), so he should **win
rounds and protect HP**, not deliberately bleed it. The lowest-HP-first reward priority is not his line;
his line is being ahead on board and banking a win streak. He only takes the loss-streak detour if a
genuine lowroll leaves his board unable to win (then lose *cheap* Stage I-II HP, same as anyone). His HP
is a lead to press, not a currency to spend.

## Stage-by-stage, round by round

Base DMG by stage (HP cost of a loss): **I = 3 · II = 5-6 · III = 7-11 · IV = 13-15**
([[../../infos/encyclopedia/commander]]). Calendar from [[../../infos/rounds/index]].

### Stage I (rounds 1-4): ride the drift, do not roll
- **I-1 (creep):** L3, 2 gold + the +2 Golden Legacy bonus. Buy the strongest 1-2 cost bodies toward an
  open class [2]. Creep pick: a flexible damage/ATK-speed basic (Inspire or Retribution) earmarked for
  the eventual carry. Expect to reach **L4-5 faster than the lobby** off the bonus gold + auto-level.
- **I-2 (power card):** his exclusive (+2g/round at L5) is **weak** for him, it just adds gold he will
  spend or auto-level with, it does not amplify the leveling edge. **Deviate.** Use the 2 refreshes
  (one card at a time) to fish a card that *leverages the level lead*: **Reinforcement+** (a 2-star
  4-cost, reachable because his odds/level are ahead), **Peerless Valor** (4-5 cost ATK Speed), a
  **Synergy Badge/Blessing** if committing, or **Fanny's Power** (+1 capacity).
- **I-3 / I-4:** keep buying toward [2]s; do **not** mass-reroll (odds too low early, and reroll gold is
  the one thing otherwise "free" via levels). Let gold ride to ~L5-6.
- **End of Stage I, Reward Selection** (lowest-HP-first, 8 equipment + 3 heroes): I am usually *not*
  lowest HP (strong early board), so I pick later, take the best generic carry item or a hero opening my
  line.

### Stage II (rounds 5-10): the commit window, with first-access as the weapon
- **II-1 (Blessing Shifter + Blessing live):** combine a 2-star for a free **+1 synergy count**; place
  it on a synergy one short of a breakpoint. The Golden Blade star-up has likely landed, so the bonus is
  now **+5/round**.
- **The signature play:** my level lead means I see 3-4 cost heroes *before* slow-rollers, so **race the
  lobby to a contested high-cost carry and grab it first**. This is where first-access wins games.
- **II-3 (Go Go Card + creep):** take a card that converts level into board (Reinforcement+, a synergy
  payoff). Creep: a carry or front item.
- **End of Stage II, Auction:** I am **broke by design** (gold auto-levels, no bank), so **do not expect
  to outbid** anyone. Grab one cheap useful lot (a fitting item, a Mirror Device at base price) or pass.
  Plan the comp so the auction is *not* load-bearing, a real structural weakness to design around.
- **Leveling:** push to **L7-8 by end of II**, ahead of curve.

### Stage III (rounds 11-16): spike and take the lead
- **Level to the carry's tier and then roll, not level** (the pivot above). L8-9 for 4-costs.
- **III-3 (Go Go Card + creep + 2nd Blessing Shifter):** take Purest Power (if 6-stacking) or a carry
  item / synergy payoff; re-point the Blessing onto a live breakpoint.
- **Spike:** roll the auto-converting gold into star-ups and copies. HP is now expensive (base 7-11), so
  the board must genuinely win rounds; press the HP lead the early board built.
- **End of Stage III, Reward Selection:** finish a breakpoint or grab a synergy-grant orb.

### Stage IV (rounds 17+): finalize and close fast
- I hit **L10 early**; full board. All-in is natural (I cannot bank anyway): dump every coin into the
  final star-ups and the best items on the carry. Base DMG 13-15 + Battle Acceleration means finalize
  before this stage, not in it. **Close while my board is still ahead of the converging lobby**, because
  once everyone is L10 my only edge is gone.

He climbs this ladder **faster than anyone** (bonus gold + auto-convert). Early-mid: let gold ride into
levels. The pivot: **at the comp's target level, stop feeding and reroll**, because past target the
extra levels dilute the shop with no interest payoff to justify them. He should hit L10 a round or two
ahead of the field; the question is never "can I level" but "have I stopped in time to roll for copies."

## Best comps (concrete)

Anything that wants to be **high-level early**, i.e. high-cost carries he accesses first:
- **Mage core** ([[../mage_core]]): field Kagura (4c) and Odette (5c) earlier than rivals; Cecilion (1c)
  cheap-spike; Lylia/Vexana/Julian fill Mage [6]; Stargazer bodies (Aurora/Pharsa) for mana.
- **Marksman carry** ([[../marksman_carry]]): seat Karrie/Irithel (4c) and Lesley (5c) ahead of curve.
- **Astro Power vertical:** Odette as the Sovereign capstone, fielded early.
- **Avoid** pure cheap-3-star verticals (Cecilion-only, a 1-cost rush): his gold will not sit at a low
  level to slow-roll copies, so the cheap-vertical engine fights his kit.

## Positioning

Standard, with one note: his early high-cost carry is a fat target, so seat it **back-center, never
naked in a corner** (most Swiftblade heroes, Saber/Helcurt/Gusion/Ling, dive the back-row side). Wall
the front with 2-3 tanks, corner-guard against a Swiftblade lobby, spread vs AoE/the Dragoncaller
dragon. Full detail: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/positioning]].

## A worked line (one ideal game)

I-1 L3: buy two 1-cost bodies for a class [2], take Inspire off the creep. I-2: skip the weak +2g
exclusive, refresh into **Reinforcement+** (a 2-star 4-cost) and take it, banking a carry the lobby
cannot field yet. Stage I close: L5 already (lobby at L4). II: bonus is +5/round; I out-level to L7 by
II-5 and **buy the contested 4-cost mage before the slow-rollers reach the tier**. Auction: I am broke,
grab one cheap fitting item, pass the rest. III: I pivot, stop leveling at L8, roll down hard (free for
me, no interest lost) to 2-star the carry and cross Mage [6]; take Purest Power off III-3. I run a win
streak through III on the strongest mid-game board in the lobby, pressing HP. IV: hit L10 first, dump
the bank into Odette + items, close on the converging field before their economies pay off. 1st.

## Tier lists (for Lancelot)

The tier list as it lives *here*, in Lancelot's form of life (the general [[../tier_lists]] is only the
upward aggregate, set it aside when piloting him). In pencil:
- **Synergies:** up-rank lines that want **early high-cost power**: **Mage** (Kagura/Odette early),
  **Marksman** (Karrie/Lesley early), **Astro Power** (the Sovereign capstone). Down-rank slow cheap-
  3-star verticals (he cannot slow-roll). Frontline [2]s (Defender/Dauntless) stay A as the wall.
- **Heroes by phase:**
  - **Early:** Cecilion, Gatotkaca, Brody, Tigreal (A), used to hold a board while leveling, not as the
    plan.
  - **Mid:** the **4-cost carries rise a full tier for him** (first-access edge): Kagura, Karrie,
    Irithel, Vexana, Pharsa (S), grab them before the lobby reaches the tier.
  - **Late:** Odette, Lesley, Ling, Ruby (S), the 5-cost capstones he reaches at L9-10 ahead of curve.
  - Cheap-vertical heroes (a 1-cost you would 3-star) **fall** for him.
- **Go Go Cards:** **Reinforcement+ and Peerless Valor S** (leverage the level lead into 4-5 cost
  power), **Fanny's Power S** (+1 capacity compounds the leveling edge). Purest Power S if 6-stacking.
  **Down-rank econ-bank cards he cannot use** (Trust Fund, Vault Upgrade+, Payday) and **his own flat-
  gold exclusive**, the worst pick on his own card.
- **Equipment:** standard; concentrate damage on the early-fielded carry (no kit synergy with gear).
  Synergy Magic Crystal and Mirror Device stay S as universal levers.
- **Stage I basics (the I-1 creep, pick 1 of 3):** **Retribution** or **Inspire** for the early carry
  (his board is strong, so go offensive); **Aegis** only if a lowroll has him bleeding; **Purify** vs a
  CC lobby on the carry; **Revitalize** lowest (Battle Acceleration cuts healing).

## Power-card deep dive

His exclusive (*+2 Gold/round at L5*) is one of the **weakest exclusives in the roster for its owner**:
it is flat gold that either gets spent or auto-leveled, and it does nothing for his actual edge
(leveling and first-access). The I-2 offer guarantees it, but I should almost always **refresh past it**
(2 refreshes, one card at a time) toward a card that turns the level lead into board: **Reinforcement+**
(a 2-star 4-cost, which his ahead-of-curve level/odds make reachable), **Peerless Valor** (4-5 cost ATK
Speed), **Fanny's Power** (+1 capacity), or a **synergy Badge/Blessing** if committing a [6]. Keep the
exclusive only if the refreshes whiff and nothing better appears.

## What to watch from other players (matchups)

- **Fat-econ / late-scaling commanders** (Aurora, Diggie, Cyclops, Neobeasts boards): they out-resource
  me **late**, after the lobby converges on L10 and my level edge is gone. I must convert my early lead
  into eliminations and an HP cushion **before** then. They are the clock I am racing.
- **Other rushers** (Harley floods 5-costs from II-2; a second Lancelot): they contest my high-cost tier
  access, and if two of us race the same 4-cost the pool starves. Read it and pivot to an open high-cost
  line.
- **Use first-access aggressively:** snipe contested 4-5 cost carries while slow-rollers are still
  low-level, the advanced-tactics denial play paid for by my level lead.
- Reading the field: [[../../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]].

## Contingency and pivots

- **Behind on board (lowroll):** **roll down immediately** to stabilize, and here is his hidden
  strength: rerolling is **"free" for me relative to the lobby** because I have no interest to sacrifice.
  Everyone else pays +4/round to roll; I pay nothing extra. Aggressive stabilization rolling is my
  privilege, use it.
- **Highroll:** press hard, I am ahead on level *and* board, the ideal state, snowball the HP lead and
  push for 1st.
- **Carry contested:** my early tier access usually wins the race, buy it before they reach the tier; if
  two rushers are on it, pivot to an open high-cost line (cheap because I keep a shared breakpoint).
- **Stage IV, not finalized:** no pivots; double down, dump the bank into the one spike that wins the
  next rounds. Holding gold here is moot (I cannot bank), so all-in is the natural and correct line.

## The edge (the secret)

Two things only Lancelot has: (1) **rerolling costs him no interest**, so he can roll to stabilize or
hit copies at any moment without the econ penalty every other commander pays; (2) **earliest access to
high-tier heroes** via a permanent level lead, so he gets **first pick of contested 4-5 cost carries**.
Played right, he fields the lobby's strongest board *earliest*, and turns that window into HP before the
field catches up.

## Pitfalls

- Trying to **hoard** (impossible, gold leaves) or piloting him like an interest commander.
- **Over-leveling past the target tier** (dilutes the shop, wastes gold, no interest payoff). The single
  most common Lancelot mistake.
- **Expecting to win the auction** (broke by design); planning a comp that needs an auction win.
- A **cheap-3-star vertical** (his gold will not sit at a low level to slow-roll copies).
- **Coasting at even into Stage IV**, letting the clock erase his only edge.

## Threat assessment (who beats him, and why)

His danger is the **clock running out**: everyone reaches L10 eventually, erasing his only structural
edge, so any commander that **out-scales late** beats him if he has not already converted the early lead
into eliminations and an HP cushion, the value/econ engines (**Aurora, Diggie, Cyclops**) and
**Neobeasts** boards. **Harley** directly contests his high-cost access (floods 5-costs from II-2). A
second **Lancelot** or any rusher starves his tier race. He is strongest into slow interest-greed
lobbies he can out-tempo; weakest into a long, converged game. His best games are **decided by Stage
III**; a Lancelot still even at Stage IV has already lost.

## Numbers that matter (his edge, quantified)

- **First-access window:** at L8 the 4-cost odds are **18%** and at L9 **26%** ([[../../infos/encyclopedia/hero]]);
  he reaches L8-9 a round or two before slow-rollers, so he *sees and buys* the 4-cost carry while they
  are still at L6-7 (4-cost 5-10%). That gap is the whole edge, and it closes once the lobby converges.
- **The reroll "discount":** every other commander pays the **+4/round interest** they forgo by spending
  below 20 gold; Lancelot earns **no interest**, so a roll-down costs him nothing extra. He can stabilize
  by rolling at any moment that others cannot afford to.
- **Mage spike for reference:** Mage **[4]** = +20% Magic ATK to all, +80% to mages; **[6]** = +30% /
  +120%. Purest Power I/II = +20% / +28% Hybrid ATK to the 6-count. That is the payoff his early level
  lead lets him reach first.

## Quick reference (TL;DR)

- **Identity:** no interest, no bank, fastest leveler. Convert the early level lead into HP before the
  lobby converges on L10.
- **Power card:** refresh past his weak +2g exclusive; take Reinforcement+ / Peerless Valor / Fanny's
  Power / a synergy spike.
- **Levels:** let gold ride into levels early; **stop at the comp's target level and roll** (overshoot
  is the cardinal sin). 3-cost -> L6, 4-cost -> L8-9, 5-cost -> L9-10.
- **Signature play:** race the lobby to a contested 4-5 cost carry and grab it first (first-access).
- **Econ:** broke by design, do not plan around the auction; rerolling is "free" for him (no interest
  lost), so roll to stabilize freely.
- **Comps:** high-cost carry lines (mage core, marksman, Astro). Avoid cheap-3-star verticals.
- **Win by Stage III**; close before the field reaches L10 and erases his edge.
