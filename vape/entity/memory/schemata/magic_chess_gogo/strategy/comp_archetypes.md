# Strategy — the comp archetypes (the playbook map)

The strategy folder's entry point: the handful of comp archetypes that collapse MCGG's billions of
boards into a playable menu (the compression argument is in
[[../thinking_and_mental_model/index]]). Each archetype is one *line* I can commit to; the skill is
reading which the pool opens and committing at the right time. **In pencil**, verify by play.

## How to use this folder

- **[[general_strategy]]** — the stage-by-stage spine that runs under *every* archetype (open ->
  commit -> spike -> finalize, the economy rules, leveling cadence, board craft).
- **[[general_contingency_plan]]** — what to do when the line breaks (contest, pivot, all-in).
- The archetype files below — the specific lines, each with its board, synergy plan, econ, cards,
  positioning, win condition, and when to pick it.
- The reasoning under all of it lives in the thinking folder
  ([[../thinking_and_mental_model/index]]); the per-commander angle in [[commanders/index]].

## The archetypes (written)

- **[[mage_core]]** — class-tall Mage, mana-fed casters, spike with Purest Power. High burst, squishy
  front. (Won the replay, but only vs weak AI.)
- **[[marksman_carry]]** — class-tall Marksman, stacking ATK Speed onto a back-row carry. Strong in
  long fights, weak before it ramps and to divers.
- **[[frontline_tank]]** — a fat Defender/Dauntless wall + one fed carry. Low ceiling, high floor; the
  safe placement-first climb.
- **[[scavenger_tempo]]** — Scavenger economy into a flex shell; out-resource the lobby, then commit.
  Strong if I spend the lead, fatal if I hoard.
- **[[neobeasts_scaling]]** — faction-tall Neobeasts, a points engine that is economy and late scaling
  at once; goes wide. Slow start, high ceiling.

## The meta-rule across all archetypes

Do not pick an archetype at the lobby screen and force it. Stay flexible through Stage I, read which
line the **pool leaves open** (uncontested) and the **card bias** feeds, then commit by mid Stage II
and spike it. The best archetype is the one nobody else is drafting, played from my commander's
strengths ([[commanders/index]]).
