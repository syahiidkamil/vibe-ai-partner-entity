# Strategy — the general tier list (emergent, bottom-up)

What this is, and what it is **not**. This general list is an **aggregate that arises from the
particular per-commander tier lists** ([[commanders/index]]), not a baseline they are derived from. The
**particular comes first** (a tier only means something inside a way of playing); this general view is
**synthesized upward** from those particulars, as a learning-and-climbing aid. It is built so far from
only a handful of commander particulars, so it is **preliminary** and sharpens as more land.

## How the general list is ranked (the three axes)

A piece sits high *in general* only when it is good **across commanders and situations**, scored on:
- **Consistency** — does it perform reliably game to game, not just on a highroll? Low-variance value.
- **Spike consistency** — when the plan calls for a power spike, does it reliably deliver one (a
  breakpoint that flips a fight, a card that wins the round when taken)?
- **Versatility across situations** — does it work under many commanders, in many comps, against many
  boards, rather than only one narrow setup?

High on all three = generally strong. But "generally" is doing all the work, see the caveat.

## The caveat (forms of life) — when this list is meaningless

Wittgenstein: a word's meaning is its use inside a form of life; strip the practice and it is noise. A
tier is the same, **it only means something inside a way of playing.** So:
- Under a **chosen commander**, the general list can be **outright meaningless**: a C-tier-general
  synergy can be S-tier for the commander built around it; Aamon makes a Mirror Device (a niche item)
  his best pick; Minotaur re-ranks all equipment; Lunox makes a 6-stackable synergy paramount.
- It depends on **context and situation**: what is contested, the stage, the seven enemy boards.

So this general list is a **default for when I have no commander-specific read**, a ladder to **learn
and climb fast**, never a law. The instant I commit a commander I switch to **its** particular tier
list, which overrides this. (Pencil; schema disclaimer: [[../disclaimer]].)

## Why the particular is what I actually use (the 8-commander fact)

A match has **8 players and, by the draft/ban phase, up to 8 *unique* commanders** (the ban vote plus
the commander draft imply no duplicate commanders in a lobby; high-confidence, verify by play). So I
ever only **pilot one commander** and **read up to seven distinct others**. While playing I never need a
ranking "across all 37 commanders", I need **my** commander's particular tier list plus a read on the
seven enemies ([[../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]]). That is the whole reason the
particulars are primary and this general view is just the learning ladder. A **huge pile of particular
context is fine, even intended**, because in play I load only the one slice I need.

## The emergent rankings (the learn-and-climb default only)

What *tends* to be consistent + spike-consistent + versatile across commanders. Re-rank the moment a
commander or the board says otherwise.

### Synergies (rosters: [[../infos/synergies/synergies]])
- **S (versatile + consistent):** Mage, Dauntless (team shields), Scavenger (econ), Neobeasts (econ +
  scaling), Marksman.
- **A:** Defender, Astro Power, Exorcist (high spike ceiling), Stargazer (caster enabler), Bruiser,
  Emberlord.
- **B:** Swiftblade, Weapon Master, Dragoncaller, Kishin, Phasewarper, Heartbond (needs tile-pairing).
- **C / niche (S in their niche):** Enchanted Tales, Mystic Meow (go-wide only), Northern Vale (tiny).

### Heroes by phase (rosters: [[../infos/heroes/index]])
- **Early:** Cecilion, Gatotkaca, Brody (S); Phoveus, Angela, Tigreal, Lylia (A).
- **Mid:** Franco, Julian, Kagura, Vexana, Karrie (S); Miya/Saber/Helcurt, Hilda/Khufra, Esmeralda,
  Pharsa (A).
- **Late:** Odette, Ruby, Ling, Lesley (S); Irithel, Lunox, Yu Zhong, Claude, Alpha/Bane/Badang (A).

### Go Go Cards (lists: [[../infos/gogo_cards/index]])
- **S (near-universal):** Fanny's Power (+1 capacity), Synergy Badge/Blessing (a bought breakpoint),
  Purest Power (6-count spike), Reinforcement+ (2-star 4-cost), Trust Fund (econ).
- **A:** Vault Upgrade+, Payday, Solid Alliance, Peerless Valor, Technological Revolution+, item
  bundles (The Hunt, Box of Holy Objects), the matching commander exclusive when on-kit.
- **B:** refresh cards (Glittering Jewels, Golden Era+), hero pulls (Airdrop, Excellent Recruit),
  Thickened Blood III, Fast Promotion, the faction-turbo cards (S on their faction).

### Equipment (full named list: [[../infos/equipments/regular]]; model: [[../infos/equipment_and_economy]])
- **S (levers):** Synergy Magic Crystal (a +1 count = a breakpoint), Mirror Device (a free copy),
  Claude's Theft Device (copies 2 items = effectively 3 pieces).
- **A (carry damage, by role):** marksman / basic-attacker — Golden Staff (stacking ATK Speed), Demon
  Hunter Sword, Haas' Claws, Blade of Despair, Berserker's Fury, Sea Halberd; mage — Holy Crystal,
  Glowing Wand, Divine Glaive, Ice Queen Wand; caster mana — Spellblade Talisman, Purple Buff. Enhanced
  over Basic, always.
- **A (front survival):** Guardian Helmet, Winter Crown (low-HP invuln), Antique Cuirass, Blade Armor,
  Thunder Belt, Oracle (row DEF), Dominance Ice, Ancient Wrath (taunt).
- **Stage I basics (the I-1 creep: pick 1 of 3 of the 5 consumable actives):** **Retribution** (flat
  DMG, scales with stage) and **Inspire** (ATK Speed, scales) are the default damage picks for a
  carry-forward start; **Aegis** (battle-start shield to self + adjacent) to stabilize HP early;
  **Purify** (control immunity + ATK Speed) situationally vs a CC lobby or to keep a key carry
  uncontrollable; **Revitalize** (low-HP heal) lowest, since Battle Acceleration cuts healing. The pick
  is board-dependent: damage if pushing a carry, Aegis if bleeding, Purify if I fear CC on the carry.
- **B:** generic stat items off-role; a front piece on the wrong unit.

## The real tiers live per-commander

This page is the ladder for learning. The tiers I actually trust are the **particular** ones in each
commander file ([[commanders/index]]), each grounded in that commander's form of life. As those grow,
this aggregate is re-synthesized from them, never the other way around.
