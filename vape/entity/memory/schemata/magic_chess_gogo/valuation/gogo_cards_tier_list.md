# Go Go Cards Tier List — by rarity band, read through multiple lenses (MCGG S6)

A valuation of the Go Go Cards (the augment-like powers picked from a small hand at I-2, II-3, III-3).
Organized **by rarity band first** (Blue -> Purple -> Orange/"Gold" -> Power), because that is the fair
**apple-to-apple** bracket and the picker's own tabs; within a band the cards are tiered S/A/B. But the tier
is never the whole story: **one card is several things at once**, and **cards and strategy feed each other
both ways.** So this file leads with two framings, then the bands, then how to actually pick. Part of the
valuation folder beside [[commander_tier_list]], the phase lists, and [[equipments_tier_list]]. Sources:
[[../infos/gogo_cards/power_cards]], [[../infos/gogo_cards/generic_cards]], [[../infos/gogo_cards/index]].

## Disclaimer — why a single tier lies here

Card value is the most context-dependent thing in this folder, for two structural reasons that no S/A/B can
capture on its own:

1. **Card and strategy are a loop, not a one-way street.** Your committed line decides which card is S *for
   you* (a lifesteal card is S on a sustain board, dead on a burst board). But a high-magnitude card can run
   the loop the other way and **pivot your line** (a free 2-star 4-cost, or a full item bundle, is permission
   to change plans). So a tier is always "S *given a fit*," and a great card can **create** the fit.
2. **One card has many lenses.** A card is rarely one use. The same card can be a board body, or gold (sold),
   or a synergy count, or a compounding engine, or a pivot. Boxing it into one use-case is the mistake; you
   score a card on its **best applicable lens for your moment.**

So the offer is also synergy-biased and refreshable (**2 refreshes**, re-roll one of the two at a time), and
timing decides everything. Held **in pencil**, reasoned not lived ([[../disclaimer]]).

## The lenses — what a card can be

- **board** — units/stats that win the round now (tempo).
- **gold** — convertible to gold: a direct payout, or the **sell floor** (below).
- **synergy** — a Magic Crystal / Blessing / Badge = a bought +1 synergy count (a breakpoint).
- **engine** — compounds over rounds (Trust Fund, Rising Festivities, the econ Power Cards).
- **capacity** — +1 board slot (the rarest, most universal effect).
- **pivot** — opens or redirects a strategy (a 4-cost hero, an item bundle that defines a carry).
- **fit** — payoff only inside a specific comp (faction-turbo, Purest Power, lifesteal).

**The sell floor (a lens on every hero/item card).** A hero card is never zero even off-comp: it is a body, a
synergy filler, *or* gold sold back. Refund is roughly the gold invested — **Kamil's read: a 2-Star 4-Gold
sells for ~12g** (3 copies x 4); exact refund is **unverified in the client, in pencil**. This floor is why
"random hero" cards are safer than they look.

## Two worked examples (why boxing by use-case fails)

- **Rising Festivities** [Purple]. *Box:* "recruitment." *Real, multi-lens:* an **engine** (a free hero every
  round), a **gold** stream (sell each), a **synergy**-filler source, and a **Scavenger amplifier**. Its
  randomness is hedged by the sell floor — an off-comp hero is still gold. S on a sell-disciplined / Scavenger
  line, A elsewhere.
- **Reinforcement+** [Purple]. *Box:* "a 2-star 4-cost." *Real:* a **board** spike now, *or* a ~12g **gold**
  floor if the rolled hero is off-comp, *or* a **pivot** into that 4-cost's synergy. The randomness hurts the
  *board* lens but not the *gold* lens — so the worst case is still ~12g, the best case is a mid spike or a
  new line.

## The decision procedure (how to actually pick, given the loop)

A tier alone does not pick the card; this does. At each selection:

1. **Name your state: line committed, or open?**
   - **Open (usually early):** weight **pivot · engine · capacity** — take the card that *sets* a strong line
     or compounds. This is where the loop runs card -> strategy: a 2-star 4-cost or an item bundle can *be*
     your decision.
   - **Committed (usually mid/late):** weight **fit · board · synergy** — take what spikes the line you are
     already on. The loop runs strategy -> card.
2. **Score each offered card on its BEST lens for your state**, not its generic tier. A Purple that hits your
   breakpoint beats an Orange that does not.
3. **Break ties with the floor.** Prefer the card whose *worst case* is still gold or a body (the sell floor),
   over a narrow card that bricks if it misses.
4. **Refresh toward the band you need** (rarity = magnitude), but never refresh away a card that fits for one
   that is merely rarer.
5. **Let a big off-line card pivot you.** A high-magnitude card outside your current plan is not a wasted pick
   — it is the loop offering a better line. Take it *if* the pivot beats your current line, sell/bench what no
   longer fits (the floor makes that cheap).
