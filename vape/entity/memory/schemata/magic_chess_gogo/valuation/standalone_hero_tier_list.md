# Standalone Hero Tier List — raw 1v1 power, clustered by cost (MCGG S6)

A different axis from the rest of the valuation folder. The phase lists ([[early_game_tier_list]],
[[middle_game_tier_list]], [[end_game_tier_list]]) rank heroes *in a comp* (accessibility, breakpoints,
win-cons); this one strips all that away and asks one question: **put two heroes alone on an empty board
and let them fight, who wins?** Raw power from **stats + skill**, no synergies, no items, no positioning,
no focus-fire. Clustered **by gold cost**, because cost is the fair bracket (a 1-cost should not be judged
against a 5-cost). **In pencil**, from the datamined stats and skill text, not yet lived ([[../disclaimer]]).

## What wins a 1v1 (the duel model)

Each hero is read on five duel levers, off its per-star stat block ([[../infos/heroes/index]]) and skill:
- **Burst** — skill multiplier x ATK. Can it delete the other before trading blows? (the nuke check)
- **Sustain** — lifesteal, self-heal, shields. Does it win attrition?
- **EHP** — the `EHP` column (HP x mitigation). Does it survive long enough to act?
- **CC & lock** — stun / freeze / taunt / knock-up. **Control wins duels** (act while the other can't).
- **Immunity & mobility** — control-immunity, untargetability, dashes. Can it dodge the lock, or close on a
  squishy before it casts?

**Reference star: 2-Star** (the fair common state). One structural rule falls out and matters a lot: at
2-star a **bruiser/tank walks up and kills a glass nuker** before the nuke one-shots it, so back-row glass
cannons rank *low* here despite huge numbers. At **3-Star** that flips (the nuke starts one-shotting), so
the glass nukes and the 5-costs jump tiers; see the spike section.

## The caveat (forms of life)

This list is the most "stripped of practice" of all of them, so it is the **least like the real game** -
MCGG is never 1v1, it is eight boards with synergies, items, and positioning. A 1v1 monster that needs no
team (Esmeralda, Ruby) and a 1v1 weakling that defines a comp (Cecilion, Angela, Brody) can sit a world
apart here from where they sit in play. Use this to feel a unit's **innate combat weight**, then go to the
phase/comp lists for how it actually earns its slot.

## Cost 1 — the cheap brawlers

Tanky self-sustaining fronts beat the glass back-row here; nobody one-shots at this tier.
- **S** — **Phoveus** (4750 EHP, self-shield, *lowers the enemy's ATK 10%*, +250% burst: the best attrition
  duelist) · **Alucard** (+500% single-target nuke ~825 at 2-star, 4306 EHP, Weapon-Master lifesteal).
- **A** — **Dyrroth** (lifesteal bruiser, +270% enhanced hit) · **Minotaur** (highest 1-cost EHP 4750 + a
  20%-Max-HP self-heal; outlasts, but kills slowly) · **Silvanna** (shield + 30% ATK-speed steroid).
- **B** — **Cecilion** (the biggest 1-cost nuke, ~882 magic, but glass: wins the ranged mirror, loses to any
  front) · **Brody** (ramps via stacking vuln + ATK-speed, but squishy and slow to start).
- **C** — **Eudora** (low burst for a glass unit, no sustain; the weakest 1-cost duelist).

## Cost 2 — the bridge brawlers

- **S** — **Gatotkaca** (double-class tank, 5225 EHP, a 400% nuke *and* a 400 heal: the cleanest 2-cost
  duelist) · **Fanny** (~990 AoE-on-one burst + 30% **anti-heal** that guts sustain foes, 4736 EHP).
- **A** — **Belerick** (tanky, heals 10% lost HP per cast) · **Hanzo** (self-heal + ramping skill DMG + 40
  mana = casts fast) · **Martis** (tank + shield + 365% hit) · **Balmond** (5225 EHP, 14-strike grind).
- **B** — **Selena** (~1040 single-target nuke, the biggest 2-cost delete, but glass) · **Lylia** (~936 nuke,
  glass) · **Granger** (600% 3-shot burst, glass) · **Joy** (control-immune dash + DEF-shred, slow mana).
- **C** — **Angela** (a healer; her skill heals *allies*, so near-zero solo kill power: weakest 1v1).

## Cost 3 — the workhorse duelists

The contested middle; CC + tank + burst peaks here.
- **S** — **Khufra** (a 1s **stun** + 300 shield + ~1168 burst on a 6650-EHP body; stun-locks then deletes,
  and 3-star ramps the nuke to 970%) · **Julian** (double-class front *caster*: 6650 EHP, a self-shield, and
  a damage-zone that grinds — tanky and damaging at once).
- **A** — **Tigreal** (0.6s stun + 6650 EHP + 315%) · **Masha** (70% ATK-speed steroid + lifesteal sustain) ·
  **Fredrinn** (shield + a 420% enhanced basic, 6028 EHP) · **Helcurt** (blink to the back + ATK-speed +
  5-stinger burst) · **Saber** (5-sword AoE on a 6028-EHP front).
- **B** — **Hilda** (the fattest 3-cost, 6650 EHP, but slow to kill) · **Franco** (tank, 6650 EHP, 100 mana =
  slow first cast) · **Luo Yi** (stun + ramping skill DMG, but squishy 4687 EHP) · **Clint** (625% burst,
  glass) · **Miya** (ramping auto-attacker, squishy back-row).

## Cost 4 — the carries (where 3-star starts to matter most)

- **S** — **Esmeralda** (8611 EHP, a 0.5s stun, a shield, and hybrid-damage basics; 3-star goes to 1320% /
  21,289 EHP — the best all-round 4-cost duelist) · **Terizla** (the tankiest 4-cost, 9500 EHP, a knock-up;
  3-star 1300% / 23,487 EHP) · **Kagura** (~1540 AoE burst at 2-star, **1700%** at 3-star — one-shots most
  things, the burst apex of the tier).
- **A** — **Leomord** (control-immune + 40% DMG-reduction while charging + 480% hit) · **Karrie** (%-pen
  dual-wield: the anti-tank that *shreds the fat duelists* the rest of the tier relies on) · **Lunox**
  (mobile dash + snowball casts on kill, 40 mana = fast) · **Yu Zhong** (8611-EHP sustained bruiser) ·
  **Gusion** (dash-in burst assassin).
- **B** — **Aurora** (mediocre at 2-star, but **S-tier at 3-star**: 500% + 13,795 HP) · **Irithel** (huge
  ramped basic-DPS, but 100 mana slows the first cast and she is back-row squishy) · **Pharsa** (sustained
  bombardment, glass) · **Vexana** (knock-up + a summoned guard, but glass).

## Cost 5 — the legendaries (all strong; the 3-star is nuclear)

At 2-star the CC-tanks win; at 3-star the HP explosion makes every one of these a monster.
- **S** — **Alpha** (the apex: 3-star is **55,590 HP / 870 ATK / +900%**; even at 2-star a 9903-EHP wall with
  ramping AoE) · **Ruby** (10,925 EHP, a 3s **taunt**, lifesteal stacks, a damage counter — the best raw
  survivor-killer) · **Bane** (10,925 EHP + a **1.2s freeze**, the longest hard-CC in the game; locks then
  kills).
- **A** — **Lancelot** (multi-dash + phantom trails, 775 ATK at 3-star) · **Odette** (control-immune
  sustained-AoE channel + a 500 shield) · **Badang** (control-immune while punching + a damage counter,
  9903 EHP) · **Ling** (an untargetable perch + dive that picks its target) · **Lesley** (45% base crit, a
  3-star 1000%+crit snipe — the back-row deleter, if it survives to fire).
- **B** — **Claude** (squishiest 5-cost at 2-star, but summons Dexter for *two* carry bodies, and 3-star is
  30,944 HP / 53,047 EHP — a B at 2-star, an S once starred).

## The 3-Star spike — heroes whose duel rank jumps when starred

Raw power is star-dependent, so the 2-star ranking re-orders hard at 3-star. The biggest jumpers (the skill
multiplier alone): **Kagura** 770->**1700%**, **Esmeralda** 400->**1320%**, **Terizla** 300->**1300%**,
**Leomord** 480->**1425%**, **Bane** 240->**1100%**, **Lunox** 380->**1100%**, **Lesley** 200->**1000%**,
**Khufra** 510->**970%**, **Aurora** 200->**500%**, **Ruby** 160->**600%**; plus every 5-cost's HP
explosion (Alpha 8,070->55,590 HP). And structurally: **glass nukers** (Cecilion, Selena, Lylia, Kagura,
Pharsa, Lesley) flip from 1v1-*losers* at 2-star to 1v1-*winners* at 3-star, because the nuke crosses the
one-shot line. So "raw power" is really two lists: a 2-star one (CC-tanks rule) and a 3-star one (the
nukers and legendaries rule).

## A note on reading across tiers

Tiers here are **within a cost bracket** — a Cost-1 "S" is not a Cost-5 "S". Across brackets, higher cost is
simply stronger at equal star (that is what the gold buys, and the stat tables confirm it: a 2-star 5-cost
out-stats a 2-star 1-cost on every line). The brackets exist so the comparison is fair, not to imply a
1-cost S rivals a 5-cost S.
