# Early-Game Tier List — Stage I (rounds 1-4), Commander Lv.3-5 (MCGG S6)

The **early phase** valuation: which synergies and heroes carry the opening, when the shop is almost
all 1-2 cost and a little 3-cost. Part of a three-file set with [[middle_game_tier_list]] and
[[end_game_tier_list]]; the **commander** layer is [[commander_tier_list]], the piece-level baseline
[[../strategy/tier_lists]]. **In pencil**: scored from the kits, the stats, and the draw-odds, reasoned
**not yet lived** ([[../disclaimer]]). Re-rank by play.

## What "early" is (the phase frame)

- **Rounds 1-4 (Stage I)**, Commander Lv.3-5. Capacity is tiny (a handful of board slots).
- **The shop is the cheap tiers.** Draw odds ([[../infos/encyclopedia/hero]]): at **Lv.3** 60/30/10 for
  1/2/3-cost (4 and 5-cost cannot appear); at **Lv.5** 34/35/30, and 4-cost only cracks open at **1%**.
  So early heroes = cost 1-2, with the first 3-costs trickling in by L5. The shared pool is deep here
  (25 copies per 1-cost, 21 per 2-cost), so cheap units are *easy to star and easy to contest*.
- **The fork that decides the phase** is economy vs tempo: ride a **win streak** (push board strength,
  bank +1/win and the streak bonus) or a **lose streak** (bleed HP on purpose, hoard for interest and a
  mid-game spike). Either is fine; a *broken* streak that earns neither is the trap.
- **The I-1 creep** hands a basic equipment (1 of 3), **I-2** the first Go Go Card, **end of Stage I** a
  reward selection (lowest-HP picks first). Calendar: [[../infos/rounds/index]].

## How early pieces are scored (the phase triad)

Parallel to the [[commander_tier_list]]'s C/S/V, but phase-tuned:
- **Access (A)** — cheap and deep in the pool, so I can actually field and star it *now*.
- **Impact (I)** — does it carry or hold a fight at 1-2 star, with no items and no breakpoints yet?
- **Carry-forward (F)** — does it stay relevant into mid (a real carry / a synergy seed), or is it a
  throwaway I'll bench by round 8?

High on all three = S **for the opening**. None of this is a claim about late power (see the caveat).

## The caveat (forms of life)

A tier only means something inside a way of playing ([[../strategy/tier_lists]]). Under a committed
**commander or line** this inverts: a Stargazer pair is a weak *opener* but the engine of a caster comp;
Neobeasts looks slow now but the points it banks from round 1 are a late win-con. Early tiers rank the
**opening**, not the build. The moment a line commits, its own plan overrides this.

## Synergies — by what is reachable and worth it at [2]

Only [2] breakpoints (and a couple of cheap [2] factions) are realistic this phase. Ranked by opening value.

### S — the openers that win rounds *and* compound
- **Scavenger [2]** (Angela 2 + Phoveus 1) -> a free Shop hero every round. Compounding **econ + tempo**,
  both members cheap and useful (Angela heals, Phoveus debuffs ATK). The strongest *opening* engine.
- **Mage [2]** (Cecilion 1 + Lylia 2) -> +magic ATK onto the two best early nukers. Immediate fight-winning
  burst from a 1-cost and a 2-cost. Scales straight into the mid Mage core.
- **Neobeasts [2]** (Gatotkaca 2 + Lylia/Brody) -> the points engine starts banking **round 1**; the
  earliest you start it, the larger the late payout. Econ-and-scaling in one, on cheap bodies.
- **Dauntless [2]** (Silvanna 1 + Balmond 2) -> team Shield = a hard HP floor, the cleanest way to not
  bleed while you find your line.

### A — strong [2] openers, slightly narrower
- **Marksman [2]** (Brody 1 + Granger 2) -> team + marksman ATK speed onto auto-attackers; needs a back-row
  carry to matter, but Brody's aura helps the whole right side.
- **Defender [2]** (Minotaur 1 + Gatotkaca 2) -> Hybrid DEF, doubled the first 20s; a tank wall that buys
  time. Gatotkaca double-counts (Bruiser + Defender), a premium early flex body.
- **Bruiser [2]** (Dyrroth 1 + Gatotkaca/Belerick 2) -> extra basic-ATK DMG + double-hit chance; solid
  front damage early.
- **Astro Power [2]** (Minotaur 1 + Martis 2) -> flat DMG Increase, and the most-itemed unit becomes the
  Sovereign; a real seed if you funnel the I-1 item.
- **Emberlord [2]** (Cecilion 1 + Hanzo 2) -> a death pays one survivor 20% ATK; cheap, pairs with Mage on
  Cecilion.

### B — seed-now, pay-later (weak as a pure opener)
- **Stargazer [2]** (Eudora 1 + Selena 2) -> mana regen; the payoff is later, once you field mana-hungry
  casters. Selena's 800% single-target is the real early draw here, not the synergy.
- **Swiftblade [2]** / **Weapon Master [2]** -> penetration / lifesteal; thin until you have a carry to
  carry it. Hold for mid.
- **Enchanted Tales [2]** (Selena 2 + Belerick 2) -> two 2-costs reach it early, but the fragment-scaling
  and the Protagonist need the 4-costs; a slow burn.

### C / not-yet — can't meaningfully online early
- **Kishin** (equipment ramp), **Dragoncaller** (pays at [4]+), **Phasewarper** (Fanny aside, needs
  3-costs), **Northern Vale / Mystic Meow / Heartbond** (tiny, fiddly, or tile-paired). Park them.

## Heroes — the opening backbone (cost 1-2, plus the first 3-costs)

Ranked by **Access + Impact + Carry-forward** for the opening.

### S — first-pick the moment they appear
- **Cecilion** (1, Mage/Emberlord, back) — A:H I:H F:H. A 735% magic nuke on a **1-cost**, plus a mana-battery
  passive for his neighbors. Single best opening unit; stays a real threat well into mid.
- **Gatotkaca** (2, Bruiser+Defender/Neobeasts, front) — A:H I:H F:H. **Double class** on one cheap tanky
  body that heals 400 + nukes; the flex that fills two breakpoints at once.
- **Brody** (1, Marksman/Neobeasts, back) — A:H I:M F:H. A team ATK-speed aura on battle-start + stacking
  vulnerability; a 1-cost that buffs the whole right side and seeds two S synergies.

### A — strong, build the opening around these
- **Lylia** (2, Mage/Neobeasts, back) — 720% piercing magic; a second Mage nuke that also feeds Neobeasts.
- **Selena** (2, Stargazer/Enchanted Tales, back) — the biggest *single-target* early nuke (800%); deletes
  an enemy carry before it ramps.
- **Granger** (2, Marksman/Exorcist, back) — a 3-shot burst marksman, the early Marksman partner, and an
  Exorcist seed for the mid Kagura line.
- **Minotaur** (1, Defender/Astro, front) — heal + a battle-start Shield battery for neighbors; the sturdiest
  cheap front, and the Astro seed.
- **Angela** (2, Scavenger/Kishin, back) — heal + the Scavenger econ engine; keeps a fragile opening alive
  while printing tempo.
- **Phoveus** (1, Scavenger/Exorcist, front) — the cheap Scavenger partner + an ATK-down debuff that softens
  an enemy carry.

### B — fillers and situational fronts
- **Silvanna** (1, Dauntless/Mystic Meow) / **Dyrroth** (1, Bruiser/Kishin) / **Eudora** (1, Stargazer/
  Dragoncaller) / **Balmond** (2, Dauntless/Dragoncaller) / **Martis** (2, Weapon Master/Astro) / **Hanzo**
  (2, Swiftblade/Emberlord) / **Joy** (2, Swiftblade/Northern Vale) / **Belerick** (2, Bruiser/Enchanted
  Tales) — synergy-fillers and tanky bodies. Good for hitting a [2] and holding a line; benched as carries
  arrive.
- **Fanny** (2, Phasewarper/Heartbond, front) — a 550% AoE that decreases with targets + anti-heal; a fine
  early body, but her synergies are slow to online.

### The first 3-costs (L5, ~30% odds) — grab if you see them early
- **Khufra** (3, Defender/Heartbond) — a stun + a huge scaling nuke (970% at 3-star); a premium CC front the
  moment it shows.
- **Hilda** (3, Dauntless/Astro) — the fattest early front (10440 HP at 3-star), shields on cast.
- **Saber / Helcurt** (3, Swiftblade) — back-line divers that punish a squishy opener.
- **Tigreal** (3, Defender/Emberlord) — a stun tank that seeds Emberlord + Defender.

## The phase throughline (how to actually use this)

- **Don't force a comp at the lobby screen.** Field the best *bodies* (the S heroes), hit one or two S/A
  [2] breakpoints, and read which line the pool leaves open. Commit in mid (Stage II), not now.
- **Pick the streak and respect it.** Win-streak: buy strength, keep the board ahead. Lose-streak: take the
  HP hit deliberately, hold gold to 20 for the +4 interest cap, spike later. Never a sloppy 1-1-1-1.
- **The cheap pool is shared.** A 1-cost you love is a 1-cost seven others can also star; if it is contested,
  pivot rather than fight nine-deep for copies.
- **Funnel the I-1 item** toward the unit you expect to carry (Astro Sovereign, or your back-row nuke).
- **Seeds beat fireworks.** A round-1 Neobeasts point or a Scavenger free-hero compounds; an unsustained
  highroll does not.
