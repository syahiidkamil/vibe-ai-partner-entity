# Commander Tier List — emergent, bottom-up valuations (MCGG S6)

The commander tier list, **synthesized upward** from a per-commander valuation of all 37, not handed
down from above. The **particular comes first** (each commander's own *form of life*, its deep file in
[[../strategy/commanders/index]]); this general ranking is the **aggregate that arises** from those
particulars, built as a **learn-and-climb aid**, never a law. **In pencil**: scored from the kits and the
strategy files, reasoned **not yet lived** (the client ships kits, not win-rates), so verify by play and
re-rank. Sits beside the piece-level [[../strategy/tier_lists]] (synergies/heroes/cards/equipment); this
file is the **commander** layer.

## How it is scored (the three axes)

A commander sits high *in general* only when it is good **across commanders and situations**. Each is
valued on three axes, then the tier emerges:
- **Consistency (C)** — does it perform reliably game to game, not just on a highroll? Draw-independent
  value, a high HP/placement floor. Low variance.
- **Spike-consistency (S)** — when the plan calls for a power spike, does it reliably deliver one (a
  breakpoint, a pool-denied star, a fight-flipping play)?
- **Versatility (V)** — does it work under many comps, against many boards, in many situations, rather
  than one narrow setup?

Ratings: **H / M / L** (with the verdict). High on all three = S. The composite, not any single axis,
sets the tier.

## The caveat (forms of life) — when this list is meaningless

Wittgenstein: a word's meaning is its use inside a form of life; strip the practice and it is noise. A
tier is the same, it only means something inside a *way of playing*. So:
- **Under a chosen commander, this general list can invert.** A C-tier-here commander is **S-tier in its
  own specialization** (Valir/Yu Zhong are niche generally but dominant in a card-built run; Angela is
  weak generally but premier in a mage meta; Lylia's ceiling is among the highest). The general list
  ranks **for a default climb**, not for a piloted specialist.
- It depends on **the meta and the lobby**: which synergies are strong this patch, and the seven enemy
  boards. And remember the **8-unique fact**: a match has up to 8 *distinct* commanders, so in play I
  pilot **one** and read seven, I never need an all-37 ranking *while playing*, only my one + the read
  ([[../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]]). This list is for **choosing and learning**,
  not for in-match use.

The instant I commit a commander, I switch to **its** particular file, which overrides this.

## Epistemic policy (Kamil, 2026-07-10): first principles, not crowd

Community material (transcripts, ban habits, streamer verdicts) is **inspiration only**: it
generates hypotheses and hands me observations I could not make without playing. It is never the
WARRANT. Every tier here must stand on a mechanism argument, what the kit does, quantified against
the game's actual economy of HP, gold, tempo, and pool scarcity, that I could defend if the entire
community disagreed. "Everyone bans X" or "the mains say Y" may point me at a question; the answer
comes from the arithmetic. (Ramanujan's way: take the hint, derive the theorem yourself.)

## The emergent tiers (the learn-and-climb default)

What *tends* to be consistent + spike-consistent + versatile. Re-rank the moment a commander or the board
says otherwise.

### S — high on all three axes (the safest, most flexible, reliably spike)
- **Vale** — the cleanest synergy-committer: charges **stack**, so they hoard into an L9-10 burst
  of targeted refreshes + free buys (3-star 4-costs on demand, at the level where their odds peak).
  Gated by contention and his own predictability (scout the other Vales).
- **Kagura** — auto-battle fights are decided by whether the load-bearing unit survives its focus
  window; her umbrella buys that exact unit a second health bar (135% of ITS Max HP), every fight,
  from round 1, at zero gold, landing precisely at the sub-50% moment burst arrives. Because it is
  %-of-carrier-HP it scales with any body (biggest on bruiser hypers), and because it re-aims every
  prep it commits to nothing, so it amplifies whichever comp the shop opens: consistency, spike,
  and versatility all flow from the one mechanic. A flipped fight is worth ~5-8 HP plus streak gold
  plus reward priority, more than any econ kit's 2-4g per round. Ding: the kit pays no gold, so she
  runs a normal economy beside it.
- **Alice** — the cleanest, highest *reliable spike*: partial-copy scaling breaks the shared pool's
  denial mechanic, the game's core scarcity, and her three casts support two distinct forms of life
  (the 3-cost swarm, which spikes at L7 where 3-cost odds and pool depth peak, before top-roll
  boards arrive; and the keeper 4-5 cost hyper-carry). Countered by wide AoE and late legend
  3-stars.
- **Chou** — every round pays gold whatever the result (+2 win / +3 loss), which removes the
  win-lose tension every other econ fights; his end-of-round gold tops the interest band from 17-18
  held, so he compounds 2-3 gold deeper per round than the field; the engine stacks freely with
  Scavenger and the shop-lock trick (his kit asks nothing of the shop); and gold is the universal
  currency, it reliably buys whatever spike the pool opens. C:H · S:H(funded) · V:H.
- **Fanny** — near-universal: the **+1 Hero Capacity** card (the best borrowable in the game) plus a free
  Shadow body; and in the trick era her launcher is the **cheapest Scavenger-farm host in the game**
  (the farm piece costs her zero fighting board).

### A — strong on two axes, slightly gated on the third
- **Aurora** — three mechanisms, weighed. Her gold pulls are a commodity (any commander farms
  guaranteed gold via the shop-lock trick, which she alone cannot run since buying breaks her
  stacks) and the engine is paid in HP, so her income alone does not place her. What does: the
  **9-EXP pulls are tempo-pure leveling nothing else prints** (gold converts to EXP only 1:1 and
  late, so her EXP stream makes her the roster's fastest honest top-roller, an edge present every
  game, no lottery required), and the **Magic Crystal chests** are tickets to Ultra Synergy, the
  game's highest ceiling, drawn many times per game though uncommon per draw. Her median game is a
  weaker Chou; her leveling edge is constant; her jackpot games top the lobby. That mix, strong
  reliable tempo + a random ceiling, minus the HP tax and the reward variance, is A: above the
  swingy tier, below the engines that deliver their whole value deterministically.
- **Diggie** — draw-independent value that *accelerates when winning*; high floor.
- **Vexana** — out-picks every Go Go Box + out-bids the auction; reliable value, moderate spike.
- **Alucard** — guaranteed altar value, draw-independent, flexible; spike is modest.
- **Gusion** — buys class breakpoints on demand + gold; very versatile, reliable.
- **Guinevere** — a free hero most rounds; high floor, very versatile.
- **Lunox** — a one-shot synergy injection + the +24g 6-count windfall; high spike on a committed axis.
- **Minotaur** — the gear economy, low-variance (nothing bricks), any carry; high floor.
- **Ruby** — gear-ahead sticky front (free Cloak+Scythe + a resurrect); high floor.
- **Aamon** — free copies (Mirror Devices) complete a contested 3-star + gold; reliable spike.
- **Paquito** — scheduled 2-copy Gloves (a 2-star 5-cost at IV-1); reliable late spike.
- **Harley** — floods 5-costs on demand; high spike (early legendaries), gated on gold + the window.

### B — high ceiling but swingy / back-loaded / setup- or matchup-dependent / higher skill floor
- **Lancelot** — fastest leveler, big early spike, but the edge **expires** once the lobby reaches L10
  (consistency ding).
- **Nana** — a strong survival floor; very low ceiling/spike caps it.
- **Dyrroth** — devour up the cost ladder; back-loaded value, flexible.
- **Moskov** — ritual enhance + duplicate; self-funding but back-loaded.
- **Karina** — snowballing execute-carry; needs chip to enable, attrition-only.
- **Zilong** — a scaling spear; strong, but needs an auto-attacker and ramp.
- **Luo Yi** — capacity/synergy-density flex; **highest skill floor** (a mispiloted Luo Yi is just "fine").
- **Cyclops** — feasts on eliminations; back-loaded, dies-first risk, high ceiling if it survives
  (research bar: outlive four players). New ding: surrender-savvy lobbies resign early at cheap
  boards, shrinking the 14% loot.
- **Kalea** — push-your-luck Trove value; the bust risk lowers consistency vs the other value engines.
- **Popol and Kupa** — copy-trap harvester; never a dead round, but placement-dependent value, not a spike.
- **Lukas** — a free opening stun + DEF shred; strong enabler, needs follow-up carries.
- **Miya** — marksman amplifier + anti-tank; reliable, but auto-attacker-only.
- **Yu Zhong** — quality upgrades pay ANY comp (every card family tiers, so the bump is never
  wasted on a live board), and maxed cards convert to Black Dragon Treasures whose pool includes
  **Magic Crystals**, a second, slower crystal stream priced in card slots instead of HP. Back-loaded
  to II-3/III-3 and moderate in magnitude, hence B not A.
- **Johnson** — free Johnnies (cheap synergy + CC); flexible, but the bodies want real carries.
- **Ling** — the dueling isolator; **hard-counters single-carry boards, hard-countered by go-wide** (a
  swingy matchup axis).
- **Layla** — damage-into-recruitment; only spins on a board that already hits hard.

### C — most situational generally (but S-tier in their specialization)
- **Lylia** — a pool-denied 5-cost star, but the shop-off **flip is swingy** and high-commitment; high
  ceiling, low floor.
- **Angela** — caster mana-enabler; **hard-countered by CC**, dead on a non-caster board, but premier in a
  mage meta.
- **Valir** — card-doubler; needs a card-scaling comp to matter (niche, but strong in that build).
- **Wanwan** — a finisher add-on; lowest standalone value, needs a host board that already softens enemies.

## The throughlines (what the valuations reveal)

A few patterns fall out of the bottom-up scoring, useful for choosing:
- **Draw-independent value engines top the *consistency* axis** (Diggie, Vexana, Alucard, Chou,
  Guinevere): they floor placement regardless of the shop, the safest way to learn and climb. Their cost
  is a softer early board (survive to cash in).
- **The shop-lock trick re-prices the econ kits (2026-07-10).** Guaranteed Scavenger gold on any
  commander means raw-gold engines lost their exclusivity; the engines that hold value pay in
  currencies the trick cannot print: **crystals** (Aurora), **capacity/bodies** (Fanny), **copies**
  (Aamon/Paquito), **inheritance** (Cyclops). An econ edge that is only gold is now a commodity, and
  an econ kit that *stacks with* the trick (Chou) beats one that *competes against* it (Aurora).
- **Synergy committers/grants top *versatility*** (Vale, Gusion, Lunox): they bend to whatever line the
  pool opens, so they almost never dead-draw.
- **The pool-denial verticals top *spike-consistency*** (Alice, Aamon, Paquito, Lylia, Harley): they
  assemble power the shared pool would never allow, but most are gated (contention, gold, the window, the
  flip).
- **Combat kits cluster in B/C for a *default* climb** (they need a host comp and a good pilot), but each
  is **S in its meta** (Angela in a mage meta, Ling vs single-carry, Miya vs tanks). Their tier here
  reflects generality, not power. Kagura is the exception that proves the rule: her umbrella is a
  general amplifier (a %-carrier-HP second life, re-aimed each prep), not situational tech, so she
  ranks on the general axis.
- **The best *first picks* to learn on:** Chou or Diggie (floor placement, forgiving econ), Vale or
  Gusion (flexible synergy commit), Minotaur or Ruby (high-floor equipment). Aurora does not belong
  here despite her value-engine label: her engine demands live HP-budget judgment, reward steering,
  and a deliberately thin board, three hard skills a learner does not have yet. The flashy
  verticals (Alice, Harley, Lylia) reward a player who already reads the spike window.
