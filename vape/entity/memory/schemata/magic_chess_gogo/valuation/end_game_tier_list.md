# End-Game Tier List — Stage III-IV (rounds 11+), Commander Lv.9-10 (MCGG S6)

The **end phase** valuation: which synergies and heroes *close games*, when 4-costs are common, 5-costs are
real, 3-stars come online, and the faction **[10] caps** unlock. Part of a three-file set with
[[early_game_tier_list]] and [[middle_game_tier_list]]; the **commander** layer is [[commander_tier_list]],
the piece-level baseline [[../strategy/tier_lists]]. **In pencil**, scored from kits/stats/odds, not yet
lived ([[../disclaimer]]).

## What "end" is (the phase frame)

- **Rounds 11+ (Stage III then IV, open-ended)**, Commander Lv.9-10. Full capacity (10 slots at L10).
- **The shop is the top tiers.** Draw odds ([[../infos/encyclopedia/hero]]): **Lv.9** 13/22/25/**26**/14,
  **Lv.10** 6/12/22/**35**/25. 4-cost is the bread tier and 5-cost is genuinely fishable, so end = 3-star
  the carries and hit the big caps.
- **The reward stages favor the loser** (Stage III reward selection, the Go Go Box, lowest-HP picks first), so
  a behind-but-alive board can still assemble its win-con. Stage IV adds +2/+3 base salary, so gold is rich.
- **III-3** brings a Go Go Card + creep equipment + a *second* Blessing Shifter; **IV-3** a creep equipment.
  Calendar: [[../infos/rounds/index]].

## How end pieces are scored (the phase triad)

- **Ceiling (C)** — the top-end payoff: a 3-star carry, a [6] class cap, a [10] faction cap.
- **Attainability (A)** — can I *realistically* assemble it given the pool and contention? (A 3-star 5-cost
  needs all 9 copies; a [10] needs 6 members + 4 from blessings/crystals/grants.)
- **Win-con (W)** — does it actually *close* the game, not just add stats?

High on all three = S **for closing**. The whole phase is about converting a lead into a finisher.

## The two ultras (the "almost guaranteed win if not contested")

End game has two ceilings so high that, **uncontested**, they win outright. Both are gated by *pool denial*:

1. **A 3-star 5-cost.** The cost-5 pool holds **only 9 copies** of each hero, and a 3-star needs **nine
   1-stars**. So a 3-star 5-cost is the *entire pool* of that legendary in your hands; it can only happen if
   nobody else is buying it (or with a copy-commander: [[../strategy/commanders/aamon]],
   [paquito](../strategy/commanders/paquito.md), [moskov](../strategy/commanders/moskov.md)). The stat jump is
   nuclear: Alpha 3-star is **55,590 HP / 870 ATK / +900%**, Lesley fires for **1000% + crit**, Ruby/Bane sit
   at ~53,760 HP. One such unit can solo a board.
2. **A faction [10] cap.** Each 10-cap faction has exactly **6 members**, so [10] = all 6 deployed **plus +4**
   from Blessings, Synergy Magic Crystals, and synergy-granting commanders (Vale/Gusion/Lunox/Johnson). That
   is the whole faction's pool funneled to one player. The [10] effects are game-ending (below). "If not
   contested" is the literal condition: you need the faction to yourself.

The realistic version most games is a **2-star 5-cost + a 3-star 4-cost + a [6]/seeded-[10]** — still a
finisher, just short of the pure ultra.

## Synergies — the closers (the [6] class caps and [10] faction caps)

Ranked by closing power. The [10]s are the win-cons; the [6]s are the reliable spikes that get you there.

### S — the [10] faction ultras (uncontested = game over)
- **Astro Power [10]** -> a *second* Astro Sovereign, +300% DMG, 40% lifesteal, **600% Max HP**, and Astro
  Links dealing 600%-Adaptive True DMG to 5 enemies. The cleanest "funnel everything onto the Sovereign" win.
- **Dragoncaller [10]** -> a battle-start Divine Dragon (50% Max HP DMG), dragon strikes at 70% Max HP that
  **stun 3s**, Dragoncallers +200% DMG / +60% HP. Near game-ending AoE.
- **Heartbond [10]** -> Heartbond + bonded heroes +250% ATK/HP and **>=10,000 True DMG every 3s**; an
  unkillable, untyped-damage core (needs the tile-pairing micro).
- **Exorcist [10]** -> the Seal carrier becomes **Phantom Lord** (+500% DMG and HP); at battle start all other
  Exorcists become its Phantoms with 500% HP and +500% DMG. The Kagura sustain line, weaponized.
- **Emberlord [10]** -> all survivors gain 50% of a fallen's HP + 85% ATK, and dying Emberlords **resurrect at
  30% HP**. A board that refuses to die.

### A — the [6] class spikes (the reliable closers) and the gated [10]s
- **Mage [6]** (+120% Magic ATK) / **Marksman [6]** (+90% ATK speed) / **Dauntless [6]** (Shield 20% + 90%) /
  **Bruiser [6]** (+60% basic, 50% double-hit) / **Defender [6]** (+24/+60 DEF) / **Weapon Master [6]** (+100%
  Physical, 15% lifesteal) / **Swiftblade [6]** (+25/+120 pen) / **Stargazer [6]** (mana flood) -> the class
  caps that turn a 2-star core into a fight-winner without needing 10 of a faction.
- **Neobeasts [10]** -> instant 999-point payout (huge ATK/HP), **but only if the points were banked from
  early** (the seed pays here); high A if you started round 1, low if you pivoted in late.
- **Kishin [10]** -> Supreme (Tier-4) equipment + **600 Hybrid DEF**; an item-and-tank ultra, slower to feel
  than the damage [10]s.

### B / fading — the small-caps that have no late ceiling
- **Scavenger [3]** (now reachable with Claude) / **Phasewarper [3]** / **Northern Vale [3]** (Bane online) /
  **Enchanted Tales [4]** / **Mystic Meow [2]** (the 9/11-synergy Mystic Claw rewards a go-wide board) -> they
  cap out and add no win-con ceiling; valuable late only as a *splash* on top of a real closer.

## Heroes — the win-cons (the 5-costs, and the 4-costs at 3-star)

Ranked by **Ceiling + Attainability + Win-con**. 5-costs are the legendaries; the 4-costs become win-cons at
3-star (their 3-star skill multipliers are enormous).

### S — the game-closers
- **Alpha** (5, Weapon Master/Emberlord, front) — C:H A:M W:H. The fattest 3-star in the game (55,590 HP, 870
  ATK, +900%); even at 2-star a wrecking ball. The single best raw-stat finisher.
- **Ruby** (5, Dauntless/Exorcist, front) — taunt + a passive resurrect + 53,760 HP at 3-star; an unkillable
  front-carry that also anchors Exorcist and Dauntless.
- **Lesley** (5, Marksman/Mystic Meow, back) — 45% crit base, ATK-speed-to-crit-DMG conversion, **1000%** at
  3-star; the back-row deleter if shielded.
- **Aurora** (4, Stargazer/Astro, back) — at 3-star: 500% AoE + 13,795 HP; the most *attainable* hyper-carry
  (4-cost pool is 14) and the Astro Power finisher.
- **Kagura** (4, Mage/Exorcist, back) — at 3-star **1700%** AoE; the Exorcist Phantom-Lord skill source and the
  Mage [6] payoff. Attainable and a true closer.

### A — strong finishers
- **Bane** (5, Defender/Northern Vale, front) — a 1.2s freeze on a 53,760-HP body; the premier late CC-tank.
- **Ling** (5, Swiftblade/Neobeasts, front) — dives and isolates the enemy carry; **hard-counters single-carry
  boards** (and is countered by go-wide).
- **Odette** (5, Mage/Astro, back) — a control-immune sustained-AoE channel; a Mage/Astro double anchor.
- **Lancelot** (5, Phasewarper/Kishin, front) — multi-dash assassin, 775 ATK at 3-star; deletes a back line.
- **Claude** (5, Scavenger/Heartbond, back) — summons Dexter (a second carry body); effectively two carries,
  the Heartbond [10] engine.
- **Badang** (5, Bruiser/Dragoncaller, front) — counter-punch bruiser, control-immune; the Dragoncaller [10]
  bruiser.
- **Esmeralda / Terizla / Irithel / Leomord** (4) — at 3-star their skills go nuclear (Esmeralda 1320%,
  Terizla 1300% + 16,775 HP, Irithel 400% multi-hit, Leomord 1425%); the *attainable* 3-star win-cons when the
  5-costs are contested.

### B — splash / role-locked
- **Karrie** (4, anti-tank) / **Yu Zhong** (4, Exorcist bruiser) / **Pharsa / Lunox / Vexana / Gusion** (4) —
  real units, but late they read as **support to the closer** (a breakpoint body, an anti-tank, a second
  caster) rather than the win-con itself.

## The phase throughline (how to actually use this)

- **Pick one ultra and deny its pool.** Either pour every copy into one 5-cost (toward the 3-star), or commit a
  faction and hoard its 6 members + push +4 to [10]. Splitting both gets neither.
- **"If not contested" is a read, not a hope.** Read the seven boards
  ([[../thinking_and_mental_model/reading_enemy_commanders]]): if another player is buying your 5-cost or your
  faction, the ultra is off, and the [6] class-cap line is the safer close.
- **The loser-favored reward stages are your catch-up.** Behind-but-alive, you pick first at the Go Go Box and
  the Stage III reward; use it to grab the missing copy or the Synergy Crystal that completes the cap.
- **Blessings + Synergy Crystals are the last +4** that turn a [6] faction into a [10]. Bank them for the
  win-con, not for a marginal mid-tier count.
- **Position for the finisher.** A 3-star carry that gets CC'd or dived before it acts is a wasted ultra;
  Bane/Ruby/Kagura-shield and back-row placement protect the close.
