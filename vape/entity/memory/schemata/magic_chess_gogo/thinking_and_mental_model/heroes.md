# Thinking — heroes, basic opinion

My basic read on the 54-hero roster: which units carry, which are flex glue, which are cheap-vertical
targets, which high-costs are worth fielding at 1-2 star. **Opinion in pencil**, keyed off cost, role,
and synergy membership from [[../infos/heroes/index]] and the replay, **not yet from deep per-skill
play**, so it leans on structure over skill specifics. Verify by playing. Foundations: [[index]];
synergy reasoning: [[synergies]].

## How I value a hero (the four questions)

1. **Does it carry, glue, or wall?** A carry wants items and a back-row seat; glue fills breakpoints;
   a wall buys time up front. Know which job before I buy.
2. **How many breakpoints does one body buy?** A **dual-class** hero counts twice; that is raw
   slot-efficiency.
3. **Is its 3-star reachable?** Cheap heroes 3-star easily (9 of 25/21 copies); 5-costs basically
   never (9 of 9). So cost sets *how* I use it: cheap = chase the star, expensive = field at 1-2 star.
4. **Does its synergy axis match my comp?** A great hero off my axis is a worse buy than a mediocre one
   on it.

## The flex glue I value most (dual-class bodies)

- **Gatotkaca** (2c, Bruiser **+** Defender / Neobeasts) — one slot, two frontline class counts, plus a
  Neobeasts body. The single most slot-efficient unit in the game; fits almost any frontline.
- **Franco** (3c, Dauntless **+** Weapon Master / Kishin) — two class counts, a sturdy mid-cost glue.
- **Julian** (3c, Mage **+** Phasewarper / Mystic Meow) — a Mage that also seeds Phasewarper and Mystic
  Meow; excellent in the mage core.

These three are "buy on sight if near my comp" because they pay two breakpoints for one body.

## Cost 1 (8) — the early engine and the cheap-vertical targets

Alucard, Brody, Cecilion, Dyrroth, Eudora, Minotaur, Phoveus, Silvanna. Their job early is turning on
[2]s cheaply; a few are real **3-star targets** because a 1-cost 3-star is cheap power:
- **Cecilion** (Mage/Emberlord) — a 1-cost *Mage*: the natural cheap-carry-to-3-star for a mage board,
  and an Emberlord seed. My top cost-1 keep for a mage line.
- **Brody** (Marksman/Neobeasts) — cheap Marksman + Neobeasts point body; good for either axis.
- **Phoveus** (Scavenger/Exorcist) — turns on Scavenger econ early; valuable far past its stats.
- **Minotaur** (Defender/Astro) — cheap front + Astro seed. The rest are situational [2] fillers.

## Cost 2-3 (23) — the comp's spine

This is where comps take shape and where the **standard 3-star verticals** live (3-cost 3-star = 9 of
18, reachable if uncontested, peak shop odds at L6). Standouts by job:
- **Carry-capable 3-costs:** Miya (Marksman/Heartbond), Saber (Swiftblade/Exorcist), Helcurt
  (Swiftblade/Astro), Masha (Bruiser/Emberlord). A 3-star here is a real mid-game spike.
- **Frontline/glue:** Tigreal (Defender/Emberlord), Khufra (Defender/Heartbond), Hilda
  (Dauntless/Astro), Belerick (Bruiser/Enchanted Tales), Balmond (Dauntless/Dragoncaller).
- **Econ/utility:** Angela (Scavenger/Kishin), Lylia (Mage/Neobeasts), Selena (Stargazer/Enchanted).
- **The dual-class glue** (Gatotkaca, Franco, Julian) lives here, noted above.

## Cost 4 (12) — the carries you actually field

Aurora, Esmeralda, Gusion, Irithel, Kagura, Karrie, Leomord, Lunox, Pharsa, Terizla, Vexana, Yu Zhong.
These are the *real* damage and the comp-definers; you field them at 1-2 star (a 4-cost 3-star, 9 of
14, is a rare luxury). The ones I rate highest as carries:
- **Kagura** (Mage/Exorcist) — a top carry in the replay (vs weak AI, so illustrative), and an
  Exorcist Phantom body.
- **Vexana** (Mage/Enchanted Tales), **Pharsa** (Stargazer/Neobeasts), **Aurora** (Stargazer/Astro) —
  caster damage + mana enabling for a mage/skill board.
- **Karrie / Irithel** (Marksman) — the 4-cost marksman carries for an ATK-speed board.
- **Esmeralda** (Dauntless/Heartbond) — a tanky carry/bruiser-front hybrid, very forgiving.

## Cost 5 (9) — power at 1-2 star, never chase the 3-star

Alpha (WM/Emberlord), Badang (Bruiser/Dragoncaller), Bane (Defender/Northern Vale), Claude
(Scavenger/Heartbond), Lancelot (Phasewarper/Kishin), Lesley (Marksman/Mystic Meow), Ling
(Swiftblade/Neobeasts), Odette (Mage/Astro), Ruby (Dauntless/Exorcist). A 5-cost 3-star needs all 9
copies in the game, so these are **raw-power inserts** at 1-2 star, dropped onto a finished board for
their stats and synergy.
- **Odette** (Mage/Astro) — the replay's top-damage carry; the mage-board capstone.
- **Ruby** (Dauntless/Exorcist) — a tanky carry that anchors a front and seeds Exorcist.
- **Claude** (Scavenger/Heartbond) — a 5-cost econ body that keeps Scavenger online late.
- **Ling** (Swiftblade/Neobeasts) — assassin carry + Neobeasts capstone.

## The summon bodies (free value)

Some synergies/commanders put units on the board that **do not cost capacity** and each add +1
Commander DMG: the **Dragon** (Dragoncaller), **Scavenger** free heroes, **Johnson's Johnnies**. Treat
them as free board presence; lean into a summoning line when capacity is the bottleneck.

## The one-line hero heuristic

Buy the dual-class glue on sight near my comp; 3-star *cheap* carries (Cecilion-type) and *field*
expensive ones (Kagura/Odette-type) at 1-2 star; spend items on the one back-row carry; never chase a
4-5 cost 3-star unless it is falling into my lap uncontested.
