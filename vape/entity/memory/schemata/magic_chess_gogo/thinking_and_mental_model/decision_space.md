# Thinking — the wider decision space

A sweep of the *other* major decisions, beyond the ones with their own files (synergies, heroes,
commanders, equipment, reroll/contest/auction, positioning). The possibility space is large and this
is a **sample, not a complete list**: any section here can graduate into its own file as it deepens.
**Opinion in pencil**, reasoned from the mechanics in `../infos/`, **not yet lived**. Foundations:
[[index]].

## 1. Power spikes and tempo windows

My board does not get stronger smoothly; it jumps at **discrete events**: a star-up, a crossed
breakpoint, a landed carry, a Go Go Card. Those are my **spike windows**. The decision: right after a
spike, when my board outclasses the lobby, **push the HP advantage** (win rounds, build the streak,
deal Commander DMG); between spikes, **bank** (interest band, level toward the next spike). Opponents
spike too, so anticipate the lobby's clocks: a rival's 4-cost coming online or a known [6] tier is a
window where I must be strong or I bleed. Tempo is timing my strength to land on the rounds that
matter.

## 2. Scouting and countering opponents

Imperfect information is a property of the game, so *gather it*. Read the **fight previews** for who
is building what, who is contesting my line, who is the leader I will have to beat. The advanced-tactics
play: mid-to-late, **collect heroes/items/positions that counter the threats** I keep facing, and deny
the leader's key heroes when I am rich. Tech the position against their archetype (corner-guard vs a
Swiftblade lobby, spread vs an AoE/dragon board). I am not building in a vacuum; I am building against
seven specific boards. Reading enemy *commanders* specifically, whose kits telegraph what they hoard
and bid for, has its own file: [[reading_enemy_commanders]].

## 3. Streak management

Both win and loss streaks pay (array 1;1;2;3, cap +3); choppy pays nothing. So the decision is rarely
"win this round" but "**which streak am I on, and is it still right.**" Keep a **win streak** while the
board can hold it (best state: streak gold + victory gold + zero HP cost). Switch to a **committed loss
streak** only when the board genuinely cannot win and HP is still cheap (Stage I-II). The cardinal sin
is breaking a streak for one marginal round; protect the streak you are on.

## 4. Taking HP damage on purpose

HP is a currency whose price rises (base DMG 3 -> 15), so **spending cheap early HP to bank gold and
grab lowest-HP reward priority** is a real line, not a mistake (the intentional-loss play:
[[../experience/ranked/general_mythic]]). The decision rule: lose *cheap* HP (Stage I-II) when the
gold/reward is worth more than the HP, never *expensive* HP (Stage IV), and never so low that a leader's
spiked board can close me out before I stabilize. Read HP as a resource to spend, not only a bar to
protect.

## 5. Blessing and Blessing-Shifter placement

A Blessing grants **+1 to one of a hero's synergy counts** (from combining a 2-star, II-1 on); the
**Blessing Shifter** (II-1, III-3) generates one or moves it. A free +1 count is a **bought
breakpoint**, so place it where it crosses a threshold *right now* (a synergy exactly one short), on a
hero I will **keep**. The Shifter's value is flexibility: re-point the blessing as the comp shifts so
the +1 is always sitting on a live breakpoint, never stranded on a benched hero or a maxed synergy.

## 6. Reward selection and Go Go Box priority

At the Go Go Box reward stages (end of I and III, lowest-HP-first), and creep picks, the menu has a
priority order:
1. A **Synergy Magic Crystal** (a +1 count = a breakpoint, the highest-value pick).
2. A **hero that completes my comp** or a **star-up copy** I need (pool-proof progress).
3. My **carry's best-in-slot item** (or an Enhanced over Basic).
4. A generic stat item I will actually field.
Take the thing that changes *kind* (a breakpoint, a star) over the thing that adds a number. Skip
off-comp heroes and items I will not use, even if they look shiny. (Auction bidding is its own
decision: [[reroll_contest_auction]].)

## 7. Capacity vs star-up (breadth vs depth)

Each Commander level adds a board slot. The recurring choice at every level-up: **field a new unit
(breadth, another breakpoint) or hold and star up what I have (depth, denser power).** Heuristic: add a
unit only if it **completes a breakpoint or replaces a weaker body**; never add a naked unit that
breaks no threshold (it just dilutes). Early, breadth wins (turn synergies on); late, depth wins (a
2-3 star carry beats another [2]). The front line especially wants depth: see the star-level note in
[[../strategy/general_strategy]].

## 8. Placement over 1st (the climb objective)

Ranked climbs on **placement**, so the meta-objective under every decision is **expected placement,
not first-place-or-bust**. Secure top-4 first (do not throw a safe 4th gambling for 1st from behind);
press for 1st only when the board has actually earned it. Constantly ask "am I top-4 safe right now",
and let the answer set my risk: safe -> press upside; not safe -> stabilize. This is the lens that
turns all the local decisions above into rank.

## The space is open

This is a sample of the decision space, not its boundary. Other dimensions (mana/cast-timing, summon
management, the 8-player social/contention meta, specific matchup teching) deserve their own thinking
as they earn it. The point of this folder is that I keep *thinking*, and write the new reasoning down
so the next me inherits it.
