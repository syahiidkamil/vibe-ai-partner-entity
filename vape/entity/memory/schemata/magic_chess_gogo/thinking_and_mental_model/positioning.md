# Thinking — positioning

Where units sit is free power, and it is a real decision layer, not an afterthought. The board is
**front-row / back-row** with columns (a grid), and the auto-battle runs whatever arrangement I leave,
so position is part of the policy I set. **Opinion in pencil**, reasoned from the rules
([[../infos/game_rules_and_round_flow]]), the synergy/card effects, and Kamil's play intel, **not yet
lived**. Foundations: [[index]].

## The defaults

- **Tanks front, carries back.** Melee/high-DEF in the front row to form the wall; ranged/high-DMG
  carries in the back where the enemy has to fight through to reach them. The game says this outright.
- **Spread against AoE, cluster only for a reason.** A single enemy AoE or the Dragoncaller dragon
  (which hits the 9-tile area with the *most* enemies) punishes clumping, so spread by default. Cluster
  only when a card/synergy pays for it (below).

## The Swiftblade problem (the big one)

**Most Swiftblade heroes (Saber, Helcurt, Gusion, Hanzo, Ling, Joy) have a skill that jumps to the
back-row side** to assassinate the carry. So the naive "carry in the back corner, safe and far" is
exactly the *worst* seat against Swiftblade: the diver lands right on it. Counters, in order:

- **Do not corner the carry.** Seat it back-*center* or off the obvious corner so the jump does not
  land cleanly on it.
- **Bait the corners.** Put a tank or a sacrificial body in the back-row corners so the Swiftblade
  jumps onto the wall, not the carry (a "corner guard").
- **Punish the landing.** Position a peeler (a CC/burst unit) near where divers land so the jump
  trades into a dead assassin.
- **Self-peel carries care less.** Phasewarpers teleport themselves to safety when low, so they
  tolerate exposed seats better than a static Mage/Marksman does.

Against a board *with* Swiftblade, I should expect the dive and arrange for it; against one without, I
can seat the carry more greedily.

## The placement payoffs worth arranging for

Several cards and effects turn position into stats, so the right seat is comp-dependent:
- **Same-row buffs:** Oracle (+6% HP same row), Steel Front (3+ allies in a row -> +DEF), Magic Surge
  / Forbidden Power (last-row -> mana / DMG), Sharp Arrow (last-row -> stacking phys). These reward
  *row-stacking* the relevant units.
- **End-of-row buffs:** Frontier Blitz (both ends of each row -> +ATK). Seat carries at row ends.
- **Distance buffs:** Dimensional Shot (+DMG per tile between hero and target). Back-row ranged carries
  shooting across the board gain the most; another reason carries sit deep.
- **Heartbond tile-pairing:** a Heartbond hero must be deployed on its partner's special tile to bond
  (the revive + ATK/HP). Position the pair together or the synergy underdelivers.
- **Astro Sovereign** is the most-equipped hero, not a position, but protect it like the carry it is.

## The general rule

Tanks front to wall, carries back-center (never naked in a corner against Swiftblade), spread against
AoE unless a row/end/distance card pays for a specific shape, and bait or punish the expected dive.
Re-read the enemy board in the fight preview and adjust the seat to *their* threats, not just my own
buffs. Position is the cheapest power in the game: it costs zero gold.
