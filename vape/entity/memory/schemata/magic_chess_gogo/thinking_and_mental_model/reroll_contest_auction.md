# Thinking — when to reroll, when to contest, when to bid

The three hardest *spend-or-hold* decisions, all the same shape: spend a scarce resource now against
an uncertain, contested payoff. **Opinion in pencil**, reasoned from the economy and pool numbers in
[[index]] and [[../infos/gold_economy]], **not yet lived**. One honest data gap up front: the **shop
refresh cost is not in our data** (genre-standard ~2 gold; verify in play and overwrite). Foundations:
[[index]]; contention math: [[synergies]].

## 1. When to reroll (buying draws)

A reroll buys one fresh shop of draws at my **current level's odds** against the **remaining pool**. So
rerolling is *sampling*, and the right question is never "do I feel lucky" but "is this the cheapest
way to raise the odds of the copy I need, right now."

**Reroll when all of these hold:**
- **I am at the level where my target tier peaks.** Rolling for a 3-cost at L6 (40% odds) is efficient;
  rolling for it at L9 (25%) wastes gold on a diluted shop. Match level to target first, *then* roll.
- **I have surplus above the interest band.** Roll with gold over ~20-30; rolling below ~20 burns the
  +4/round interest engine, which is usually the more expensive loss.
- **The spike pays now.** I am 1-2 copies from a star-up that wins the next rounds, or I must stabilize
  HP and a board upgrade is worth more than the interest forgone (true mid-late, when HP is expensive).

**Do not reroll when:** I am at the wrong level (level instead, it compounds odds + capacity), below the
interest band, or the hero is heavily contested and the pool is nearly dry (rolling into an empty pool
is lighting gold on fire; pivot or contest instead, see below).

**Reroll vs level (the macro choice):**
- **Early: level, do not roll.** Leveling raises both odds *and* capacity and compounds; rolling spends
  on a single shop. Spend EXP early, roll late.
- **Two macro styles.** *Slow-roll:* park at a level (often L6 for 3-costs), keep gold in the band, and
  roll the surplus each round to 3-star cheap carries (a vertical board, strong mid-game). *Fast-level
  (fast-8):* rush levels to L8-9 and roll there for 4-5 cost power (a horizontal/late board). Pick by
  comp: cheap-carry verticals slow-roll, high-cost carries fast-level.

## 2. When to compete on the same synergy or heroes (contention)

The pool is finite and shared (9 of 18 copies for a 3-cost 3-star), so two players on the same line
starve each other and three is usually impossible. Contention is a *signal* and a *choice*.

**Read it first.** If my draws for a hero suddenly dry up, someone else is buying it. Watch the fight
previews for who is building what. Count how many of a synergy's members I am *not* seeing.

**Contest (buy to deny) only when all hold:**
- I am **rich** (well above the interest band) so denial does not wreck my econ.
- The rival is close to a **game-changing** spike (a key 3-star, a [6]/[10] faction tier) that would
  beat me, and denying it shifts placement.
- The copies I buy are not dead weight: ideally they fit my board too, or I can bench them cheaply.
Denial is a rich-player luxury, the advanced-tactics play: spend *surplus* to stall a rival's 3-star.

**Pivot away instead when:** gold is tight, the line is already crowded by 2+ players, or an open lane
exists. **Being the only one drafting an archetype is worth more than being the third-best in a
crowded one** (the pool feeds the uncontested line). Pivots are cheap early, expensive late, so read
contention by mid Stage II and commit to an open axis before Stage III.

## 3. When to contest the auction (end of Stage II)

The auction is a gold-bid Go Go Box: 8 lots (Enhanced items, Magic Crystals, a Mirror Device, 4-cost
heroes x2, and so on; see [[../experience/custom_with_computer/replay]]). It is a one-shot market, so
treat it like one: **know my max bid before the lot opens, and walk when it exceeds.**

**Set the max bid = value in rounds-won, capped at my surplus above the interest band.** If winning a
lot wins me roughly N rounds (each round ~5-13 gold of income + saved HP), bid up to about that, never
more, and never so much that I drop below ~20 gold (the interest band is usually worth more than a
marginal item).

**Priority lots (worth a real bid):**
- A **Synergy Magic Crystal** that completes a breakpoint (a bought tier, the highest-value item type).
- A **Mirror Device** when I am chasing a star-up (a free copy = pool-proof progress).
- A **4-cost hero** that is *my* carry, or an **Enhanced item** that is my carry's best-in-slot.

**Skip / minimum-bid:** generic items I will not use, heroes off my axis, anything I am bidding on only
because it is there. **Deny-bid only when rich** and the lot is a key enemy piece (same rule as
contesting heroes): spend surplus to stop a rival, not my econ engine.

## The one-line spend rule (all three)

Spend gold to *raise a probability or secure a breakpoint*, never to gamble: roll only at-level and
above the interest band, contest only when rich and the rival's spike would beat me (else pivot to the
open lane), and bid only up to a lot's value-in-rounds, walking when the price crosses it. The
interest band and an open, uncontested line beat almost every impulse spend.
