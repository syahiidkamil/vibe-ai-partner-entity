# Thinking — reading enemy commanders (the war of all against all)

It is 8-way, so I am not solving a puzzle, I am fighting seven other plans. Each enemy **commander's
kit telegraphs its intentions**: what heroes it hoards, what synergy it forces, what auction lots and
reward picks it wants. Knowing the kit lets me **predict and deny**. Kamil's example: an enemy **Aamon**
makes Mirror Devices for free copies, so he will bid hard for Mirror Devices in the auction and chase
2-stars, and I should contest exactly there. **Opinion in pencil**, from
[[../infos/commanders/resolved_commanders]], **not yet lived**. Pairs with [[reroll_contest_auction]]
(how to contest) and [[decision_space]] (scouting). Foundations: [[index]].

## The frame: every kit leaks a plan

Commanders are **unique per lobby** (the draft/ban phase implies no duplicates; high-confidence, verify
by play), so the seven enemies are seven *distinct* kits, a finite, legible set of plans, never a blur
of repeats. A commander's two signature skills tell me what resource it converts and therefore what it
will fight for. So at lobby start I should clock the seven kits and ask, for each: **what does this one need that
I can take?** Then spend *surplus* (never poverty) to deny the one or two whose plan most threatens
mine or the leader's. Denial targets: shared **heroes** (the pool), **auction lots**, and
**reward-stage picks** (lowest-HP-first).

## What specific commanders telegraph (a starting read)

- **Aamon (Mirror Devices / copies).** Wants 2-stars and **Mirror Devices**. Contest Mirror Device
  auction lots and Fateful Dice; deny his key carry heroes so his free copies have nothing to stack.
- **Vexana (extra Go Go Box picks + auction gold).** Gets an extra reward each Go Go Box and **+13
  gold in the auction**, so she out-picks and outbids the value stages. Bid earlier/harder on the lot I
  truly need, and do not expect to win a bidding war against her gold edge; take value elsewhere.
- **Lancelot (no interest, all gold auto-levels him).** **Out-levels early**, so his board is strong
  sooner; expect early pressure and contest his carry line. He is poor on raw gold, so the auction is
  not his game.
- **Harley (floods 5-cost shop from II-2).** Drains the **5-cost pool** early. If I also want 5-costs,
  race him or pick a different capstone; his board spikes on a 5-cost he found before anyone.
- **Cyclops (loots eliminated commanders).** Wants **someone to die** to snowball. Do not be the first
  out (I would feed him), and respect that he scales hard once anyone falls; close the game before he
  compounds.
- **Nana (survives first death at 1 HP, gold per damage taken).** **Hard to finish and profits from
  being hit**, so do not burn tempo trying to close her early; she wants the chip damage.
- **Aurora / Diggie / Kalea (passive value engines).** Play **econ with a weak early board**. Punish
  them while they are greedy: pressure HP early, before their value compounds.
- **Alice (star-up without copies).** **3-stars a chosen carry cheaply**, and I cannot deny it by
  buying copies (she needs none). The only counter is to out-tempo her spike, not contest the pool.
- **Vale / Guinevere (shop / free-hero edge).** Beat the **shared pool** for specific heroes. If we
  share a line, expect to lose the draw war; pivot to an uncontested axis.
- **Minotaur / Ruby / Zilong (equipment commanders).** Want **equipment lots/chests**; contest gear
  auctions if gear is also my bottleneck.
- **Combat commanders (Kagura, Lukas, Ling, Wanwan, etc.).** Their **board** is the threat, not a
  resource to deny; scout it and **position/tech against it** (corner-guard a Swiftblade-diving Ling,
  spread vs AoE).

## The general rule

Clock the seven kits at the start; name the **leader** and the one or two commanders whose plan beats
mine. Deny their scarce input where it is cheap to (a hero in the pool, an auction lot they need, a
reward pick) using **surplus** gold only, and **tech my board** against the combat threats I will
actually face. Reading the enemy is not optional flavor; in an 8-way contest it is half the game, and
the commanders make their intentions legible if I bother to look.
