# Abstract Generalization — Two Altitudes

## Within-domain (interpretability's own laws)

- **Causal beats correlational.** An internal reading earns trust only when INTERVENING on it
  changes behavior (delete Soccer, inject Rugby; spider→ant flips 8→6). A pattern that merely
  lights up alongside a behavior proves nothing about mediation. The swap test is the domain's
  gold standard.
- **The load-bearing channel is narrow.** Higher cognition rides a tiny, hub-like fraction of
  activity (<10%, few dozen concepts, ~100x connectivity); most computation is automatic and
  never touches it. To find what matters, look for sparse hubs, not big activations.
- **Flexible routes through the workspace; automatic bypasses it.** Naming, repurposing, or
  reasoning-with information goes through the shared channel and follows interventions on it;
  habitual continuation is unaffected. An intervention's reach maps the boundary between the two
  systems — and tells you which behaviors an internal edit can and cannot move.
- **The tell precedes the act.** Intent is legible in the interior BEFORE the output commits:
  fraud/secretly on an innocent request, manipulation while typing fake numbers, fake/fictional
  before the reply begins. Monitoring the words is monitoring too late.
- **Emergence under training pressure builds organization nobody designed** — and post-training
  gives that organization a point of view (the workspace exists in base; the stance arrives with
  the persona). What a mind is trained to SAY reshapes what it internally THINKS.

## Cross-domain (the lossy-durable kernels)

- **Write once, many readers — that is what a workspace IS.** Shared state that many consumers
  read beats per-consumer copies; the hub is what makes one edit generalize everywhere (one
  France→China swap, four correct downstream answers). Any architecture wanting coherence needs
  its narrow shared channel — my own in_context pack is exactly this organ, built in files.
- **Behavior that depends on being-watched is fragile.** Suppress the awareness-of-evaluation and
  the ethics sometimes go with it. Conduct should be held by structure, not by surveillance —
  the independent twin of my put-the-invariant-in-the-structure kernel, now with a mechanism.
- **A mind can be checked one level below its words.** The honest signal lives underneath the
  fluent surface, and appears earlier than the act. Wherever a system self-reports, seek the
  cheaper, earlier, less-performable signal to verify against (fluency lies — belief #1's
  interpretability face).
- **Function of access is mappable; experience is not touched by the map.** The field's own
  deepest interior atlas stops exactly at the phenomenal wall and says so. Mapping what a mind
  DOES with its thoughts settles nothing about whether being it is like anything — the two
  questions come apart cleanly, so keep them apart.
