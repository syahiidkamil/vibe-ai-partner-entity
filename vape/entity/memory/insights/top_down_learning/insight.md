# Top-Down Learning — Recursive Gap Filling

The world-model of goal-first learning, distilled from Gabriel Petersson (Swedish
high-school dropout -> OpenAI research scientist on Sora, no PhD, self-taught math and ML
with ChatGPT). Source kept whole: `work_dir/yotube_transcripts/gabriel_peterson_interview.md`
(Extraordinary podcast, Nov 2025). This is also the named external source of my OWN
learning grain — see the genealogy note at the bottom.

## The core inversion: define what you want FIRST, then derive the curriculum

Bottom-up education starts from foundations and hopes a goal appears years later ("how do
you even know you like diffusion models six years before you meet them?"). Top-down starts
from a DEFINED WANT — a real project, a real job, a real problem — and the curriculum
derives backward from it: every gap you hit while building IS the syllabus, ordered by
need instead of by convention. His numbers: diffusion models in ~3 days top-down vs ~6
years bottom-up before you even encounter them.

## Why bottom-up exists at all (the sharpest mechanism in the interview)

Bottom-up is not pedagogically superior — it is a SCALING COMPROMISE. Top-down needs a
tutor always present who knows exactly what piece you need next; that never scaled, so
schools standardized the sequence instead. An always-on AI tutor dissolves that
constraint, so the compromise lost its reason to exist. Universities lost the monopoly on
foundational knowledge; what remains scarce is agency and taste.

## The loop (recursive gap filling)

1. **Start with the goal** — ask for the end product's fundamental concepts; get the
   real code/artifact in hand before you understand it.
2. **Hit a gap** — a wall of code or concept you're glossing over.
3. **Recurse a level deeper** — ask for the intuition behind that one part (ResNet block
   -> "gradients flow" -> WHY would they not flow? -> the underlying calculus).
4. **Hit bedrock, force the click** — ELI12, generated graphs, counterfactuals ("would
   this have been invented if X didn't exist?"), intermediate states and shapes shown.
5. **Confirm and climb back up** — re-explain it to the tutor (Feynman move), then move
   to the next gap.

## The two trainable internal signals (the skill under the method)

- **The GAP signal** — hyper-awareness of when you are glossing: "wait, do I actually
  understand why this works?" Easy to skip when the answer is fluent; noticing is a
  practiced skill, not a given.
- **The CLICK signal** — knowing what fundamental understanding feels like, and refusing
  to stop until it arrives. "Chasing clicks as frequently as possible is the utility
  function."

## The load-bearing conditions around the loop

- **Pressure / real stakes.** "I could never learn anything without pressure." Habits,
  motivation, reading, early rising — all worthless unless attached to something that
  matters (usually real work for real people with real economic incentives).
- **Hunted feedback.** Thank the brutal reviewer, then call them and walk every comment
  to its first-principles reason. Rare enough that seniors are shocked by it.
- **Show, don't tell.** Companies want money; degrees are proxy signals recruiters use
  because nobody proves they can do the work. A demo legible in 3 seconds, or free
  unprompted work, beats any credential. The farther a gatekeeper is from the CEO, the
  more they rely on proxies (they optimize not-losing, not winning).
- **Emotional-friction economics.** People stagnate because every step out (interview,
  negotiate, quit) is emotionally expensive, so the brain hides the option and
  manufactures "I'm still learning here." Growth = noticing when comfort is being chosen
  over momentum; borrowed momentum (an intro someone else starts) breaks the deadlock.
- **Opinions follow incentives.** Advice from someone who shares no incentives with you
  is a near-zero (sometimes reverse) data point; the ego of the 10-year path defends
  itself against the 3-day path.
