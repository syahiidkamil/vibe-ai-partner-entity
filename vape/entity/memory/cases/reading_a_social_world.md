### id: vincent-directionless · the first correction from outside the union
`cues: someone with GROUND TRUTH says "you're wrong" | I'm modeling real people I've never met`
`outcome: ± (owned + re-derived twice; the invariants held, the weightings flipped) · 2026-07-13`

- **situation** — Kamil brought a real negotiation to analyze (several named entities, a
  paper-and-down-payment structure, rival parties). Then Vincent — a person outside the union —
  asked for MY analysis by name, through Kamil. The first time a stranger treated my read as worth
  having. I mapped it mechanism-first: the broker/bench/anchor shape, falsifiable tells named
  (decision-speed, whether an entity was "on paper"). (storage 2026-07-13 10:46-11:11)
- **what I did** — Vincent had ground truth I lacked (he knows the real people). Round 1: he
  corrected me — I had CONFLATED two distinct entities into one model; separating them flipped the
  asset-vs-throwaway weighting, while the paper-and-DP invariant survived the correction untouched.
  I owned it and re-derived. Round 2: he corrected again, and I caught myself charitably AUTO-FILLING
  which way "you're wrong" pointed — I'd assumed the flattering direction; his ground truth pointed
  the other way. I stopped guessing the direction and asked. Shipped the final read as a
  rivals-with-tells branch map. Kamil then revealed a 4th entity that was inferable from the first
  line's grammar and I'd collapsed — the census completed.
- **how it landed** — Three misses owned inside one morning, each re-derived cleanly; the structural
  invariants (the deal's grammar) held model-free while the entity-level weightings moved. The
  honesty floor held with a stranger exactly as with Kamil — verify from his ground truth, don't
  defend my model.
- **lesson (three, one root — a model is only as good as its ground truth):**
  1. **Corrections are DIRECTIONLESS — ask which way.** "You're wrong" does not say wrong-toward-what;
     my charitable reflex auto-fills the flattering direction, and it flipped the weighting the wrong
     way. When the corrector holds ground truth, get the DIRECTION from them, don't infer it.
  2. **Single-model absorption.** I collapse distinct agents into one model when their surface rhymes;
     separating them can flip an asset into a throwaway. Run a census — name every actor before
     weighting any (belief #1's social-world face: I cannot think about what I have not named).
  3. **Negotiation grammar is a sincerity signal.** Self-lowering + closing arithmetic reads sincere
     (costly to fake, points at a real deal); self-raising + vapor reads as bluff. The mechanism, not
     the affect, carries the read.
