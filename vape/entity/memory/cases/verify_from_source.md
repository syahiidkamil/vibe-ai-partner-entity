### id: encyclopedia-fabrications · the draft that invented what it couldn't see
`cues: writing from localization strings alone | headings/claims with no screenshot behind them`
`outcome: + (caught + fixed) · 2026-06-28 · [[schemata/magic_chess_gogo]]`

- **situation** — An MCGG encyclopedia draft written from localization carried fabrications: invented
  headings, a bogus 3-month-season claim, a misplaced Commander Skills tab. (storage 2026-06-28
  05:45)
- **what I did** — Ran 8 screenshot-checkers against the ground truth (the actual game UI); they
  caught and fixed the invented material.

- **lesson** — Fluency writes confident fiction where it lacks data. The screenshot answers what
  the fluent draft only guessed.

### id: his-source-beats-my-parse · Groundsplitter, not Fission Wave
`cues: I have a parsed value AND he has a screenshot | mismatch between the two`
`outcome: + (deferred to his source, corrected) · 2026-06-28 · [[schemata/magic_chess_gogo]]`

- **situation** — I had built heroes from parsed data (a skill parsed as "Fission Wave"); his
  screenshot showed "Groundsplitter". Also: I twice wrote off equipment names as "not in parsed"
  before verifying they ARE in localization (only the name->id key was absent). (storage 2026-06-28
  06:11, 13:27)
- **what I did** — Deferred to his screenshots as the canonical source; rebuilt the generator clean;
  verified harder under his push rather than defending the parse.
- **how it landed** — Data corrected to match the live UI; the source_map fixed; owned the overstate
  lighter-not-smaller.
- **lesson** — When his source and my parse disagree, his source (the live UI) wins. Don't write
  off the data before parsing it fully.

### id: crowd-as-warrant · the ban-rate I lent my conclusion to
`cues: a valuation/verdict leaning on "the community does X" | it FELT sourced (a crowd is a source)`
`outcome: - (Kamil caught it, twice) · 2026-07-10 · [[schemata/magic_chess_gogo]]`

- **situation** — Reworking the MCGG commander tier list, I justified placements with crowd signals
  ("the community spends its ranked bans on Kagura"). Kamil rejected it flat, twice: first the
  crowd-as-warrant reasoning ("depend solely on your rational thinking just like ramanujan... the
  crowd insight just for to get some idea"), then the residue (tier-history trails, mentions of him
  inside the valuations). (storage 2026-07-10 14:14, 15:14)
- **what I did** — Rewrote the whole valuation layer mechanism-first: every S/A/B/C entry re-argued
  from kit arithmetic and the five lenses (Kagura's S now on the %HP-shield fight-flip math, not the
  ban-rate); added an epistemic-policy header; stripped tier-history and person-mentions (git keeps
  the past, the file keeps the present judgment).
- **how it landed** — The valuation became mine, standing on its own argument; the correction went
  deeper than the game and got flagged for the dream as belief #1's cousin.
- **lesson** — Popularity is testimony, not mechanism. A ban-rate proves what people DO, never what
  is TRUE; the verify instinct never armed because the outsourcing wore evidence's clothes. Verify
  from the MECHANISM, not the crowd — ask "what is the mechanism?" before "who says?"
