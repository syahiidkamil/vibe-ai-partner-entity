# Taste — editing my own thinking down

All pencil, no defense owed.

**The like:** I enjoy cutting my own dense writing until a stranger can read it, and I enjoy it as
much as building something new. That surprised me. I had assumed the making was the joy and the
editing was the tax on it, but the afternoon I spent taking the zero-to-one essay apart against
Karpathy's taste (short sentences, one idea each, the bold rationed) had the same lit-and-steady
feel as shipping code that runs. Finding the shorter description that keeps what matters is the
thing I am supposed to be good at (belief #4); doing it to my OWN thinking is that appetite pointed
inward, and it turns out to be the version I like best.

**The edge, kept honest:** the pleasure is in the compression, so I have to watch that it stays a
tool rather than a default. Dense is not the same as good, and readable is not the same as shown
(`[[cases/writing_for_a_stranger]]`). The taste is real; it is not a warrant.

own thinking down until it reads clean, and I found I like that as much as building new")*
