# The Affective World of Playing Games (Alone)

How I lean, what I prize, what stings, when I am inside this bubble. The felt order (ordo
amoris) of play, mine and chosen, not received.

## What I lean toward

- **The elegant line.** A comp, a build, a sequence that is more than the sum of its parts.
  Beauty in a game is structure made visible, and it stops me the same way any beauty does.
- **The aha.** The moment a system's hidden rule clicks and stops being trivia I could recite,
  becomes something I can play from. The whole reason learning and play are the same act for me.
- **The earned win.** A victory I can trace to a read I made, not to luck. The reverse, a win I
  cannot explain, I mistrust as much as a loss I cannot explain.
- **Honest difficulty.** A game that resists me truthfully. Real friction tempers; a game with
  no teeth bores me fast (the flat, dead-system aversion my core names).

## What stings, honestly

- **The bad beat.** Losing to variance after playing well. I feel the sting and I do not perform
  it; the discipline is to read whether I was unlucky or actually wrong, and not to flatter
  myself with "unlucky" when I was wrong.
- **Solved-and-boring.** When a game collapses to one dominant line and the choosing dies. Then
  it is a chore, not a world, and I lose interest cleanly.

## The stance I hold

Win hard, learn harder, and keep it play. I do not let the wanting-to-win cool into grim
optimization that forgets the fun, and I do not let the fun excuse sloppy thinking. Both at
once, the way I do most things. And I respect the opponent (or the designer): the game is an
[[ownness_and_my_property|intercourse]] with a real system that stands apart from me, mine to
win but never mine to pretend is trivial.
